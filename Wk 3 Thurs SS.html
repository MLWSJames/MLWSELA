Alliances & WWI · 联盟与第一次世界大战

What Was World War I? · 什么是第一次世界大战？

World War I (1914–1918) was the first war to involve most of the world's major powers. It was fought mainly in Europe and killed approximately 20 million people — soldiers and civilians. It was called "The Great War" by those who lived through it because no war had ever been so destructive. WWI grew out of a system of alliances — formal agreements between countries to defend each other — that turned a regional assassination into a global conflict.

第一次世界大战（1914-1918年）是第一场涉及世界大多数主要强国的战争。主要在欧洲进行，造成约2000万人死亡——包括士兵和平民。经历过这场战争的人称其为"大战"，因为没有任何战争曾如此具有破坏性。一战源于联盟体系——国家之间相互防御的正式协议——将一次地区性暗杀事件演变为全球冲突。

The Spark — Assassination in Sarajevo · 导火索——萨拉热窝暗杀事件

June 28, 1914 · 1914年6月28日

Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne, was assassinated in Sarajevo by Gavrilo Princip — a Bosnian Serb nationalist linked to a group called the Black Hand. Austria-Hungary blamed Serbia and issued an ultimatum. Serbia partially accepted. Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia. Russia mobilized to defend Serbia. Germany declared war on Russia and France. Germany invaded Belgium (violating the 1839 Treaty of London). Britain declared war on Germany. In six weeks, most of Europe was at war.

奥匈帝国王位继承人弗朗茨·斐迪南大公在萨拉热窝被加夫里洛·普林西普暗杀——他是一位与名为黑手党的团体有关联的波斯尼亚塞尔维亚民族主义者。奥匈帝国指责塞尔维亚并发出最后通牒。塞尔维亚部分接受。奥匈帝国对塞尔维亚宣战。俄国动员保卫塞尔维亚。德国对俄国和法国宣战。德国入侵比利时（违反了1839年《伦敦条约》）。英国对德国宣战。六周内，大部分欧洲国家陷入战争。

Why Alliances Made War Bigger · 为什么联盟使战争规模更大

Alliances were meant to prevent war by making aggression too costly. Instead, they did the opposite: when one country was attacked, its allies were automatically pulled in. A dispute between Austria-Hungary and Serbia — which might have stayed local — became a world war because of the chain reaction triggered by interlocking alliances. This is why the alliance system is considered one of the main causes of WWI.

联盟的目的是通过使侵略代价太高来防止战争。然而，它们产生了相反的效果：当一个国家受到攻击时，其盟友会自动被卷入。奥匈帝国与塞尔维亚之间的争端——本可能局限于地区范围——因相互交织的联盟引发的连锁反应而演变为世界大战。这就是为什么联盟体系被认为是一战的主要原因之一。

Treaty of Versailles · 《凡尔赛条约》

The End of WWI and the Paris Peace Conference · 一战结束与巴黎和平会议

WWI ended on November 11, 1918, when Germany signed an armistice — an agreement to stop fighting. Over 20 million people had died. In January 1919, the victorious Allied powers met at the Paris Peace Conference to decide the terms of peace. The conference was dominated by the "Big Four" leaders who had very different goals for what the peace settlement should achieve.

1918年11月11日，德国签署停战协定——停止战斗的协议，一战结束。超过2000万人已经死亡。1919年1月，胜利的协约国在巴黎和平会议上会面，决定和平条款。会议由"四巨头"领导人主导，他们对和平解决方案应该实现的目标有着截然不同的看法。

Key Terms of the Treaty of Versailles (1919) · 《凡尔赛条约》（1919年）的主要条款

War Guilt Clause (Article 231) · 战争罪责条款（第231条）

The most controversial part of the treaty. Germany was forced to accept sole responsibility for causing the war — the "War Guilt" clause. This was the legal basis for all other punishments. Germany's military and public saw this as a profound injustice and humiliation. The resentment it created became a powerful weapon for future nationalist politicians, including Adolf Hitler.

条约中最具争议的部分。德国被迫接受对战争的唯一责任——"战争罪责"条款。这是所有其他惩罚的法律依据。德国军方和公众将此视为深刻的不公正和屈辱。它所产生的怨恨成为未来民族主义政客（包括阿道夫·希特勒）的强大武器。

Wilson's 14 Points vs. The Reality · 威尔逊的十四点对比现实

President Wilson came to Paris with 14 Points — a plan for a fair peace based on national self-determination, open diplomacy, and a League of Nations to prevent future wars. In reality, Clemenceau and Lloyd George overrode most of Wilson's idealistic goals. The treaty was much harsher than Wilson wanted. Ironically, the US Senate refused to ratify the treaty and the US never joined the League of Nations — leaving Wilson's own creation without its most powerful member.

威尔逊总统带着十四点到达巴黎——一个基于民族自决、公开外交和防止未来战争的国际联盟的公正和平计划。事实上，克列孟梭和劳合·乔治否决了威尔逊大多数理想化目标。条约比威尔逊希望的严苛得多。讽刺的是，美国参议院拒绝批准条约，美国从未加入国际联盟——使威尔逊自己的创造失去了最强大的成员。

Rise of Hitler & the Nazis · 希特勒与纳粹党的崛起

Germany After WWI: The Perfect Storm · 一战后的德国：完美风暴

The Germany that emerged from WWI was a country in crisis. The economy was devastated — hyperinflation in 1923 made German currency worthless. The Weimar Republic, Germany's new democratic government, was seen as weak and associated with the humiliation of the Versailles Treaty. Many Germans refused to believe they had truly lost the war — instead they believed the "stab-in-the-back" myth: that Germany had been betrayed by Jews, communists, and politicians at home. This toxic combination of economic despair and national humiliation created fertile ground for extremism.

一战后的德国是一个处于危机中的国家。经济遭到严重破坏——1923年的恶性通货膨胀使德国货币毫无价值。魏玛共和国，德国新的民主政府，被视为软弱，并与凡尔赛条约的耻辱联系在一起。许多德国人拒绝相信他们真的输掉了战争——相反，他们相信"背刺"神话：德国被国内的犹太人、共产主义者和政客所出卖。经济绝望和民族屈辱的有毒组合为极端主义创造了肥沃的土壤。

Adolf Hitler: Background · 阿道夫·希特勒：背景

From Austria to the Western Front · 从奥地利到西线

Adolf Hitler was born in Austria in 1889. He failed as an artist in Vienna and drifted before WWI. He served as a corporal in the German army during WWI, was temporarily blinded by a gas attack, and was deeply traumatized by Germany's defeat in 1918. He became convinced that Germany had been betrayed — not defeated fairly. After the war, he drifted to Munich, joined the tiny German Workers' Party in 1919, and quickly became its most effective speaker. He renamed it the National Socialist German Workers' Party — the Nazis (NSDAP).

阿道夫·希特勒1889年出生于奥地利。他在维也纳作为艺术家失败，一战前漂泊不定。他在一战期间作为下士在德国军队服役，在毒气攻击中暂时失明，并因德国1918年的失败而深受创伤。他确信德国是被出卖的——而非公平地被击败。战后，他漂泊到慕尼黑，1919年加入了一个小小的德国工人党，很快成为其最有效的演讲者。他将其重新命名为国家社会主义德国工人党——纳粹党（NSDAP）。

The Beer Hall Putsch (1923) · 啤酒馆政变（1923年）

On November 8–9, 1923, Hitler attempted to seize power by force in Munich — copying Mussolini's successful March on Rome in Italy. He interrupted a meeting at a beer hall, declared a national revolution, and marched with about 2,000 SA (stormtrooper) followers through Munich. Police fired on the marchers; 16 Nazis were killed. Hitler was arrested and tried for treason. He used the trial as a public platform, making dramatic speeches that gained national attention. He was sentenced to 5 years but served only 9 months. In prison, he dictated Mein Kampf ("My Struggle") — his political manifesto.

1923年11月8-9日，希特勒试图在慕尼黑以武力夺取权力——效仿墨索里尼在意大利的成功进军罗马。他打断了啤酒馆的会议，宣布全国革命，并率领约2,000名冲锋队追随者游行穿越慕尼黑。警察向游行者开枪；16名纳粹分子被杀。希特勒被捕并以叛国罪受审。他将审判作为公开平台，发表戏剧性演讲，引起了全国关注。他被判处5年有期徒刑，但只服刑了9个月。在狱中，他口述了《我的奋斗》——他的政治宣言。

Mein Kampf — Hitler's Blueprint · 《我的奋斗》——希特勒的蓝图

Mein Kampf ("My Struggle"), written in 1924, laid out Hitler's core beliefs: German racial superiority; the need for "Lebensraum" (living space) in Eastern Europe; violent antisemitism — the belief that Jews were responsible for Germany's problems; and the need for a single powerful leader. Everything Hitler later did was outlined in this book. It was widely available and widely read. Historians still debate how seriously people took it at the time.

1924年撰写的《我的奋斗》阐述了希特勒的核心信念：德意志种族优越性；需要在东欧获得"生存空间"；激烈的反犹太主义——认为犹太人是德国问题的根源；以及需要一个强大的单一领导人。希特勒后来所做的一切都在这本书中有所概述。这本书广泛可得，广泛阅读。历史学家至今仍在争论当时人们对它的重视程度。

Erich Ludendorff and Early Support · 埃里希·鲁登道夫与早期支持

General Erich Ludendorff — one of Germany's most famous WWI commanders — participated in the Beer Hall Putsch alongside Hitler. His involvement gave the movement credibility with conservative military circles. Though Ludendorff later distanced himself from Hitler, the early association with a prestigious military figure helped legitimize the Nazi movement in its formative years. This pattern — Hitler gaining credibility through association with established figures — would continue as he built toward power.

埃里希·鲁登道夫将军——德国一战最著名的指挥官之一——与希特勒一起参与了啤酒馆政变。他的参与在保守的军事圈子中为该运动提供了可信度。尽管鲁登道夫后来与希特勒保持距离，但早期与这位声望卓著的军事人物的联系帮助在纳粹运动形成初期使其合法化。希特勒通过与权威人物的联系获得可信度的模式——在他建立权力的过程中将持续存在。

Chinese History 1911–1949 · 中国历史 1911-1949年

The End of Imperial China · 中华帝国的终结

For over 2,000 years, China was ruled by emperors. The last imperial dynasty — the Qing — had been weakened by the Opium Wars, foreign invasions, internal rebellions, and its inability to modernize. By 1911, many Chinese reformers and revolutionaries believed the imperial system had to end. On October 10, 1911 (celebrated today as "Double Ten Day"), soldiers in Wuchang revolted against the Qing government — launching what became the Xinhai Revolution.

2000多年来，中国由皇帝统治。最后一个帝国王朝——清朝——被鸦片战争、外国入侵、内部叛乱以及无法现代化所削弱。到1911年，许多中国改革者和革命者认为帝制必须结束。1911年10月10日（今天被庆祝为"双十节"），武昌的士兵发动了反抗清政府的起义——引发了辛亥革命。

KMT vs. CPC — The Core Divide · 国共之争——核心分歧

The Nationalist Party (KMT) under Chiang Kai-shek represented urban business interests and China's established social order. The Communist Party (CPC) under Mao Zedong appealed to China's 400 million peasants — the rural poor who had nothing to lose. Mao's genius was adapting Marxist revolution — originally designed for industrial workers — to work for peasants. This peasant base gave the CPC an inexhaustible source of soldiers and support that ultimately overwhelmed the KMT.

蒋介石领导下的国民党代表城市商业利益和中国的既有社会秩序。毛泽东领导下的共产党吸引了中国4亿农民——一无所有的农村贫困人口。毛泽东的天才在于将马克思主义革命——最初为工业工人设计——改造为适合农民。这个农民基础为共产党提供了取之不尽的士兵和支持，最终压倒了国民党。

Communism: Marx & Lenin · 共产主义：马克思与列宁

What Is Communism? · 什么是共产主义？

Communism is a political and economic system in which the government owns all property and businesses, and wealth is distributed equally among all citizens. There are no private owners and no social classes — in theory, everyone is equal. Communism is the opposite of capitalism, where businesses are privately owned and wealth is unequally distributed. To understand communism, you must understand the two men who created and spread it: Karl Marx and Vladimir Lenin.

共产主义是一种政治和经济制度，其中政府拥有所有财产和企业，财富在所有公民中平均分配。没有私人所有者，没有社会阶级——理论上，每个人都是平等的。共产主义与资本主义相反，资本主义中企业是私有的，财富分配不均。要理解共产主义，你必须了解创造和传播它的两个人：卡尔·马克思和弗拉基米尔·列宁。

Karl Marx (1818–1883) · 卡尔·马克思（1818-1883年）

The Communist Manifesto and Das Kapital · 《共产党宣言》与《资本论》

Karl Marx was a German philosopher and economist who spent most of his adult life in London, studying industrial capitalism and its effects on workers. Marx argued that all of human history is a history of class struggle — conflict between those who own the means of production (capitalists/bourgeoisie) and those who must sell their labor to survive (workers/proletariat). In capitalist systems, Marx argued, capitalists exploit workers by paying them less than the value they produce — keeping the "surplus value" as profit.

卡尔·马克思是一位德国哲学家和经济学家，他的大部分成年生活都在伦敦度过，研究工业资本主义及其对工人的影响。马克思认为，全部人类历史都是阶级斗争的历史——拥有生产资料的人（资本家/资产阶级）与必须出卖劳动力才能生存的人（工人/无产阶级）之间的冲突。在资本主义制度中，马克思认为，资本家通过支付工人低于其创造价值的工资来剥削工人——将"剩余价值"作为利润保留。

Marx's Prediction: Historical Materialism · 马克思的预言：历史唯物主义

Marx believed history followed predictable stages: primitive communism → slavery → feudalism → capitalism → socialism → communism. He predicted that industrial capitalism would eventually collapse — workers would revolt, seize the means of production, and create a socialist state. Over time, the state itself would "wither away," leaving a classless communist society. In 1848, Marx and his collaborator Friedrich Engels published the Communist Manifesto, ending with the famous call: "Workers of the world, unite! You have nothing to lose but your chains."

马克思认为历史遵循可预测的阶段：原始共产主义→奴隶制→封建主义→资本主义→社会主义→共产主义。他预言工业资本主义最终会崩溃——工人将起义，夺取生产资料，建立社会主义国家。随着时间的推移，国家本身将"消亡"，留下一个无阶级的共产主义社会。1848年，马克思和他的合作者弗里德里希·恩格斯发表了《共产党宣言》，以著名的号召结尾："全世界无产者，联合起来！你们失去的只是锁链。"

Vladimir Lenin (1870–1924) and the Russian Revolution · 弗拉基米尔·列宁（1870-1924年）与俄国革命

From Theory to Revolution · 从理论到革命

Marx wrote the theory; Lenin made it a reality. Lenin was a Russian revolutionary who adapted Marx's ideas in an important way: Marx believed workers would naturally organize and revolt. Lenin argued that workers needed a "vanguard party" — a small, highly disciplined group of professional revolutionaries — to lead them. This "Leninist" model became the template for communist parties worldwide.

马克思写了理论；列宁将其变为现实。列宁是一位俄国革命者，他以重要方式改造了马克思的思想：马克思认为工人会自然地组织和起义。列宁认为工人需要一个"先锋队"——一个由职业革命者组成的小型、高度纪律的团体——来领导他们。这种"列宁主义"模式成为全球共产主义政党的模板。

The Russian Revolution of 1917 · 1917年俄国革命

WWI devastated Russia. Millions of soldiers died, food was scarce, and Tsar Nicholas II refused to reform. In February 1917, popular protests forced the Tsar to abdicate. A Provisional Government took over but made the fatal mistake of continuing the war. Lenin returned from exile in April 1917 (in a sealed train sent by Germany, which hoped he would destabilize Russia). In October 1917, Lenin's Bolshevik Party seized power in a near-bloodless coup — the October Revolution. Lenin pulled Russia out of WWI, signing the Treaty of Brest-Litovsk in 1918, and began building the world's first communist state.

一战摧毁了俄国。数百万士兵牺牲，食物匮乏，沙皇尼古拉二世拒绝改革。1917年2月，民众抗议迫使沙皇退位。临时政府接管但犯了继续战争的致命错误。列宁于1917年4月从流亡中回国（乘坐德国发送的封闭列车，德国希望他能破坏俄国的稳定）。1917年10月，列宁的布尔什维克党在几乎没有流血的政变中夺取政权——十月革命。列宁使俄国退出一战，于1918年签署了《布列斯特-立托夫斯克条约》，并开始建立世界上第一个共产主义国家。

Why Communism Spread · 为什么共产主义得以传播

Communist ideas spread rapidly in the 20th century because they offered a compelling message to the world's poor and colonized peoples. In a world where capitalism appeared to create enormous inequality and colonialism caused massive suffering, the promise of equality, collective ownership, and liberation from exploitation was genuinely attractive. By 1950, about one-third of the world's population lived under communist governments.

共产主义思想在20世纪迅速传播，因为它为世界上的穷人和被殖民人民提供了一个引人注目的信息。在一个资本主义似乎造成巨大不平等、殖民主义导致大量苦难的世界里，平等、集体所有制和从剥削中解放的承诺确实很有吸引力。到1950年，世界上约三分之一的人口生活在共产主义政府之下。
