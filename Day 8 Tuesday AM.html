How Islam Spread / Rashidun Caliphate · 伊斯兰教的传播 / 拉希顿哈里发国

Four Ways Islam Spread · 伊斯兰教传播的四种方式

After Muhammad's death in 632 CE, Islam expanded rapidly across the Middle East, North Africa, and beyond. This expansion happened through several different methods working together: military conquest brought new territories under Muslim political control, trade carried Islamic ideas along merchant routes, pilgrimage to Mecca connected distant communities to the faith, and missionaries actively taught Islamic beliefs to new populations.

穆罕默德于公元632年去世后，伊斯兰教迅速扩展到中东、北非及其他地区。这种扩展是通过几种不同的方式共同实现的：军事征服使新的领土纳入穆斯林的政治控制之下，贸易将伊斯兰教的理念沿着商路传播开来，前往麦加的朝圣将遥远的社群与信仰联系在一起，传教士则主动向新的人群传授伊斯兰教的信仰。

The Rashidun Caliphate · 拉希顿哈里发国

After Muhammad died, the Muslim community needed a new form of leadership since he had been both political and religious leader. The Rashidun Caliphate (632–661 CE) — meaning "rightly guided" — was the first Arab Muslim empire, led by a series of four caliphs chosen to succeed Muhammad. This period established the basic structure for governing the rapidly growing Muslim world.

Caliphs and Caliphate Structure · 哈里发与哈里发国的结构

Unlike a king who inherits power through family bloodline, the early caliphs were selected by leading members of the Muslim community based on their close relationship to Muhammad and their reputation for piety and leadership. This structure combined religious authority with political and military command, giving the caliph responsibility over both the spiritual and worldly affairs of the growing empire.

A New Kind of Leadership: The caliphate combined religious and political authority in a single office, a structure that differed sharply from both the Byzantine emperor's role and the feudal kings of medieval Europe.
一种新型的领导方式：哈里发国将宗教与政治权威合并于同一个职位，这种结构与拜占庭皇帝的角色及中世纪欧洲的封建国王都截然不同。

Rapid Expansion Under the Rashidun · 拉希顿时期的迅速扩张

During the Rashidun period, Muslim armies achieved remarkably swift military success, conquering vast territories including Persia, Egypt, and parts of the Byzantine Empire within just a few decades. This combination of strong leadership, motivated armies, and the weakened state of neighboring empires (including Byzantium, exhausted from earlier wars) allowed Islam to spread at a historically rapid pace.

在拉希顿时期，穆斯林军队取得了异常迅速的军事胜利，在短短几十年内征服了包括波斯、埃及以及拜占庭帝国部分地区的广阔领土。强有力的领导、士气高昂的军队，以及邻近帝国（包括因先前战争而疲惫不堪的拜占庭）实力的衰弱，这些因素共同作用，使伊斯兰教以历史上罕见的速度迅速传播。

Umayyad & Abbasid Dynasties · 伍麦叶王朝与阿拔斯王朝

The Umayyad Dynasty (661–750) · 伍麦叶王朝（661–750年）

The Umayyad Dynasty replaced the Rashidun Caliphate and shifted leadership from a chosen position to hereditary rule within one family. The Umayyads made Arabic the official state language across their vast empire, employed viziers (chief ministers) to help administer government affairs, and expanded the empire's borders from Spain to Central Asia. Non-Muslims living under Umayyad rule were generally allowed to practice their faith but paid a special tax called the jizya.

The Abbasid Dynasty (750–1258) · 阿拔斯王朝（750–1258年）

The Abbasid Dynasty overthrew the Umayyads and moved the capital to Baghdad, which became one of the largest and most culturally vibrant cities in the world. The Abbasids practiced greater cultural inclusion than the Umayyads, welcoming non-Arab Muslims into positions of power and scholarship. Religious scholars known as the ulema gained significant influence in interpreting Islamic law, and this period saw mass conversion to Islam across the empire's diverse population.

A Shift in Approach: While the Umayyads centered power around Arab identity, the Abbasids built a more multicultural empire that drew on Persian, Greek, and other traditions — helping fuel the intellectual flowering of the Golden Age.
方式的转变：伍麦叶王朝以阿拉伯身份为权力中心，而阿拔斯王朝则建立了一个更具多元文化色彩的帝国，吸收了波斯、希腊及其他传统——这助力了黄金时代知识繁荣的兴起。

Why These Shifts Mattered · 这些转变为何重要

The transition from Umayyad to Abbasid rule reflects a broader pattern in the early Islamic world: a movement away from a narrowly Arab-centered empire toward one that incorporated diverse peoples, languages, and traditions. This more inclusive approach under the Abbasids helped create the conditions for the explosion of learning and culture that would define the Golden Age of Islam.

从伍麦叶王朝到阿拔斯王朝的转变，反映了早期伊斯兰世界一种更广泛的模式：从一个狭隘的以阿拉伯为中心的帝国，转向一个融合了多元民族、语言与传统的帝国。阿拔斯王朝这种更具包容性的方式，为日后定义伊斯兰黄金时代的知识与文化大爆发创造了条件。

Golden Age of Islam · 伊斯兰黄金时代

What Was the Golden Age of Islam? · 伊斯兰黄金时代是什么？

The Golden Age of Islam refers to a centuries-long period of remarkable achievement in science, medicine, mathematics, philosophy, and the arts across the Islamic world. Scholars in cities like Baghdad translated and preserved Greek, Persian, and Indian texts while making major original contributions of their own, helping to preserve knowledge that might otherwise have been lost to Europe during this period.

伊斯兰黄金时代指的是伊斯兰世界在科学、医学、数学、哲学与艺术领域取得卓越成就的一个长达数百年的时期。巴格达等城市的学者们翻译并保存了希腊、波斯与印度的文献，同时也作出了自己重要的原创贡献，帮助保存了这一时期可能在欧洲已经失传的知识。

Geographic Reach · 地理范围

At its height, the Islamic world stretched from Spain in the west to Central Asia in the east, encompassing a vast network of cities connected by trade and scholarship. This map of changing dynasties shows how political control shifted over centuries, even as the broader Islamic civilization and its cultural achievements continued to grow across this enormous territory.

Connected Centers of Learning: Cities such as Baghdad, Cordoba, and Cairo became hubs where scholars exchanged ideas across vast distances — demonstrating how trade networks could also become networks for the exchange of knowledge.
相互联系的学术中心：巴格达、科尔多瓦和开罗等城市成为学者们跨越遥远距离交流思想的中心——这表明贸易网络同样可以成为知识交流的网络。

Lasting Contributions · 持久的贡献

Scholars during the Golden Age made foundational contributions across many fields: advances in algebra and the numeral system later adopted by Europe, major medical texts that remained standard references for centuries, innovations in astronomy and optics, and the preservation of classical philosophy through careful translation and commentary. Much of this knowledge eventually reached medieval Europe, helping lay groundwork for the later Renaissance.

黄金时代的学者们在许多领域作出了基础性的贡献：代数学的进步与后来被欧洲采用的数字系统、在数百年间仍作为标准参考的重要医学文献、天文学与光学方面的创新，以及通过细致的翻译与注释保存下来的古典哲学。这些知识中的大部分最终传到了中世纪欧洲，为后来的文艺复兴奠定了基础。

Sunni and Shia Islam · 逊尼派与什叶派

Origins of the Schism · 分裂的起源

The split between Sunni and Shia Islam originated in a disagreement over who should lead the Muslim community after Muhammad's death — a disagreement that deepened at the transition from the Rashidun Caliphate to the Umayyad Dynasty. Those who later became known as Shia ("partisans") believed leadership should remain within Muhammad's own family, specifically through his cousin and son-in-law, Ali. Those who became Sunni believed leadership should go to whoever the community judged most capable, regardless of bloodline.

逊尼派与什叶派之间的分裂，起源于在穆罕默德去世后由谁来领导穆斯林社群这一争议——这一争议在从拉希顿哈里发国向伍麦叶王朝过渡时进一步加深。后来被称为什叶派（"追随者"）的人认为，领导权应留在穆罕默德自己的家族内，具体来说是通过他的堂弟兼女婿阿里。而后来被称为逊尼派的人则认为，领导权应交给社群认为最有能力的人，无论其血统如何。

Sunni View 逊尼派观点Shia View 什叶派观点
Leadership should go to the most capable, chosen by the community 领导权应交给最有能力的人，由社群推选Leadership should remain within Muhammad's family, through Ali's lineage 领导权应留在穆罕默德的家族内，通过阿里的血脉传承

Ibadi Islam: A Third Path · 伊巴德派：第三条道路

Alongside the Sunni-Shia divide, a smaller branch called Ibadi Islam also emerged from this early period of disagreement. Ibadis held that leadership should be based purely on piety and ability, rejecting both hereditary claims and many of the political compromises made by early Sunni leadership. Ibadi communities survive today primarily in Oman and parts of East Africa.

More Than a Simple Split: The Sunni-Shia division is often described as a single schism, but the existence of Ibadi Islam shows that the early disagreement over leadership actually produced multiple distinct responses, not just two.
不仅仅是简单的分裂：逊尼派与什叶派的分裂常被描述为单一的分裂事件，但伊巴德派的存在表明，早期关于领导权的争议实际上产生了多种不同的回应，而不仅仅是两种。

Why This Division Still Matters · 为什么这一分裂仍然重要

The Sunni-Shia schism, which began as a political dispute over leadership, gradually developed into distinct religious, legal, and cultural traditions within Islam. Today, Sunni Muslims make up the majority of the global Muslim population, while Shia Muslims form significant communities especially in Iran, Iraq, and parts of the Gulf region. Understanding this early schism helps explain religious and political dynamics that continue to shape the Middle East today.

逊尼派与什叶派的分裂最初是一场关于领导权的政治争议，逐渐发展成为伊斯兰教内部独特的宗教、法律与文化传统。如今，逊尼派穆斯林占全球穆斯林人口的多数，而什叶派穆斯林则在伊朗、伊拉克及海湾地区部分地方形成了重要的社群。理解这一早期的分裂，有助于解释至今仍在塑造中东局势的宗教与政治动态。

Great Schism & Introduction to the Crusades · 大分裂与十字军东征导论

The Great Schism (East-West Schism) · 大分裂（东西教会分裂）

In 1054 CE, Christianity split into two main branches in an event known as the Great Schism, or East-West Schism. The Christian Church based in Constantinople (which became Eastern Orthodox Christianity) and the Church based in Rome (which became the Roman Catholic Church) divided over disagreements about Church leadership, theology, and practice that had been building for centuries.

公元1054年，基督教分裂为两大主要分支，这一事件被称为大分裂，或称东西教会分裂。以君士坦丁堡为基地的基督教教会（后来发展为东正教）与以罗马为基地的教会（后来发展为罗马天主教会），因数百年来不断积累的关于教会领导权、神学与实践的分歧而分裂。

A Pattern of Religious Division · 宗教分裂的一种模式

The Great Schism within Christianity happened only a few centuries after the Sunni-Shia split within Islam — both showing how large religious traditions, once they grow across vast distances and diverse cultures, can develop separate centers of authority that eventually break apart over questions of leadership and practice.

Distance and Division: Just as Sunni and Shia Islam developed differently across different regions, the physical and cultural distance between Rome and Constantinople contributed to the Eastern and Western Churches drifting apart over time.
距离与分裂：正如逊尼派与什叶派在不同地区有着不同的发展，罗马与君士坦丁堡之间的地理与文化距离，也促使东西方教会随着时间的推移逐渐疏远。

Introduction to the Crusades · 十字军东征导论

By the late 11th century, the Byzantine Empire faced growing pressure from Muslim Turkish forces, prompting the Byzantine emperor to request military help from Western Europe. Pope Urban II responded in 1095 CE by calling for a series of religious wars — the Crusades — aimed at reclaiming Jerusalem and the Holy Land from Muslim control. This call combined religious devotion, political ambition, and the recently divided Christian world into a centuries-long series of conflicts between Christian Europe and the Islamic world.

到11世纪末，拜占庭帝国面临着来自穆斯林突厥势力日益增长的压力，促使拜占庭皇帝向西欧请求军事援助。教皇乌尔班二世于公元1095年作出回应，号召发起一系列宗教战争——十字军东征——旨在从穆斯林手中夺回耶路撒冷与圣地。这一号召将宗教信仰、政治野心以及刚刚分裂的基督教世界结合在一起，引发了基督教欧洲与伊斯兰世界之间长达数百年的一系列冲突。
