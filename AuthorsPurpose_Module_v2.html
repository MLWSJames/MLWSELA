📘 Part 1 — Why Do Authors Write? 作者为什么写作？

The Big Idea / 核心概念

Every author writes for a reason. This reason is called the author's purpose. Before you can analyze a text deeply, you must ask yourself: Why did the author write this? What do they want from me as a reader?

每位作者写作都有原因，这个原因叫做作者目的。在深入分析一篇文章之前，你必须问自己：作者为什么要写这个？他们希望读者得到什么？

There are three main purposes. Think of them as three doors — every text opens one of these doors (and sometimes more than one).

有三个主要目的。把它们想象成三扇门——每篇文章都会打开其中一扇（有时甚至不止一扇）。
📰 Inform / 告知 给读者新知识

The author gives you facts, data, or explanations. The goal is to teach you something true about the world — not to convince you of an opinion.

📢 Persuade / 说服 改变读者的想法或行动

The author wants you to believe something or do something. They use evidence, emotional language, and strong opinions to push you toward their view.

🎭 Entertain / 娱乐 让读者享受故事

The author creates a world, characters, and events that engage your imagination and emotions. Fiction, poetry, and personal essays often entertain.

💡 Remember / 记住 A text can have more than one purpose. A novel might entertain AND persuade. A science article might inform AND explain. But there is usually one primary purpose that drives the whole text.
一篇文章可以有不止一个目的。小说可能既娱乐又说服。科学文章可能既告知又解释。但通常有一个主要目的驱动整篇文章。
📘 Part 2 — How Techniques Reveal Purpose 手法如何揭示目的

Techniques are clues / 手法就是线索

Authors choose their techniques deliberately. The techniques they use are clues that tell you their purpose. When you identify a technique, you can ask: Why did the author use this here? What effect does it create?

作者刻意选择他们的手法。他们使用的手法是告诉你目的的线索。当你识别出一种手法时，你可以问：作者为什么在这里使用这个？它创造了什么效果？
Technique / 手法What It Does / 作用Likely Purpose / 可能目的
Imagery 意象Creates vivid mental pictures using sensory detail 用感官细节创造生动的心理画面Entertain / Persuade
Metaphor 暗喻Calls one thing another thing to deepen meaning 将一事物说成另一事物以加深含义Entertain / Persuade
Simile 明喻Compares two things using like or as 用"like"或"as"比较两件事物Entertain / Inform
Personification 拟人Gives human qualities to non-human things 赋予非人类事物人类特质Entertain / Persuade
Dialogue 对话Shows characters speaking; reveals personality and conflict 展示角色说话；揭示个性和冲突Entertain
Monologue 独白One character speaks at length; reveals inner thoughts 一个角色长篇独白；揭示内心想法Entertain / Persuade
Stage directions 舞台指示Instructions in a play [in brackets] 戏剧中用括号标注的指示Entertain (drama)
Point of view (1st) 第一人称视角Uses "I" — reader is inside the narrator's mind 使用"我"——读者进入叙述者的内心Entertain / Persuade
Point of view (3rd) 第三人称视角Uses "he/she/they" — narrator is outside the story 使用"他/她/他们"——叙述者在故事之外Entertain / Inform
📝 Example — Persuasion through Imagery / 例子：通过意象说服
"Every minute, a rainforest the size of a football field disappears — swallowed by machines that leave only silence and ash."
每分钟，一片足球场大小的雨林消失了——被机器吞噬，只留下寂静和灰烬。
The imagery of "silence and ash" makes you feel the loss — this emotional effect is in service of persuasion. The author is not just informing; they want you to act. / "寂静和灰烬"的意象让你感受到失去——这种情感效果服务于说服。作者不只是告知，他们希望你采取行动。
📝 Example — Personification for Entertainment / 例子：用拟人娱乐
"The old clock on the mantle coughed and wheezed, loyal to its slow, grumbling tick."
壁炉台上的旧时钟咳嗽着、喘息着，忠实地保持着它缓慢的、抱怨式的滴答声。
The clock has human qualities — coughing, grumbling. This is personification used for entertainment: it makes the scene come alive and creates character. / 时钟具有人类特质——咳嗽、抱怨。这是用于娱乐的拟人：它让场景充满活力并创造个性。
📘 Part 3 — A Strategy for Identifying Author's Purpose 识别作者目的的策略

Use the STOP strategy / 使用STOP策略

When you read a passage, use these four steps:

S — Subject: What is the text about? 主题：文章关于什么？

T — Tone: Does it feel emotional, neutral, or playful? 语气：感觉是情感化的、中立的还是轻松的？

O — Opinion: Does the author express a personal opinion? 观点：作者是否表达个人意见？

P — Purpose: Based on S, T, O — what is the author's purpose? 目的：基于S、T、O——作者的目的是什么？

当你阅读一段文章时，使用以下四个步骤识别作者目的。
⚠️ Common Mistake / 常见错误 Many students choose "inform" for every passage because it contains facts. But facts can be used to persuade. If the author uses facts to build an argument or tell you what to do, the purpose is persuade, not inform.
许多学生对每段文章都选择"告知"，因为其中包含事实。但事实可以用来说服。如果作者使用事实来建立论点或告诉你该做什么，目的是说服，而不是告知。

📘 Part 1 — Beyond Inform, Persuade, Entertain: Specific Purpose Verbs 更精准的目的动词

Why more specific? / 为什么要更精准？

On Tuesday, you learned the three big categories. Today you will go deeper. Strong readers and writers do not just say "the author persuades." They say "the author convinces," "the author criticizes," or "the author reveals." These words are more accurate — and they make your analysis much more powerful.

周二你学习了三个大类别。今天你将更深入。优秀的读者和作者不只说"作者说服"，他们说"作者使人信服"、"作者批评"或"作者揭示"。这些词更准确——让你的分析更有力量。
🔍 Explain / 解释 使复杂变简单

The author makes something complex easy to understand. They break it into steps, use examples, and define terms. Textbooks often explain.

💡 Reveal / 揭示 揭露隐藏的真相

The author brings hidden, surprising, or unknown information to light. The reader is supposed to feel shocked or newly aware.

🎯 Convince / 使信服 引导你相信某事

The author moves you toward a specific belief or decision using logic, evidence, or reasoning — like a lawyer making a case.

🔴 Criticize / 批评 指出缺陷和问题

The author identifies flaws, failures, or problems in something — a policy, a person, a product — and judges them negatively.

✏️ Teach / 教导 建立技能或能力

The author helps you build a skill or ability. How-to guides, writing manuals, and instructional passages teach.

🎭 Entertain / 娱乐 通过故事带来享受

The author uses narrative, emotion, and imagination to give the reader pleasure. Fiction and personal essays entertain.

📘 Part 2 — How Purpose Verbs Connect to the Big Three 目的动词与三大类别的关系

A map of purpose / 目的地图

Think of the big three as families. The specific purpose verbs belong to those families — but they tell you exactly what the author is doing inside that family.

把三大类别想象成家族。具体的目的动词属于这些家族——但它们告诉你作者在该家族内具体在做什么。
Big Category / 大类别Specific Verbs / 具体动词
Inform / 告知Explain, Reveal, Describe, Summarize 解释、揭示、描述、总结
Persuade / 说服Convince, Criticize, Argue, Challenge, Urge 使信服、批评、论证、挑战、敦促
Entertain / 娱乐Entertain, Engage, Amuse, Move, Inspire 娱乐、吸引、逗乐、感动、激励

Note: "Teach" sits between Inform and Persuade — it builds knowledge AND changes behavior. "Reveal" sits between Inform and Persuade — it shares facts AND creates emotional impact.

"教导"介于告知和说服之间——它既建立知识又改变行为。"揭示"介于告知和说服之间——它既分享事实又创造情感冲击。
📘 Part 3 — How to Choose the Right Purpose Verb 如何选择正确的目的动词

Ask these questions / 问这些问题

1. Does the author step by step make something clear? → Explain / 解释

2. Does the author uncover something hidden or shocking? → Reveal / 揭示

3. Does the author want you to arrive at a conclusion through logic? → Convince / 使信服

4. Does the author point out what is wrong with something? → Criticize / 批评

5. Does the author build a skill or ability in you? → Teach / 教导

6. Does the author tell a story to create emotion and enjoyment? → Entertain / 娱乐

📝 Example: Reveal vs. Criticize / 例子：揭示 vs 批评
"Passage A: The company had been hiding pollution data for 30 years. Workers were never told what they were breathing."
段落A：这家公司隐瞒污染数据长达30年，工人从未被告知他们在呼吸什么。
"Passage B: This company's behavior is morally inexcusable. Their greed destroyed lives and they should be held accountable."
段落B：这家公司的行为在道德上无可辩解，他们的贪婪毁掉了无数生命，应该被追责。
Passage A reveals hidden facts. Passage B criticizes by making a moral judgment. Both deal with the same issue, but the author's purpose is different. / 段落A揭示隐藏的事实，段落B通过做出道德判断来批评。两者涉及同一问题，但作者目的不同。
💡 Pro Tip for Analysis / 分析技巧 In your SE essays, never just say "the author's purpose is to inform." Instead, use a specific verb and explain HOW: "The author's purpose is to reveal the hidden dangers of social media by using statistics and shocking examples."
在你的SE作文中，永远不要只说"作者的目的是告知"。而要使用具体动词并解释如何："作者的目的是通过使用统计数据和令人震惊的例子来揭示社交媒体的隐藏危险。"
📝 Example: Weak vs. Strong Analysis / 例子：弱分析 vs 强分析
❌ Weak: "The author's purpose is to persuade."
✅ Strong: "The author's purpose is to convince readers that phone bans in schools improve student wellbeing by citing research showing reduced anxiety and higher test scores."
❌ 弱：作者的目的是说服。✅ 强：作者的目的是通过引用显示焦虑减少和考试成绩提高的研究，使读者信服学校禁止手机能改善学生的整体状态。
