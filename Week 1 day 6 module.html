Zhou, Qin & Han Dynasties / Philosophers of the Warring States overview / Confucianism · 周朝、秦朝与汉朝 / 战国时期哲学家概述 / 儒家思想

From the Zhou to the Han · 从周朝到汉朝

The Zhou Dynasty (1046–256 BCE) was China's longest-ruling dynasty, but its later centuries — known as the Warring States period (475–221 BCE) — were marked by near-constant conflict between rival kingdoms. Out of this chaos, the short-lived but powerful Qin Dynasty (221–206 BCE) unified China for the first time, followed by the long and stable Han Dynasty (206 BCE–220 CE), which built much of the foundation of Chinese civilization that followed.

周朝（公元前1046年—前256年）是中国统治时间最长的王朝，但其后期——被称为战国时期（公元前475年—前221年）——战乱几乎从未停止，各诸侯国相互争斗。在这场混乱中，短命却强大的秦朝（公元前221年—前206年）第一次统一了中国，随后是长期稳定的汉朝（公元前206年—公元220年），它为后来的中华文明奠定了大量基础。

The Hundred Schools of Thought · 百家争鸣

The instability of the Warring States period pushed thinkers to ask urgent questions: How should society be organized? What makes a good ruler? This produced a flowering of philosophy known as the Hundred Schools of Thought, each offering a different answer to the problem of disorder.

School 学派Core Idea 核心思想
Confucianism 儒家Social order comes from moral virtue, proper relationships, and respect for hierarchy 社会秩序来自道德修养、正确的人际关系和对等级的尊重
Legalism 法家Order comes from strict laws and harsh punishment, not morality 秩序来自严格的法律和严厉的惩罚，而非道德
Daoism 道家Order comes from living in harmony with the natural way, the Dao 秩序来自与自然法则"道"和谐共处

Confucius & the Foundations of Confucianism · 孔子与儒家思想的基础

Confucius (551–479 BCE) was a teacher and philosopher who believed that a well-ordered society depended on individual moral character and correctly maintained relationships — between ruler and subject, parent and child, husband and wife, older and younger, friend and friend. He taught that virtues like ren (benevolence/humaneness) and li (proper ritual and conduct) created harmony from the family outward to the state.

The Five Key Relationships: Confucius taught that if each person fulfilled their proper role within these relationships — with respect and care flowing in both directions — the entire society would naturally become stable and harmonious.
五种基本关系：孔子认为，如果每个人都能在这些关系中履行自己应尽的角色——双方都怀有尊重与关怀——整个社会自然会变得稳定和谐。

Confucianism & Government · 儒家思想与政府

Confucius believed rulers should lead by moral example rather than force, earning the loyalty of the people through virtue. This idea — that a ruler's legitimacy depends on moral behavior — became deeply influential in Chinese government, especially once the Han Dynasty adopted Confucianism as a guiding philosophy for the state.

孔子认为，统治者应以道德榜样而非武力来治理，通过德行赢得百姓的忠诚。这一理念——统治者的合法性取决于其道德行为——对中国政府产生了深远影响，尤其是在汉朝将儒家思想确立为国家指导思想之后。

Legalism / Qin Shi Huangdi & the Qin state / Daoism & Laozi · 法家 / 秦始皇与秦国 / 道家与老子

Legalism: Order Through Law · 法家：以法立序

Where Confucianism trusted moral virtue to create order, Legalism argued the opposite: human nature is selfish, so order can only come from strict laws, harsh punishments, and strong centralized state control. The most influential Legalist thinker, Han Feizi, argued that a ruler should use clear rules and consistent punishment — not moral persuasion — to keep society in line.

儒家信任道德修养能够创造秩序，法家则持相反观点：人性本是自私的，因此秩序只能来自严格的法律、严厉的惩罚和强大的中央集权统治。最具影响力的法家思想家韩非子认为，统治者应该用明确的规则和一贯的惩罚——而不是道德说服——来维持社会秩序。

Qin Shi Huangdi: The First Emperor · 秦始皇：第一位皇帝

Qin Shi Huangdi used Legalist ideas to unify China by 221 BCE and became its first emperor. He standardized writing, currency, weights, and measures across the conquered states, built a national network of roads, and connected earlier defensive walls into what became the basis of the Great Wall. His rule was extremely effective but also harsh — forced labor, heavy taxation, and brutal punishment for dissent were common.

Achievement 成就Impact 影响
Standardized writing, currency, measures 统一文字、货币、度量衡Made trade and administration possible across a huge, diverse territory 使一个庞大多元的领土上的贸易和行政管理成为可能
Roads & early Great Wall 道路与早期长城Connected the empire and helped defend against northern invaders 连接帝国并帮助抵御北方入侵者
Harsh Legalist rule 严酷的法家统治Created resentment that helped cause the dynasty's quick collapse after his death 引发了不满，导致他死后王朝迅速崩溃

Daoism: Harmony With the Dao · 道家：与道和谐共处

Daoism, founded by the philosopher Laozi, offered a very different vision. Laozi taught that people and rulers alike should follow the Dao ("the Way") — the natural order of the universe — rather than forcing rigid laws or rituals onto society. He emphasized simplicity, humility, and wu wei ("non-action" or effortless action), the idea that the best way to govern is often to interfere as little as possible.

Wu Wei in Practice: A Daoist ruler governs lightly, trusting that society naturally settles into balance — much like water finding its own level — rather than forcing order through strict laws or constant intervention.
无为的实践：道家统治者轻施治理，相信社会自然会达到平衡——就像水会自然找到自己的水平面——而不是通过严格的法律或不断的干预来强行制造秩序。

Hinduism / Caste System / Hindu Texts (Mahabharata, Ramayana, Bhagavad Gita) · 印度教 / 种姓制度 / 印度教经典（摩诃婆罗多、罗摩衍那、薄伽梵歌）

What Is Hinduism? · 什么是印度教？

Hinduism developed gradually over thousands of years in South Asia, blending the beliefs of early Vedic peoples with later religious and philosophical traditions. Unlike many religions, it has no single founder and no single sacred text — instead it is a vast collection of beliefs, practices, and texts united by shared ideas like dharma (duty/righteous living), karma (the consequences of one's actions), and samsara (the cycle of rebirth).

印度教在南亚经过数千年逐渐发展形成，融合了早期吠陀民族的信仰与后来的宗教和哲学传统。与许多宗教不同，它没有单一的创始人，也没有单一的圣典——它是一系列由共同理念联系在一起的信仰、实践和经典的总和，如达摩（责任/正当的生活方式）、业（行为的后果）和轮回（重生的循环）。

The Caste System · 种姓制度

Hindu society in ancient India was traditionally organized into a system of social classes known as caste, which determined a person's occupation, social status, and the people they could marry. The system was generally divided into four main groups (varnas), with many more specific sub-groups (jatis) within them.

Varna 瓦尔纳Traditional Role 传统职责
Brahmins 婆罗门Priests and scholars 祭司与学者
Kshatriyas 刹帝利Warriors and rulers 武士与统治者
Vaishyas 吠舍Merchants, farmers, and traders 商人、农民与贸易者
Shudras 首陀罗Laborers and servants 劳工与仆人
Caste & Karma: Many Hindus traditionally believed a person's caste in this life reflected karma from past lives, and that fulfilling one's caste duties properly could lead to a better rebirth.
种姓与业：许多印度教徒传统上认为，一个人在今生的种姓反映了前世的业，正确履行种姓职责可以带来更好的轮回。

Sacred Texts: Stories That Teach Dharma · 圣典：教导达摩的故事

Hindu teachings are passed down through major epics and texts. The Mahabharata is one of the longest epic poems in the world, telling the story of a great war between royal families. Embedded within it is the Bhagavad Gita, a philosophical dialogue about duty, action, and devotion. The Ramayana tells the story of Prince Rama's quest to rescue his wife Sita, illustrating ideals of loyalty, honor, and righteous duty.

印度教教义通过重要的史诗和经典流传下来。摩诃婆罗多是世界上最长的史诗之一，讲述了王室家族之间一场大战的故事。其中包含薄伽梵歌，一段关于责任、行动与信仰的哲学对话。罗摩衍那讲述了罗摩王子营救妻子悉多的故事，体现了忠诚、荣誉与正当责任的理想。

Buddhism / Four Noble Truths / Noble Eightfold Path / Emperor Ashoka · 佛教 / 四圣谛 / 八正道 / 阿育王

Siddhartha Gautama & the Birth of Buddhism · 悉达多·乔达摩与佛教的诞生

Buddhism began in the 6th century BCE with Siddhartha Gautama, a prince from northern India who gave up his royal life to search for the cause of human suffering. After years of searching, he reached enlightenment (a state of complete understanding and peace) and became known as the Buddha, meaning "the Awakened One." He then spent the rest of his life teaching others what he had learned.

佛教起源于公元前6世纪，创始人是悉达多·乔达摩，一位放弃皇室生活去寻找人类苦难根源的北印度王子。经过多年的探索，他达到了觉悟（一种完全理解与平静的状态），被称为佛陀，意为"觉醒者"。此后，他将余生用于向他人传授自己所领悟的道理。

The Four Noble Truths · 四圣谛

At the core of Buddhist teaching are the Four Noble Truths, which diagnose the problem of suffering and offer a path beyond it.

Truth 真谛Meaning 含义
1. Life involves suffering 人生皆苦Suffering (dukkha) is a basic part of existence 苦是存在的基本特征
2. Suffering has a cause 苦因Suffering comes from craving and attachment 苦来自渴望与执着
3. Suffering can end 苦灭It is possible to end suffering by ending craving 通过消除渴望可以终止苦难
4. There is a path to end suffering 道The Noble Eightfold Path leads to the end of suffering 八正道引向苦的终结

The Noble Eightfold Path · 八正道

The Noble Eightfold Path is the practical guide for ending suffering, often grouped into three areas: wisdom (right view, right intention), ethical conduct (right speech, right action, right livelihood), and mental discipline (right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration). Following this path is meant to gradually free a person from craving and lead toward enlightenment.

The Middle Way: The Buddha taught a "Middle Way" between extreme luxury and extreme self-denial — neither indulging every desire nor punishing the body — as the most balanced path toward enlightenment.
中道：佛陀教导一种介于极端奢华与极端自我克制之间的"中道"——既不放纵每一种欲望，也不折磨身体——作为通向觉悟最为平衡的道路。

Emperor Ashoka & the Spread of Buddhism · 阿育王与佛教的传播

Ashoka, an emperor of the Maurya Dynasty in India, ruled through brutal military conquest before converting to Buddhism and dramatically changing his approach to governing. He renounced violent expansion, promoted nonviolence and religious tolerance, and sent missionaries throughout Asia, helping Buddhism spread far beyond India into places like Sri Lanka, Central Asia, and eventually China.

阿育王是印度孔雀王朝的一位皇帝，他在皈依佛教并彻底改变治理方式之前，通过残酷的军事征服进行统治。他放弃了暴力扩张，倡导非暴力与宗教宽容，并向亚洲各地派遣传教士，帮助佛教传播到印度以外，远至斯里兰卡、中亚，最终传入中国。

The Silk Road / Week 1 Synthesis & Review · 丝绸之路 / 第一周综合复习

What Was the Silk Road? · 什么是丝绸之路？

The Silk Road was not a single road but a vast network of overland and maritime trade routes connecting China, Central Asia, India, Persia, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean world. It flourished especially during the Han Dynasty, when Chinese silk, spices, and goods traveled west while glass, wool, horses, and precious metals traveled east.

丝绸之路并不是一条单一的道路，而是一个连接中国、中亚、印度、波斯、中东与地中海世界的庞大陆路与海路贸易网络。它在汉朝时期尤为繁荣，中国的丝绸、香料和商品向西流通，而玻璃、羊毛、马匹和贵金属则向东流通。

More Than Trade: The Exchange of Ideas · 不仅是贸易：思想的交流

The Silk Road carried far more than goods — it was a major channel for the spread of religion, technology, language, and disease across Eurasia. Buddhism traveled from India into Central Asia and China along these very routes, and innovations like papermaking eventually moved from China toward the West.

What Traveled 传播的内容Example 例子
Goods 商品Silk, spices, glass, horses, precious metals 丝绸、香料、玻璃、马匹、贵金属
Religion 宗教Buddhism spreading from India into China 佛教从印度传入中国
Technology 技术Papermaking moving from China toward the West 造纸术从中国传向西方
Disease 疾病Illnesses spreading along trade routes between distant regions 疾病沿贸易路线在遥远地区之间传播
Connecting Civilizations: The Silk Road physically linked civilizations that students have studied separately this week — Han China, the religions of South Asia, and the wider Persian and Mediterranean world — into a single connected system of exchange.
连接文明：丝绸之路将本周分别学习的文明——汉代中国、南亚的宗教，以及更广泛的波斯与地中海世界——物理性地连接成一个统一的交流体系。

Week 1 Synthesis: Comparing Systems of Order · 第一周综合：比较秩序体系

This week covered many different civilizations and belief systems, all wrestling with a shared question: how should people and societies be organized? Confucianism, Legalism, and Daoism each gave China a different answer. Hinduism organized Indian society through caste and dharma. Buddhism offered a path to personal liberation that ultimately spread across all of Asia via networks like the Silk Road.

本周涵盖了许多不同的文明与信仰体系，它们都在思考一个共同的问题：人与社会应如何组织？儒家、法家与道家分别为中国提供了不同的答案。印度教通过种姓与达摩组织了印度社会。佛教提供了一条通向个人解脱的道路，并最终通过丝绸之路等网络传播到整个亚洲。
