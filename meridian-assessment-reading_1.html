What do "Confidence range" and "Precision" mean?
Your score is an estimate of your reading level, not an exact measurement. The confidence range shows where your true reading level most likely falls. A score of 620 with a range of 585–655 means: "very likely somewhere between 585 and 655, with 620 as the best single estimate."
Precision (SE) is the size of that uncertainty — a smaller ± number means a more exact score, and answering more questions makes the estimate more precise. Small changes between two test dates (inside the ± range) may just be normal measurement noise; changes larger than the range are more likely to show real growth.
你的分数是对阅读水平的估计值，不是精确测量。置信区间表示你的真实水平最可能落在的范围。精度（SE）表示估计的准确程度——±数值越小越精确，答题越多越精确。两次测试之间的微小变化（在±范围内）可能只是正常误差；超出该范围的变化才更可能是真实进步。

About the reading range (Lexile-style): this is an unofficial estimate to help you choose books at the right level — look for books labeled inside your range. It is not a certified Lexile® measure and comes from this assessment's own scale.
关于阅读范围（蓝思式估计）：这是帮助你选书的非官方估计值，请选择标注在你范围内的图书。它不是官方蓝思（Lexile®）测量结果。
