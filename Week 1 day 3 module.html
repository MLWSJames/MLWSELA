Indus River Valley Civilizations · 印度河流域文明

A Civilization of Cities · 城市文明

The Indus Valley Civilization (also called the Harappan Civilization) flourished from around 3300–1300 BCE in what is now Pakistan and northwest India, along the Indus River. It is known for its remarkably advanced urban planning.

印度河流域文明（也称为哈拉帕文明）在约公元前3300—1300年沿印度河在现今巴基斯坦和印度西北部繁荣发展。它以其非常先进的城市规划而著称。

Major Cities: Mohenjo-daro and Harappa were two of the largest cities, each home to tens of thousands of people, with similar grid-pattern street layouts despite being hundreds of miles apart.
主要城市：摩亨佐-达罗和哈拉帕是两座最大的城市，每座城市居住着数万人，尽管相距数百英里，却有着相似的网格状街道布局。

Urban Planning & Engineering · 城市规划与工程

Feature 特征Description 描述
Grid streets 网格状街道Straight streets running north-south and east-west 南北和东西走向的直街
Drainage system 排水系统Covered sewers and drains beneath the streets — far advanced for the time 街道下方的封闭排水沟——在当时极为先进
Standardized bricks 标准化砖块Bricks were uniform in size across the entire civilization 整个文明中砖块大小统一
The Great Bath 大浴池A large public water tank at Mohenjo-daro, possibly used for ritual bathing 摩亨佐-达罗的大型公共水池，可能用于仪式性沐浴

An Unsolved Mystery · 未解之谜

Unlike Mesopotamia and Egypt, we cannot fully read the Indus script — it has never been deciphered. We also do not know why the civilization declined around 1900–1300 BCE; theories include climate change, river shifts, and the drying of the Saraswati River.

What We Don't Know: Without decoded writing, historians rely heavily on archaeology alone to understand Indus Valley government, religion, and daily life — making it one of history's great mysteries.
我们不知道的：由于文字尚未破译，历史学家主要依靠考古学来了解印度河流域的政府、宗教和日常生活——这使其成为历史上的一大谜团。

Vedic Period & Early India · 吠陀时期与早期印度

Who Were the Vedic Peoples? · 吠陀人是谁？

After the decline of the Indus Valley Civilization, a new culture emerged in northern India between roughly 1500–500 BCE, called the Vedic Period. It is named after the Vedas, a collection of sacred hymns, rituals, and philosophical texts composed in Sanskrit.

印度河流域文明衰落后，约在公元前1500—500年，印度北部出现了一种新文化，称为吠陀时期。该时期以吠陀经命名，这是用梵文创作的圣歌、仪式和哲学文本的合集。

The Caste System Begins · 种姓制度的起源

During the Vedic Period, society became organized into varnas (social classes), the foundation of what later became the Indian caste system.

Varna 瓦尔纳Role 角色
Brahmins 婆罗门Priests and scholars 祭司与学者
Kshatriyas 刹帝利Warriors and rulers 武士与统治者
Vaishyas 吠舍Merchants and farmers 商人与农民
Shudras 首陀罗Laborers and servants 劳工与仆人
Important Note: Over centuries, this system became more rigid and hereditary, deeply shaping Indian society for thousands of years.
重要提示：数百年来，这一制度变得更加僵化并世代相传，深刻塑造了印度社会数千年。

Early Religious & Philosophical Ideas · 早期宗教与哲学思想

The Vedic Period laid the foundation for Hinduism, including early ideas about dharma (duty/righteousness), ritual sacrifice, and the gods who would later become central to Hindu belief.

吠陀时期为印度教奠定了基础，包括关于达摩（职责/正义）、祭祀仪式以及后来成为印度教信仰核心的神祇的早期思想。

The Shang Dynasty in Ancient China · 古代中国的商朝

China's First Documented Dynasty · 中国第一个有文字记载的王朝

The Shang Dynasty (c. 1600–1046 BCE) is the earliest Chinese dynasty for which we have clear written evidence. It was centered along the Yellow River in northern China.

商朝（约公元前1600—1046年）是中国最早有明确文字证据的王朝。它以中国北方的黄河为中心。

Oracle Bones & Early Chinese Writing · 甲骨文与早期汉字

The Shang are known for oracle bones — turtle shells or ox bones inscribed with questions for ancestors or gods, then heated until they cracked. Diviners interpreted the cracks as answers. These inscriptions are the earliest known form of Chinese writing.

What Oracle Bones Reveal: Questions ranged from harvests and weather to warfare and royal health, giving historians a direct window into Shang religious beliefs and daily concerns.
甲骨文揭示的信息：问题涉及收成、天气、战争和皇室健康等各方面，使历史学家能够直接了解商朝的宗教信仰和日常关注。

Bronze Technology & Society · 青铜技术与社会

The Shang Dynasty produced elaborate bronze vessels, used in religious rituals and ancestor worship. Bronze-casting required organized labor and resources, suggesting a centralized, hierarchical society with kings, nobles, and skilled artisans.

Feature 特征Significance 意义
Oracle bones 甲骨Earliest Chinese writing; religious divination 最早的汉字；宗教占卜
Bronze vessels 青铜器Ritual use; evidence of skilled craftsmanship and social hierarchy 用于仪式；展示精湛工艺和社会等级
Ancestor worship 祖先崇拜Belief that ancestors could influence the living world 相信祖先能够影响现实世界

Pre-Contact Americas · 接触前的美洲

Independent Civilizations · 独立的文明

Long before European contact, the Americas were home to diverse, complex civilizations that developed independently from those in Africa, Asia, and Europe. These societies built cities, developed agriculture, and created sophisticated art and religious systems.

早在欧洲人接触之前，美洲就拥有多样而复杂的文明，这些文明独立于非洲、亚洲和欧洲的文明发展。这些社会建造了城市，发展了农业，创造了精致的艺术和宗教体系。

The Olmec: "Mother Culture" · 奥尔梅克："母文化"

The Olmec civilization (c. 1200–400 BCE), centered in present-day Mexico, is often called the "mother culture" of Mesoamerica because later civilizations — including the Maya — adopted and adapted Olmec religious ideas, art styles, and possibly early writing.

Famous Olmec Achievement: Massive carved stone heads, some weighing over 20 tons, believed to represent rulers.
著名的奥尔梅克成就：巨大的石刻头像，有些重达20多吨，被认为代表统治者。

Other Major Pre-Contact Cultures · 其他重要的接触前文化

Development of Writing & Unit Synthesis · 文字的发展与单元综合

Writing Develops Independently Around the World · 文字在世界各地独立发展

One of the most striking patterns in early world history is that writing systems developed independently in multiple civilizations, without contact between them. This shows that complex record-keeping needs naturally arise as societies grow.

Civilization 文明Writing System 文字系统Approx. Date 大致年代
Sumer (Mesopotamia) 苏美尔（美索不达米亚）Cuneiform 楔形文字~3200 BCE
Egypt 埃及Hieroglyphics 象形文字~3200 BCE
Indus Valley 印度河流域Indus script (undeciphered) 印度河文字（未破译）~2600 BCE
China (Shang) 中国（商朝）Oracle bone script 甲骨文~1200 BCE
Mesoamerica (Olmec/Maya) 中美洲（奥尔梅克/玛雅）Early glyphic writing 早期符号文字~900 BCE onward

Why Writing Developed: A Common Pattern · 文字发展的原因：一种共同模式

In nearly every case, writing emerged from practical needs: tracking trade, recording taxes, communicating with gods, or legitimizing royal power — not as art for its own sake.

The Pattern: Agriculture → Surplus food → Trade and taxation → Need for record-keeping → Writing systems develop. This sequence repeated independently across multiple civilizations.
这一模式：农业→粮食盈余→贸易与税收→记录的需求→文字系统的发展。这一过程在多个文明中独立重复出现。

Unit 1 Synthesis: Connecting the Threads · 单元1综合：联系各条线索

This week, we have studied civilizations across the globe — from the first humans in Africa, to farming villages in the Fertile Crescent, to empires in Egypt, the Hittite lands, India, China, and the Americas. Despite never having contact with one another, these societies followed remarkably similar paths:

  • Rivers supported agriculture and population growth
  • Surplus food allowed specialization and social hierarchy
  • Cities emerged as centers of trade, religion, and government
  • Writing developed to manage growing, complex societies
  • Religion and art gave meaning to life and legitimized rulers
The Big Idea: Human civilizations on different continents, with no contact, independently arrived at similar solutions to the same challenges — suggesting these patterns reflect something universal about human societies.
核心理念：不同大洲、彼此没有接触的人类文明，独立地找到了应对同样挑战的相似解决方案——这表明这些模式反映了人类社会的某种普遍性。
