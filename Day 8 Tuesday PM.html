Impact of the Crusades · 十字军东征的影响

Quick Review: Why the Crusades Began · 快速复习：十字军东征为何开始

In the last lesson, you learned that the Byzantine emperor asked Western Europe for military help against Muslim Turkish forces, and that Pope Urban II responded in 1095 CE by calling for the First Crusade to reclaim Jerusalem and the Holy Land. The Crusades continued, on and off, for almost two hundred years (1095–1291). Today's lesson looks at what these long wars actually changed in Europe and the wider Mediterranean world.

在上一课中，你学到了拜占庭皇帝向西欧请求军事援助以对抗穆斯林突厥势力，教皇乌尔班二世于公元1095年回应，号召发起第一次十字军东征以夺回耶路撒冷与圣地。十字军东征断断续续持续了将近两百年（1095–1291年）。今天的课程将探讨这些长期战争真正改变了欧洲与整个地中海世界的什么。

Economic Impact: Trade and Italian City-States · 经济影响：贸易与意大利城邦

Crusader armies needed ships, supplies, and money to reach the Holy Land, and Italian city-states like Venice and Genoa grew extremely wealthy and powerful by providing exactly that. In exchange, these city-states gained valuable trading rights in crusader-controlled ports along the eastern Mediterranean. Soldiers returning home also brought a new taste for Eastern goods — spices, silk, sugar, and dyes — which sparked renewed long-distance trade between Europe and the Middle East and Asia.

City-State 城邦Role in the Crusades 在十字军东征中的角色
Venice 威尼斯Provided ships, loans, and supplies; gained trading posts and rights in crusader ports 提供船只、贷款与物资；在十字军占领的港口获得贸易据点与权利
Genoa 热那亚Competed with Venice for the same trade routes and port access in the eastern Mediterranean 在地中海东部与威尼斯争夺相同的贸易路线与港口准入

Cultural Impact: The Flow of Knowledge · 文化影响：知识的流动

Sustained contact with the Muslim world exposed Europeans to advanced scholarship that had been preserved and expanded during the Golden Age of Islam — including the works of Greek philosophers like Aristotle, along with major advances in medicine, mathematics, and astronomy. This flow of knowledge back into Western Europe helped lay groundwork that would later fuel the Renaissance, centuries afterward.

Knowledge Doesn't Stay in One Place: Muslim scholars had preserved and built upon Greek texts during the Golden Age of Islam. The Crusades became one important pathway for that preserved knowledge to reach Western Europe.
知识不会停留在一处：穆斯林学者在伊斯兰黄金时代保存并发展了希腊文献。十字军东征成为这些被保存的知识传回西欧的一条重要途径。

Political Impact: Weakening Empires · 政治影响：帝国的削弱

The Fourth Crusade (1204) never even reached the Holy Land — instead, crusader armies were diverted and attacked Constantinople, the Christian capital of the Byzantine Empire, sacking the city. This severely weakened Byzantium and deepened the bitterness between Eastern Orthodox and Roman Catholic Christianity that had begun with the Great Schism. Two centuries of crusading also drained the resources and manpower of European feudal nobles, slowly loosening the rigid feudal structures you studied earlier in the week.

第四次十字军东征（1204年）甚至从未到达圣地——相反，十字军军队被转向，攻击了拜占庭帝国的基督教都城君士坦丁堡，并洗劫了这座城市。这严重削弱了拜占庭帝国，并加深了东正教与罗马天主教之间自大分裂以来的怨恨。两百年的十字军东征也消耗了欧洲封建贵族的资源与人力，逐渐松动了你本周早些时候所学的僵化的封建结构。

Genghis Khan and the Mongol Empire · 成吉思汗与蒙古帝国

From Temujin to Genghis Khan · 从铁木真到成吉思汗

Genghis Khan was born around 1162 with the name Temujin on the harsh Mongolian steppe. His childhood was difficult — his father was poisoned by rivals, and his family was abandoned by their tribe and left to survive on their own. Through skilled alliances, loyalty from followers, and military success, Temujin slowly defeated rival Mongol and Tatar tribes. In 1206 CE, he united the nomadic peoples of the steppe under his rule and took the title Genghis Khan, meaning "universal ruler."

成吉思汗大约于1162年出生，名为铁木真，生长在严酷的蒙古草原上。他的童年十分艰难——父亲被对手毒死，家人被部落抛弃，只能独自求生。通过精明的结盟、追随者的忠诚以及军事上的成功，铁木真逐渐击败了对立的蒙古与塔塔尔部落。公元1206年，他统一了草原上的游牧民族，并取得了成吉思汗的称号，意为"全宇宙的统治者"。

A Lifetime of Conquest · 一生的征服

Once the Mongol tribes were united, Genghis Khan led devastating military campaigns across Central Asia, northern China, and Persia. Cities that resisted Mongol armies were often destroyed without mercy, creating a fearsome reputation that spread ahead of the army. Cities that surrendered peacefully, however, were generally allowed to keep their lives, their trade, and much of their daily life — a calculated strategy that encouraged surrender rather than resistance.

Fear as a Weapon: Genghis Khan's reputation for brutality toward resisting cities was deliberately spread by the Mongols themselves, since fear could win a city's surrender without a single battle being fought.
恐惧作为一种武器：成吉思汗对抵抗城市残暴的名声，是蒙古人自己刻意传播的，因为恐惧可以在不发一战的情况下赢得一座城市的投降。

The Largest Contiguous Empire in History · 历史上最大的连续帝国

By the time of its greatest extent, the Mongol Empire stretched from Korea and China in the east to Eastern Europe in the west, and from Siberia in the north down into Persia and the Middle East — making it the largest contiguous (connected, unbroken) land empire ever to exist. This was achieved within roughly a single generation, an extraordinarily fast rate of expansion for any empire in world history.

在其极盛时期，蒙古帝国东起朝鲜与中国，西至东欧，北起西伯利亚，南达波斯与中东——使其成为有史以来面积最大的连续（相连、不间断）陆地帝国。这一切大约在一代人的时间内便已实现，扩张速度在世界历史上任何帝国中都极为罕见地快。

Division After Death · 死后帝国的分裂

When Genghis Khan died in 1227, the empire he built was eventually divided among his sons and grandsons into four major khanates. This division did not mean the empire's influence ended — each khanate continued Mongol rule and trade connections across its own region for generations.

Khanate 汗国Region Ruled 统治区域
Yuan Dynasty 元朝China, ruled by Kublai Khan 中国，由忽必烈统治
Golden Horde 金帐汗国Russia and Eastern Europe 俄罗斯与东欧
Chagatai Khanate 察合台汗国Central Asia 中亚
Ilkhanate 伊儿汗国Persia and the Middle East 波斯与中东

Why the Mongols Were So Effective · 蒙古人为何如此高效

Masters of Mounted Warfare · 骑射的大师

Mongol children learned to ride horses almost as soon as they could walk, and this lifetime of practice produced soldiers who could fire a composite bow accurately while riding at full speed. This combination of speed, mobility, and deadly accuracy let Mongol armies strike quickly, retreat just as quickly, and cover enormous distances that confused and exhausted slower-moving enemy forces.

蒙古儿童几乎从能走路起就开始学习骑马，这种终身的练习造就了能够在全速骑行中精准射出复合弓的士兵。这种速度、机动性与致命精准度的结合，使蒙古军队能够迅速出击、同样迅速撤退，并跨越使行动较慢的敌军感到困惑且疲惫不堪的巨大距离。

Military Organization and Discipline · 军事组织与纪律

The Mongol army was organized using a strict decimal system — units of 10, 100, 1,000, and 10,000 soldiers — which made commanding huge armies far more manageable than in many rival forces. Promotion within this system was based on skill and loyalty rather than noble birth, a meritocratic approach that rewarded talented soldiers regardless of their family background.

Feature 特征Why It Mattered 重要性
Decimal organization 十进制组织Made commanding massive armies efficient and predictable 使指挥庞大的军队变得高效且有序
Meritocracy 唯才是举Skilled soldiers could rise regardless of birth, motivating loyalty and effort 有能力的士兵无论出身均可晋升，激励了忠诚与努力

Psychological Warfare and Strategy · 心理战与策略

Mongol commanders used spies and scouts to gather detailed information before an attack, and often used a feigned retreat — pretending to flee in order to lure overconfident enemies into a trap. When Mongol armies needed to take a fortified city, they borrowed and adapted siege technology from the very civilizations they conquered, including Chinese and Persian engineers.

Learning From Conquered Peoples: Rather than relying only on their own traditions, the Mongols absorbed useful technology and knowledge from the peoples they defeated — a flexible approach that made them even more effective.
向被征服的民族学习：蒙古人并不仅仅依赖自身的传统，而是吸收了被他们击败的民族中有用的技术与知识——这种灵活的方式使他们变得更加高效。

Pax Mongolica — the "Mongol Peace" · 蒙古和平

Once a region came under Mongol control, the empire generally provided relative safety along its trade routes, a period historians call the Pax Mongolica. This stability revived long-distance trade along the Silk Road, allowing goods, ideas, and technology to travel more safely between China, Persia, and Europe than they had in centuries — though, as you will see in a later lesson, the same connected routes could also carry disease.

一旦某个地区落入蒙古的控制之下，帝国通常会在其贸易路线上提供相对的安全，历史学家称这一时期为蒙古和平。这种稳定使丝绸之路上的长途贸易重新复苏，让货物、理念与技术能够在中国、波斯与欧洲之间比过去几个世纪更安全地流动——不过，正如你将在后面的课程中看到的，同样畅通的路线也可能传播疾病。

Prosperity in Song China (960–1279) · 宋朝中国的繁荣（960–1279年）

Economic Growth and Innovation · 经济增长与创新

The Song Dynasty introduced the world's first government-issued paper money, which made large trade transactions far easier than carrying heavy coins. Advances in rice agriculture — including new strains and irrigation techniques — produced much larger harvests, supporting rapid population growth and the rise of enormous, bustling cities unlike almost anywhere else in the world at the time.

宋朝发行了世界上最早的政府发行纸币，这使大额贸易交易比携带沉重的硬币容易得多。稻米农业的进步——包括新的稻种与灌溉技术——带来了更大的收成，支撑了人口的快速增长，以及当时世界上几乎无与匹敌的巨大繁忙城市的兴起。

Technological Advances · 技术进步

Song China produced some of the most important inventions in world history: early gunpowder weapons changed warfare forever, movable type printing made books far cheaper and faster to produce than hand-copying, and an improved magnetic compass made long-distance sea navigation dramatically more reliable.

Invention 发明Impact 影响
Gunpowder 火药Created new weapons that would eventually transform warfare worldwide 创造了最终改变全球战争方式的新式武器
Movable type printing 活字印刷Made producing books faster and cheaper than copying by hand 使书籍的生产比手工抄写更快、更便宜
Improved magnetic compass 改良的磁罗盘Made long-distance sea travel and navigation far more reliable 使长途航海与导航变得远更可靠

Trade and the Grand Canal · 贸易与大运河

The Grand Canal linked northern and southern China, allowing grain, goods, and people to move efficiently across the empire by water rather than slower overland routes. At the same time, large Chinese trading ships carried porcelain, silk, and tea along busy maritime routes throughout East and Southeast Asia, spreading Chinese goods and culture across the region.

大运河连接了中国南北，使粮食、货物与人员能够通过水路而非较慢的陆路在帝国境内高效流动。与此同时，大型中国商船沿着繁忙的航线，将瓷器、丝绸与茶叶运往整个东亚与东南亚地区，将中国的商品与文化传播到该地区各地。

The Civil Service Examination System · 科举考试制度

Government officials in Song China were chosen largely through a demanding civil service examination based on knowledge of Confucian classics, rather than purely through noble birth — opening a path, however narrow, for talented commoners to rise into government positions. Despite all of this prosperity and innovation, Song China was eventually conquered by the Mongol Empire, becoming the Yuan Dynasty under Kublai Khan — the same Mongol expansion you studied in the previous lessons.

Connecting the Lessons: The wealthy, advanced civilization you just studied — Song China — is the very empire that fell to the Mongol conquests described earlier in today's module.
课程之间的联系：你刚刚学习的这个富裕而先进的文明——宋朝中国——正是在今天课程前面所描述的蒙古征服中陷落的那个帝国。

Shoguns, Samurai and Medieval Japan · 幕府将军、武士与中世纪日本

Japan's Feudal Structure · 日本的封建结构

Medieval Japan developed a layered system of power that looks, at first glance, similar to feudal Europe. The emperor remained Japan's symbolic head of state, but real military and political power belonged to the shogun. Below the shogun, regional lords called daimyo controlled their own territories, and samurai warriors served and protected the daimyo in exchange for land and status.

Role 角色Function 职能
Emperor 天皇Symbolic head of state with limited real power 象征性的国家元首，实际权力有限
Shogun 幕府将军Held the true military and political power 掌握真正的军事与政治权力
Daimyo 大名Regional lords who controlled their own territories 控制各自领地的地方领主
Samurai 武士Warrior class who served and protected the daimyo 为大名服务并保护大名的武士阶层

The Samurai and Bushido · 武士与武士道

Samurai warriors followed a strict code of conduct known as bushido, meaning "the way of the warrior." Bushido emphasized loyalty to one's lord, personal honor, courage in battle, and self-discipline, even to the point of a samurai being willing to die rather than live with dishonor. This code shaped daily samurai life far beyond the battlefield, influencing manners, art, and personal identity.

武士遵循一套被称为武士道的严格行为准则，意为"武者之道"。武士道强调对主人的忠诚、个人的荣誉、战斗中的勇气以及自我约束，甚至到了武士宁愿死也不愿活在耻辱中的程度。这一准则塑造了武士战场之外的日常生活，影响着礼仪、艺术与个人身份认同。

Comparing Japanese and European Feudalism · 比较日本与欧洲的封建制度

Japanese feudalism and European feudalism share some clear similarities: both featured a layered hierarchy, an exchange of land and protection for loyalty and military service, and a warrior class bound by its own code of honor. But there were also important differences in how each system developed and operated.

Medieval Japan 中世纪日本Medieval Europe 中世纪欧洲
Samurai followed bushido 武士遵循武士道Knights followed a code of chivalry 骑士遵循骑士精神准则
Emperor kept symbolic authority while the shogun ruled 天皇保留象征性权威，幕府将军实际统治Kings held formal authority, sometimes weakened by powerful nobles 国王拥有正式权威，有时被强大的贵族削弱
Daimyo controlled territory under the shogun 大名在幕府将军之下控制领地Lords controlled territory under the king 领主在国王之下控制领地

Cultural Influences and Relative Isolation · 文化影响与相对孤立

As an island nation, Japan was somewhat geographically isolated from the Asian mainland, but this never fully cut it off from outside influence. Over centuries, Japan imported and adapted writing systems, Buddhism, art styles, and architectural ideas from China and Korea, then reshaped these borrowed elements into something distinctly its own.

Borrowing and Adapting: Like the Abbasids translating Greek texts or the Mongols adopting siege technology, medieval Japan shows another example of a civilization absorbing outside ideas and transforming them into something new.
借鉴与改造：正如阿拔斯王朝翻译希腊文献或蒙古人采用攻城技术一样，中世纪日本也展示了一个文明吸收外来理念并将其转化为全新事物的另一个例子。
