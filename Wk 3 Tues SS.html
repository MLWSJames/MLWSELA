American Revolution & Independence · 美国革命与独立

What Was the American Revolution? · 什么是美国革命？

The American Revolution (1775–1783) was the war in which thirteen British colonies in North America broke free from British rule and formed the United States of America. It was driven by Enlightenment ideas — the belief that people have natural rights and that governments only have power when the people agree to be governed.

美国革命（1775-1783年）是北美十三个英国殖民地摆脱英国统治、建立美利坚合众国的战争。它由启蒙思想驱动——即人们拥有天赋权利，政府只有在人民同意被治理时才拥有权力。

Thinker · 思想家Key Idea · 核心思想Influence on America · 对美国的影响

Colonial Grievances · 殖民地的不满

Britain taxed its American colonies heavily after the Seven Years' War (1756–1763) to pay war debts. The colonists were furious because they had no representatives in the British Parliament. Their slogan became: "No taxation without representation." Key events that escalated tensions included the Stamp Act (1765), the Boston Massacre (1770), and the Boston Tea Party (1773).

七年战争（1756-1763年）后，英国对美洲殖民地征收重税以偿还战争债务。殖民者非常愤怒，因为他们在英国议会中没有代表。他们的口号变成了："无代表，不纳税。"使紧张局势升级的关键事件包括《印花税法》（1765年）、波士顿大屠杀（1770年）和波士顿倾茶事件（1773年）。

The Declaration of Independence · 《独立宣言》

July 4, 1776 · 1776年7月4日

Thomas Jefferson wrote the Declaration of Independence, which formally announced the colonies' separation from Britain. It was based on Locke's ideas of natural rights. The key sentence declared that all men are created equal and possess the rights to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness — and that if a government destroys these rights, the people have the right to overthrow it.

托马斯·杰斐逊起草了《独立宣言》，正式宣布殖民地与英国分离。它基于洛克的天赋权利思想。关键句子宣称所有人生而平等，拥有生命、自由和追求幸福的权利——如果政府破坏这些权利，人民有权推翻它。

Consent of the Governed · 被统治者的同意

The Declaration's most revolutionary idea was "consent of the governed" — governments are only legitimate when the people agree to be ruled. This directly challenged the idea of kings ruling by divine right. It inspired revolutions in France, Haiti, and Latin America for decades.

《独立宣言》最具革命性的思想是"被统治者的同意"——只有当人民同意被统治时，政府才是合法的。这直接挑战了国王以神圣权利统治的观念，并在此后数十年激励了法国、海地和拉丁美洲的革命。

French Revolution Part 1 · 法国大革命 第一部分

France Before the Revolution · 革命前的法国

In the late 1700s, France was one of the most powerful kingdoms in Europe — but it was deeply unfair. King Louis XVI lived in the magnificent Palace of Versailles with his queen Marie Antoinette, while most French people were desperately poor and hungry. The country was almost bankrupt from fighting wars, including helping the American Revolution.

18世纪末，法国是欧洲最强大的王国之一——但它极度不公平。国王路易十六和王后玛丽·安托瓦内特住在宏伟的凡尔赛宫，而大多数法国人却极度贫困和饥饿。这个国家因参加战争——包括帮助美国革命——而几乎破产。

The Three Estates · 三个等级

French society was divided into three legal classes called "estates." This system was deeply unequal:

法国社会被分为三个法律阶层，称为"等级"。这个制度极度不平等：

Estate · 等级Who? · 是谁？Taxes? · 纳税？% of Population · 人口占比
First Estate · 第一等级 The Catholic clergy (priests, bishops)天主教神职人员（神父、主教） No · 不纳税 ~0.5%
Second Estate · 第二等级 The nobility (lords, aristocrats)贵族（领主、贵族） No · 不纳税 ~1.5%
Third Estate · 第三等级 Everyone else: merchants, lawyers, farmers, workers其他所有人：商人、律师、农民、工人 Yes — all taxes · 是，缴纳所有税 ~98%
The Crisis · 危机

By 1789, France faced a catastrophic food crisis. Bad harvests meant bread prices skyrocketed. Poor people in cities spent 80–90% of their income just on bread. Meanwhile, the nobility and clergy paid no taxes at all. Louis XVI called the Estates-General — a rare political meeting of all three estates — to solve the financial crisis. This meeting triggered the revolution.

到1789年，法国面临灾难性的粮食危机。歉收导致面包价格飙升。城市贫民将80-90%的收入用于购买面包。与此同时，贵族和神职人员根本不纳税。路易十六召开了三级会议——一个罕见的三个等级全体政治会议——以解决财政危机。这次会议引发了革命。

Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette · 路易十六与玛丽·安托瓦内特

Louis XVI was a weak king who could not make firm decisions. He was kind but indecisive at the worst possible time. Marie Antoinette was Austrian-born and was deeply unpopular with French people, who saw her as a symbol of royal extravagance. She was nicknamed "Madame Deficit" because she spent enormous amounts of money on clothing, gambling, and parties while the country starved.

路易十六是一位软弱的国王，无法做出坚定的决定。他善良但在最糟糕的时候优柔寡断。玛丽·安托瓦内特出生于奥地利，极不受法国人欢迎，人们视她为皇家奢靡的象征。她被称为"赤字夫人"，因为当国家饥寒交迫时，她在服装、赌博和聚会上挥霍了大量金钱。

French Revolution Part 2 · 法国大革命 第二部分

From Meeting to Revolution: 1789 · 从会议到革命：1789年

The Estates-General meeting in May 1789 quickly became a crisis. The Third Estate — representing 98% of the population — demanded more voting power. When the king refused, the Third Estate broke away and declared itself the National Assembly, claiming to represent all French people. This was an act of revolution: ordinary citizens were claiming the right to govern France.

1789年5月的三级会议迅速演变成危机。代表98%人口的第三等级要求更多投票权。当国王拒绝时，第三等级脱离并宣布自己为国民议会，声称代表所有法国人。这是一次革命行动：普通公民在主张治理法国的权利。

The Tennis Court Oath · 网球场宣誓

On June 20, 1789, Louis XVI locked the Third Estate out of their meeting hall. They found a nearby indoor tennis court and gathered there. The members solemnly swore they would not leave until France had a new constitution. This is called the Tennis Court Oath. It was a direct challenge to royal authority and showed that the revolution had begun in earnest.

1789年6月20日，路易十六将第三等级锁在会议厅外。他们找到附近的一个室内网球场并在那里集会。成员们庄严宣誓，在法国拥有新宪法之前不会离开。这被称为网球场宣誓。这是对王权的直接挑战，表明革命已经认真开始了。

The Storming of the Bastille — July 14, 1789 · 攻占巴士底狱——1789年7月14日

The Spark · 导火索

Louis XVI dismissed his popular financial advisor Necker on July 11, 1789. Parisians saw this as proof the king was preparing to crush the revolution by force — troops were gathering outside Paris. Panic and anger exploded. On July 14, a crowd of Parisians attacked the Bastille, an old royal prison that symbolized royal tyranny. After hours of fighting, the fortress fell. The governor was killed and his head was paraded through the streets.

1789年7月11日，路易十六解雇了他深受欢迎的财政顾问内克尔。巴黎人认为这证明国王正准备以武力镇压革命——军队正在巴黎郊外集结。恐慌和愤怒爆发了。7月14日，一群巴黎市民攻打了巴士底狱——一座象征王权专制的古老皇家监狱。经过数小时的战斗，要塞陷落。总督被杀，其头颅被游街示众。

Why July 14 Matters · 为什么7月14日很重要

The Bastille only held 7 prisoners when it fell — but that was not the point. It was a symbol of royal oppression. When it fell, it sent a message across France and all of Europe: the people had power. July 14 is still celebrated as Bastille Day — France's national holiday — every year.

巴士底狱陷落时只关押了7名囚犯——但这不是重点。它是王权压迫的象征。它的陷落向法国和整个欧洲发出了一个信息：人民拥有力量。7月14日至今仍每年作为攻占巴士底狱纪念日——法国的国庆节——被庆祝。

Declaration of the Rights of Man · 《人权宣言》

On August 26, 1789, the National Assembly adopted the Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen. Inspired directly by the American Declaration of Independence and Enlightenment philosophy, it declared: liberty, property, security, and resistance to oppression are natural rights of all men. It stated that the law must be the same for everyone, and that political power comes from the nation — not the king.

1789年8月26日，国民议会通过了《人权宣言》。直接受美国《独立宣言》和启蒙哲学启发，它宣称：自由、财产、安全和对压迫的抵抗是所有人的天赋权利。它声明法律对所有人必须平等，政治权力来自国家——而不是国王。

Reign of Terror · 恐怖统治

What Was the Reign of Terror? · 什么是恐怖统治？

The Reign of Terror (September 1793 – July 1794) was the most violent phase of the French Revolution. A radical political group called the Jacobins, led by Maximilien Robespierre, took control of France. They believed the revolution was in danger from enemies inside and outside France. To protect it, they executed anyone they suspected of being an enemy — including the king, the queen, and eventually each other.

恐怖统治（1793年9月-1794年7月）是法国大革命最暴力的阶段。由马克西米利安·罗伯斯庇尔领导的激进政治团体雅各宾派控制了法国。他们认为革命正面临来自法国内外敌人的危险。为了保护革命，他们处决了任何他们怀疑是敌人的人——包括国王、王后，最终甚至是彼此。

Robespierre and the Jacobins · 罗伯斯庇尔与雅各宾派

Robespierre believed he was saving the revolution from its enemies. He called terror "an emanation of virtue" — the idea that killing enemies was morally necessary to protect the people's freedom. The Committee of Public Safety created Revolutionary Tribunals — courts that condemned thousands to death with little evidence. The guillotine became the symbol of the Terror: a fast, mechanical execution machine used on the rich, the poor, the guilty, and the innocent alike.

罗伯斯庇尔认为他在拯救革命免受敌人侵害。他称恐怖是"美德的体现"——认为杀死敌人在道德上是保护人民自由的必要手段。公共安全委员会创建了革命法庭——以极少的证据将数千人判处死刑的法庭。断头台成为恐怖统治的象征：一种对富人、穷人、有罪者和无辜者一视同仁的快速机械处决机器。

European Coalition Wars · 欧洲联盟战争

The execution of King Louis XVI horrified the monarchies of Europe. Austria, Prussia, Britain, Spain, and others formed a coalition to crush the French Revolution before it spread to their own countries. France found itself at war with most of Europe — which Robespierre used to justify even more extreme measures at home. External war and internal terror fed each other.

路易十六被处决使欧洲各君主制国家感到震惊。奥地利、普鲁士、英国、西班牙等国组成联盟，在法国大革命蔓延到本国之前将其扼杀。法国发现自己与大多数欧洲国家处于战争状态——罗伯斯庇尔用这一点来为国内更极端的措施辩护。外部战争和内部恐怖相互助长。

Haitian Revolution · 海地革命

Saint-Domingue: The Richest Colony in the World · 圣多明各：世界上最富有的殖民地

In the late 1700s, the French colony of Saint-Domingue (modern-day Haiti) was the most profitable colony in the world. It produced about 40% of Europe's sugar and 60% of its coffee. This enormous wealth was built entirely on the labor of enslaved African people. The colony was a brutal slave society where the enslaved population outnumbered free people by more than 10 to 1.

18世纪末，法国殖民地圣多明各（今海地）是世界上最有利可图的殖民地。它生产了欧洲约40%的糖和60%的咖啡。这笔巨大的财富完全建立在被奴役的非洲人的劳动上。这个殖民地是一个残酷的奴隶社会，被奴役人口与自由人的比例超过10比1。

Seeds of Revolt · 反抗的种子

How the French Revolution Sparked Haiti · 法国大革命如何点燃海地

When the French Revolution began in 1789, it sent a contradictory message to Saint-Domingue. The Declaration of the Rights of Man said all men are free and equal — but France did not apply this to its colonies. Free people of color like Vincent Ogé demanded equal rights, were refused, and were executed after a failed revolt in 1790. The enslaved population paid close attention. They saw that the colonial system was fragile and could be challenged.

1789年法国大革命爆发时，它向圣多明各传递了一个矛盾的信息。《人权宣言》说所有人都是自由平等的——但法国并没有将这一点应用于其殖民地。像樊尚·奥热这样的自由有色人要求平等权利，遭到拒绝，并在1790年起义失败后被处决。被奴役的人口密切关注这一切。他们看到殖民制度是脆弱的，可以被挑战。

The Bois Caiman Ceremony · 布瓦卡伊曼仪式

On the night of August 14, 1791, enslaved leaders gathered in a forest called Bois Caiman for a secret ceremony. Led by a Vodou priest named Dutty Boukman, they made a pact to rise up together. Eight days later, on August 22, 1791, the enslaved people of northern Saint-Domingue launched a massive coordinated rebellion. Within weeks, hundreds of plantations were burning across the north. The Haitian Revolution had begun.

1791年8月14日夜间，被奴役的领导者在一片名为布瓦卡伊曼的森林中聚集，举行秘密仪式。在巫毒教祭司杜提·布克曼的领导下，他们立誓一起起义。八天后，1791年8月22日，圣多明各北部的被奴役者发动了大规模协调起义。几周之内，北部数百个种植园相继燃起烈火。海地革命开始了。

Why the Haitian Revolution Was Unique · 为什么海地革命是独一无二的

The Haitian Revolution was the only successful slave revolt in world history that led to the creation of a new nation. It proved that enslaved people could organize, fight, and win against a powerful colonial power. Haiti declared independence on January 1, 1804 — becoming the first Black republic in the world and only the second country in the Western Hemisphere to win independence (after the United States).

海地革命是世界历史上唯一一次成功的奴隶起义，导致了新国家的建立。它证明了被奴役的人可以组织、战斗并战胜强大的殖民势力。海地于1804年1月1日宣布独立——成为世界上第一个黑人共和国，也是西半球第二个赢得独立的国家（仅次于美国）。
