The Americas Before Columbus · 哥伦布之前的美洲

A World Apart · 一个独立的世界

For thousands of years, the peoples of the Americas developed their civilizations almost entirely cut off from Africa, Europe, and Asia. No major trade routes connected the two hemispheres, so American societies built their own systems of agriculture, government, religion, and engineering — with no contact with the empires you have studied so far this unit. This isolation is one of the most important facts to remember about the Americas before 1492.

几千年来，美洲的居民几乎完全与非洲、欧洲和亚洲隔绝，独立发展出自己的文明。两个半球之间没有主要的贸易路线相连，因此美洲社会建立了属于自己的农业、政府、宗教与工程体系——与你在本单元目前所学的帝国毫无接触。这种孤立性是关于1492年之前美洲最重要的事实之一。

The Aztec Empire: A City on a Lake · 阿兹特克帝国：湖上之城

The Aztecs built their capital, Tenochtitlan, on an island in Lake Texcoco in the Valley of Mexico. To feed a population of perhaps 200,000 people, Aztec farmers built chinampas — artificial floating gardens woven from reeds and piled with rich mud, anchored to the lake bottom. The Aztec Empire was ruled through a Triple Alliance of three city-states, which conquered neighboring peoples and forced them to pay tribute — goods, food, and labor — rather than ruling them directly.

阿兹特克人将都城特诺奇蒂特兰建在墨西哥谷地特斯科科湖中的一座岛屿上。为了养活约20万人口，阿兹特克农民建造了奇南帕——用芦苇编织、堆上肥沃淤泥并固定于湖底的人工浮动园地。阿兹特克帝国由三个城邦组成的三方联盟统治，这些城邦征服周边民族，并强迫他们缴纳贡品——物资、粮食与劳力，而不是直接统治他们。

The Inca Empire: Engineering the Andes · 印加帝国：征服安第斯山脉的工程

Centered at Cuzco, the Inca Empire stretched roughly 4,000 km along the Andes Mountains, making it the largest empire in the pre-Columbian Americas. The Inca built an extraordinary network of stone roads and rope suspension bridges across extreme mountain terrain, and used quipu — knotted cords — to record numbers and information, since they had no written alphabet. Conquered peoples owed labor service called the mit'a system, working on roads, farms, and temples instead of paying goods directly.

以库斯科为中心的印加帝国沿安第斯山脉延伸约4000公里，是哥伦布之前美洲最大的帝国。印加人在极端的山地地形中修建了非凡的石路网络与绳索吊桥，并使用奇普——打结的绳索——来记录数字与信息，因为他们没有书写文字。被征服的民族需要承担称为米塔制度的劳役，负责修筑道路、耕种与建造神庙，而不是直接缴纳物资。

Deeper Roots: Earlier American Civilizations · 更深的根源：更早的美洲文明

The Aztec and Inca were not the first complex societies in the Americas — they built on traditions going back thousands of years. The Olmec of Mexico's Gulf Coast (c. 1200–400 BCE) carved giant stone heads and are often called the "mother culture" of later Mesoamerican civilizations. The Maya developed a true writing system, advanced mathematics, and precise calendars in Central America. The Mississippian culture of North America built massive earthen mounds, including the city of Cahokia near modern St. Louis. In South America, the Chavín culture spread shared religious art and ideas through the Andes centuries before the Inca.

阿兹特克与印加并不是美洲最早的复杂社会——他们建立在可追溯至几千年前的传统之上。墨西哥湾沿岸的奥尔梅克文明（约公元前1200年至前400年）雕刻了巨大的石头人头像，常被称为后来中美洲文明的「母文化」。玛雅文明在中美洲发展出了真正的文字系统、先进的数学与精确的历法。北美洲的密西西比文化建造了巨大的土丘，包括位于今圣路易斯附近的卡霍基亚城。在南美洲，查文文化早在印加帝国之前几个世纪，就已经在安第斯地区传播共同的宗教艺术与理念。

One Continent, Many Civilizations: Just as Afro-Eurasia had many distinct empires and cultures, the Americas were home to a long, varied history of complex civilizations long before 1492.
一片大陆，多个文明：正如亚非欧大陆拥有许多不同的帝国与文化一样，早在1492年之前，美洲就已经存在悠久而多样的复杂文明历史。

The Black Death · 黑死病

A Disease Born on the Steppe · 起源于草原的疾病

The bubonic plague was caused by a bacterium called Yersinia pestis, which lived naturally among rodents such as marmots on the Central Asian steppe. Fleas that fed on infected rodents could pass the bacteria to other animals — and to humans. When the climate or rodent populations shifted, infected fleas began biting new hosts, including the black rats that traveled aboard merchant caravans and ships.

黑死病由一种名为鼠疫耶尔森菌的细菌引起，这种细菌天然存在于中亚草原上的旱獭等啮齿动物体内。吸食被感染啮齿动物血液的跳蚤，可以将细菌传染给其他动物——以及人类。当气候或啮齿动物种群发生变化时，被感染的跳蚤开始叮咬新的宿主，包括随商队与船只旅行的黑鼠。

Riding the Trade Routes: The Silk Road and Kaffa · 沿贸易路线传播：丝绸之路与卡法

You have already learned that the Pax Mongolica made Silk Road trade safer and faster than ever before. Unfortunately, the same roads that carried silk, spices, and ideas also carried infected fleas and rats westward from Central Asia. In 1346, Mongol forces besieging the Genoese trading port of Kaffa on the Black Sea reportedly catapulted plague-infected bodies over the city walls. Genoese merchants who fled Kaffa by ship may have carried the disease directly into Mediterranean port cities.

你已经学过，蒙古和平使丝绸之路的贸易比以往任何时候都更安全、更快捷。不幸的是，运送丝绸、香料与思想的同一条道路，也将被感染的跳蚤与老鼠从中亚带向西方。1346年，围攻黑海沿岸热那亚贸易港口卡法的蒙古军队，据传曾用投石机将感染瘟疫的尸体抛入城内。从卡法乘船逃离的热那亚商人，可能将这种疾病直接带入了地中海港口城市。

Connecting the Lessons: The very trade networks that made the Mongol Empire and the Silk Road so prosperous also became the highway that carried one of history's deadliest diseases into Europe.
课程之间的联系：正是那些使蒙古帝国与丝绸之路繁荣昌盛的贸易网络，也成为了将历史上最致命的疾病之一传入欧洲的通道。

Spreading Through Europe, 1347–1351 · 在欧洲蔓延，1347年至1351年

From Mediterranean ports, the plague spread rapidly along trade routes and rivers into the heart of Europe between 1347 and 1351, eventually killing an estimated one-third of Europe's population. Cities with crowded conditions and poor sanitation suffered especially severe losses, while some isolated rural areas were affected less. Medical understanding at the time could not explain bacteria or fleas, so people had no effective way to stop the disease's spread.

从地中海港口出发，瘟疫沿着贸易路线与河流迅速蔓延至欧洲腹地，在1347年至1351年间，最终导致欧洲约三分之一的人口死亡。拥挤、卫生条件差的城市遭受了尤为严重的损失，而一些孤立的农村地区受到的影响则较小。当时的医学知识无法解释细菌或跳蚤，因此人们没有有效的方法来阻止疾病的蔓延。

Economic and Social Aftershocks · 经济与社会的余震

The sudden loss of so many workers created a severe labor shortage, and surviving peasants and laborers could demand higher wages for the first time in generations — a major blow to the rigid feudal and serfdom system you studied earlier in the week. With fewer farmers, some landlords switched to less labor-intensive sheep farming. Terrified and unable to explain the disease scientifically, communities across Europe also turned to dangerous scapegoating, unfairly blaming Jewish communities and other minority groups for spreading the plague, leading to violent persecution.

如此多劳动力的骤然损失造成了严重的劳动力短缺，幸存的农民与劳工历经数代以来第一次能够要求更高的工资——这对你本周早些时候所学的僵化封建与农奴制度造成了重大打击。由于农民减少，一些地主转而从事劳动密集程度较低的养羊业。由于恐惧且无法用科学解释这种疾病，欧洲各地的社区也出现了危险的替罪羊行为，不公正地将瘟疫的传播归咎于犹太社区及其他少数群体，导致了暴力迫害。

Mansa Musa and the Mali Empire · 曼萨·穆萨与马里帝国

Gold, Salt, and an Empire Between Two Deserts · 黄金、盐与两片沙漠之间的帝国

The Mali Empire rose to power in West Africa by controlling the trade of two precious resources moving in opposite directions: gold, mined in forests to the south, and salt, mined from desert deposits to the north. Mali's rulers taxed this trade heavily, growing immensely wealthy as goods passed through cities like Timbuktu and Gao on their way across the Sahara.

马里帝国通过控制两种朝相反方向流动的珍贵资源贸易，在西非崛起为强权：南方森林中开采的黄金，以及北方沙漠中开采的盐。马里的统治者对这一贸易征收重税，随着货物经过廷巴克图与加奥等城市穿越撒哈拉沙漠，国力变得极其富裕。

Mansa Musa's Famous Pilgrimage · 曼萨·穆萨著名的朝圣之旅

Mansa Musa ruled Mali at the height of its wealth and power in the early 1300s. In 1324, he set out on a hajj — a pilgrimage to the Muslim holy city of Mecca — bringing an enormous caravan that reportedly included thousands of soldiers, attendants, and so much gold that he gave generously to people along the way. When Mansa Musa passed through Cairo, Egypt, he gave away so much gold that he reportedly disrupted the local economy, lowering the value of gold there for years afterward.

曼萨·穆萨在14世纪初马里国力最强盛、最富裕的时期统治该国。1324年，他出发前往穆斯林圣城麦加进行朝觐，率领着一支规模庞大的商队，据传随行人员包括数千名士兵、随从，以及大量黄金，他在途中慷慨地施舍给当地人。当曼萨·穆萨途经埃及开罗时，据传他施舍的黄金数量之多，扰乱了当地经济，使该地黄金的价值在此后多年内都有所下降。

Wealth That Reshaped a Region: Mansa Musa's pilgrimage is one of the clearest historical examples of how one individual's wealth could ripple outward and affect economies far beyond his own empire's borders.
重塑一个地区的财富：曼萨·穆萨的朝圣之旅是历史上最清晰的例子之一，说明个人的财富如何能够向外波及，影响远超出自己帝国边界的经济。

Camels, Caravans, and Timbuktu · 骆驼、商队与廷巴克图

Crossing the Sahara required specialized knowledge: caravans relied on camels, which could survive long stretches without water, and experienced guides who knew the location of scattered desert oases. The city of Timbuktu grew into a major center not just for trade, but for Islamic scholarship, attracting scholars, books, and students from across West Africa and the wider Muslim world.

穿越撒哈拉需要专门的知识：商队依靠能够长时间不饮水生存的骆驼，以及熟知散布于沙漠中绿洲位置的经验丰富的向导。廷巴克图城发展成为一个重要中心，不仅是贸易中心，也是伊斯兰学术中心，吸引着来自西非各地及更广阔穆斯林世界的学者、书籍与学生。

Looking East: The Swahili Coast and the Indian Ocean · 放眼东方：斯瓦希里海岸与印度洋

While Mali connected West Africa to the Mediterranean across the Sahara, a very different trade network linked East Africa to the wider world. Independent Swahili city-states such as Kilwa and Mogadishu grew wealthy along the East African coast by trading with merchants across the Indian Ocean, exchanging African gold, ivory, and enslaved people for porcelain, textiles, and spices arriving from Arabia, India, and even China.

Oral Traditions and African Historiography · 口述传统与非洲史学方法论

What Is Oral Tradition? · 什么是口述传统？

Oral tradition is history, knowledge, and culture passed down by speaking and memory across generations, rather than through writing. In much of West Africa, specially trained storytellers called griots served as living libraries, memorizing the histories of kings, kingdoms, important events, and family lineages, and performing them at ceremonies and gatherings for centuries.

口述传统是通过口头讲述与记忆，而非文字书写，代代相传的历史、知识与文化。在西非大部分地区，被称为格里奥的专门训练的说书人，充当着活的图书馆，记忆着国王、王国、重大事件与家族血统的历史，并在数百年间于仪式与聚会中讲述这些历史。

Historiography: How Do We Know What We Know? · 史学方法论：我们如何知晓我们所知道的？

Historiography is the study of how history itself gets written, including how historians decide which sources to trust and how to use them. Written documents are useful because they can be dated and directly quoted, but they are not the only valid form of historical evidence — and in many parts of the world, including much of pre-colonial Africa, written records were far less common than in Eurasia.

史学方法论是研究历史本身如何被书写的学科，包括历史学家如何决定信任哪些史料以及如何使用它们。书面文献之所以有用，是因为它们可以被确定年代并直接引用，但它们并不是唯一有效的历史证据形式——而在包括前殖民时期非洲大部分地区在内的世界许多地方，书面记录远不如亚欧大陆常见。

Why Historians Value Oral Sources · 历史学家为何重视口述史料

Oral traditions can preserve detailed and accurate information across generations, especially when memorization is treated as a formal, trained profession, as it was for griots. Historians today often cross-check oral traditions against archaeology, other written accounts from neighboring regions, and even DNA or linguistic evidence, using all available tools together rather than dismissing oral history just because it was not written down at the time.

口述传统能够在数代人之间保存详细而准确的信息，尤其是当记忆被视为一种正式、经过训练的职业时，就像格里奥那样。今天的历史学家常常将口述传统与考古学、邻近地区的其他书面记载，甚至DNA或语言学证据进行交叉核实，综合运用所有可用的工具，而不是仅仅因为口述历史当时没有被书写下来就将其否定。

Not "Less Reliable," Just Different: A trained griot's memorized history is a different type of evidence than a written chronicle — not automatically a worse one. Good historians evaluate each type of source on its own terms.
并非「不可靠」，只是不同：经过训练的格里奥所记忆的历史，是与书面编年史不同类型的证据——而不是自动地更不可靠。优秀的历史学家会根据每种史料自身的特点来评估它。

Connecting to the Mali Empire · 与马里帝国的联系

Much of what historians know about Mansa Musa, the founding of the Mali Empire, and earlier West African kingdoms comes from a combination of Arabic written accounts from North African travelers and scholars, alongside oral traditions preserved by griots and passed down within Mali and neighboring societies. Neither source alone tells the complete story — together, they give historians a much fuller picture.

历史学家对曼萨·穆萨、马里帝国的建立以及更早的西非王国的大部分了解，来自北非旅行者与学者的阿拉伯文书面记载，以及由格里奥保存并在马里及邻近社会中代代相传的口述传统两者的结合。任何一种单独的来源都无法讲述完整的故事——两者结合，才为历史学家提供了更为完整的图景。

Synthesis: Trade, Disease, and Civilizations · 综合：贸易、疾病与文明

Trade Networks as Highways — for Goods, Ideas, and Disease · 作为通道的贸易网络——商品、思想与疾病

Today's earlier lessons revealed a powerful pattern: the same trade networks that carried gold, books, and spices also carried something far more dangerous. The Black Death moved along the Silk Road from Central Asia to Kaffa, then by ship into the Mediterranean — following the exact routes that had long carried silk, paper, and ideas. Notice that the Trans-Saharan and Indian Ocean trade routes you studied today were largely separate from this path, which is part of why historians find very little evidence that the Black Death reached deep into Sub-Saharan Africa the way it devastated Europe and parts of the Middle East.

今天前几课的内容揭示了一个重要的规律：运送黄金、书籍与香料的同一贸易网络，也运送着远更危险的东西。黑死病沿着丝绸之路从中亚传到卡法，再由船只传入地中海——所走的正是长期以来运送丝绸、纸张与思想的同样路线。请注意，你今天所学的跨撒哈拉贸易路线与印度洋贸易路线，与这条路径在很大程度上是分开的，这也是为什么历史学家发现很少有证据表明黑死病像摧毁欧洲与中东部分地区那样，深入传播到了撒哈拉以南非洲。

Connectivity and Isolation: A Recurring Historical Theme · 互联互通与孤立：一个反复出现的历史主题

Across this unit, you have seen connectivity bring both benefits and dangers: the Pax Mongolica spread trade and ideas but also the plague; Mansa Musa's wealth reshaped Cairo's economy; the Crusades brought knowledge to Europe but also conflict. The Americas' isolation meant civilizations like the Aztec and Inca developed independently for thousands of years — but as you will see in the next lessons, that same isolation would soon prove devastating once sustained contact with Afro-Eurasia finally began.

在本单元中，你已经看到互联互通既带来好处也带来危险：蒙古和平传播了贸易与思想，但也传播了瘟疫；曼萨·穆萨的财富重塑了开罗的经济；十字军东征为欧洲带来了知识，但也带来了冲突。美洲的孤立意味着阿兹特克与印加等文明能够独立发展数千年——但正如你将在接下来的课程中看到的，一旦与亚非欧大陆建立起持续的接触，这种孤立将很快被证明是灾难性的。
