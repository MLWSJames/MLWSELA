Overview of Ancient Rome & the Roman Republic · 古罗马概述与罗马共和国

From Village to Republic · 从村落到共和国

According to legend, Rome was founded in 753 BCE. After expelling its last king in 509 BCE, Rome became a republic — a system in which power was held by elected officials and citizens, rather than a single hereditary monarch.

据传说，罗马建立于公元前753年。公元前509年驱逐最后一位国王后，罗马成为一个共和国——一种由民选官员和公民掌握权力的体制，而不是单一的世袭君主。

Political Institutions of the Republic · 共和国的政治制度

Roman government divided power among multiple institutions to prevent any single person from gaining too much control:

Institution 机构Role 职能
Consuls 执政官Two elected officials who led the government and army for one-year terms 两名通过选举产生、领导政府和军队、任期一年的官员
Senate 元老院A council of wealthy, experienced citizens who advised on policy and finances 由富有、经验丰富的公民组成的议事会，负责政策和财政方面的建议
Assemblies 公民大会Bodies where citizens voted on laws and elected officials 公民投票表决法律和选举官员的机构

Patricians & Plebeians · 贵族与平民

Roman society was divided into two main social classes: patricians (the wealthy aristocratic elite, who held most political power) and plebeians (common citizens — farmers, merchants, and laborers). Over time, plebeians fought for and won greater political rights, including the right to hold certain offices.

The Struggle of the Orders: Plebeians eventually created their own assembly and elected officials called tribunes, who could veto unfair laws — an early example of common people gaining political power through organized pressure.
阶层斗争：平民最终建立了自己的议会，并选出了被称为保民官的官员，他们可以否决不公正的法律——这是普通民众通过有组织的压力获得政治权力的早期例子。

Roman Military, Expansion & Economy · 罗马军事、扩张与经济

The Roman Legion · 罗马军团

Rome's military success rested on the legion — a highly organized, disciplined unit of about 5,000 soldiers. Roman soldiers trained constantly, built roads and fortifications wherever they camped, and used standardized equipment and tactics that gave them an edge over less organized enemies.

罗马军事的成功依赖于军团——一个由约5000名士兵组成的高度组织化、纪律严明的单位。罗马士兵不断训练，在驻扎的地方修建道路和防御工事，并使用标准化的装备和战术，使他们比组织较松散的敌人更具优势。

Foreign Policy & Citizenship · 外交政策与公民权

As Rome conquered new territories, it gradually extended citizenship to conquered peoples and allies. This was a key strategy — by offering legal rights and a path to citizenship, Rome built loyalty among diverse populations rather than relying purely on force.

Why This Mattered: Extending citizenship gave conquered peoples a stake in Rome's success, reducing the likelihood of rebellion and providing a steady source of soldiers and tax revenue.
为何重要：扩大公民权使被征服的民族在罗马的成功中拥有利益，减少了叛乱的可能性，并提供了稳定的士兵和税收来源。

The Roman Economy · 罗马经济

Rome's economy depended on several interconnected systems:

Economic Element 经济要素Role 作用
Roads 道路Connected the empire, enabling trade and rapid army movement 连接帝国，便利贸易和军队快速调动
Currency 货币A standardized coinage system made trade across the empire easier 标准化的货币体系使整个帝国的贸易更便利
Trade 贸易Goods flowed from Britain to Egypt to the Near East 商品从英国流通到埃及，再到近东
Slavery 奴隶制Enslaved people provided much of the labor for farms, mines, and construction 奴隶为农场、矿山和建筑提供了大量劳动力

Urban Development of Rome & Augustus's Empire · 罗马的城市发展与奥古斯都的帝国

The City of Rome Grows · 罗马城的发展

As the Republic's territory expanded, the city of Rome itself grew dramatically — eventually housing over a million people, one of the largest cities in the ancient world. Roman engineers built impressive infrastructure: aqueducts to carry fresh water, sewer systems, public baths, theaters, and the famous Colosseum.

随着共和国领土的扩张，罗马城本身也迅速发展——最终容纳了超过一百万人口，是古代世界最大的城市之一。罗马工程师建造了令人印象深刻的基础设施：用于输送淡水的水渠、排水系统、公共浴场、剧院，以及著名的罗马竞技场。

The End of the Republic · 共和国的终结

By the 1st century BCE, the Republic faced growing instability — civil wars, ambitious generals, and political violence. Julius Caesar rose to power and was eventually declared dictator for life, but he was assassinated in 44 BCE by senators fearing he would become a king.

Caesar's Heir: After years of further civil war, Caesar's adopted heir, Octavian, defeated his rivals and became the sole ruler of Rome.
凯撒的继承人：经过多年的进一步内战，凯撒的养子屋大维击败了他的对手，成为罗马的唯一统治者。

Augustus & the Birth of the Empire · 奥古斯都与帝国的诞生

In 27 BCE, Octavian took the name Augustus and became Rome's first emperor, officially ending the Republic and beginning the Roman Empire. Augustus carefully preserved the appearance of republican institutions (the Senate still met) while holding nearly all real power himself.

Republic 共和国Empire (under Augustus) 帝国（奥古斯都统治下）
Power shared among consuls, Senate 权力由执政官、元老院共享Power concentrated in one emperor 权力集中于一位皇帝
Annual elections for consuls 执政官每年选举Augustus ruled for life 奥古斯都终身统治
Senate held significant authority 元老院拥有重大权力Senate mostly advisory, real power with emperor 元老院主要起咨询作用，实权在皇帝手中

Pax Romana & Its Emperors · 罗马和平与其皇帝

What Was the Pax Romana? · 什么是罗马和平？

The Pax Romana ("Roman Peace") refers to a roughly 200-year period (27 BCE–180 CE) of relative peace, stability, and prosperity across the Roman Empire. While wars still occurred at the empire's borders, the interior provinces experienced unusual stability, allowing trade, culture, and infrastructure to flourish.

罗马和平（"罗马式和平"）指的是罗马帝国大约200年（公元前27年—公元180年）相对和平、稳定和繁荣的时期。尽管帝国边境仍有战争发生，但内部省份经历了不寻常的稳定，使贸易、文化和基础设施得以蓬勃发展。

Notable Emperors of the Pax Romana · 罗马和平时期的著名皇帝

Emperor 皇帝Reign 统治时期Known For 著名事迹
Augustus 奥古斯都27 BCE–14 CEFirst emperor; began the Pax Romana 第一位皇帝；开启罗马和平时期
Trajan 图拉真98–117 CEExpanded the empire to its largest territorial extent 将帝国扩展到其最大领土范围
Hadrian 哈德良117–138 CEBuilt Hadrian's Wall in Britain; focused on consolidation 在英国修建哈德良长城；专注于巩固疆域
Marcus Aurelius 马可·奥勒留161–180 CEPhilosopher-emperor; wrote "Meditations" 哲学家皇帝；著有《沉思录》

Life During the Pax Romana · 罗马和平时期的生活

During this period, the empire built impressive infrastructure across its territories — roads, aqueducts, amphitheaters, and forums. Trade flourished along secure roads and sea routes. Greco-Roman culture, law, and Latin language spread widely, creating lasting influence still visible today.

Why Stability Mattered: Without constant internal civil war, the empire could invest resources into infrastructure, trade, and culture rather than military conflict alone.
稳定为何重要：没有持续的内部内战，帝国可以将资源投入到基础设施、贸易和文化中，而不仅仅是军事冲突。

Fall of the Roman Empire & Comparative Collapse · 罗马帝国的衰亡与比较性崩溃

Why Did Rome Fall? · 罗马为什么衰亡？

The Western Roman Empire's decline was not a single event but a long process across several centuries, caused by multiple overlapping factors:

Political: Frequent changes in emperors, civil wars, and corruption weakened central authority
Economic: Heavy taxation, inflation, and the costs of maintaining a huge military strained resources
Military: Overextension of borders made the empire harder to defend; increasing reliance on mercenary troops
External pressure: Invasions by Germanic groups (Visigoths, Vandals, Ostrogoths) and others

政治：皇帝频繁更换、内战和腐败削弱了中央权威
经济：重税、通货膨胀以及维持庞大军队的成本使资源紧张
军事：边境过度扩张使帝国难以防御；越来越依赖雇佣兵
外部压力：日耳曼民族（西哥特人、汪达尔人、东哥特人）等的入侵

476 CE: The Traditional End Date · 公元476年：传统的终结日期

In 476 CE, the Germanic leader Odoacer deposed the last Western Roman emperor, Romulus Augustulus — an event historians traditionally mark as the end of the Western Roman Empire. (The Eastern Roman/Byzantine Empire continued for nearly another thousand years.)

公元476年，日耳曼领袖奥多亚塞罢免了最后一位西罗马皇帝罗慕路斯·奥古斯图卢斯——历史学家传统上将这一事件视为西罗马帝国的终结。（东罗马/拜占庭帝国又延续了将近一千年。）

Comparing Collapses: Rome, Persia & the Gupta Empire · 比较衰亡：罗马、波斯与笈多帝国

Empire 帝国Key Cause(s) of Decline 衰落的关键原因
Western Rome 西罗马Internal political instability + external invasions 内部政治不稳定+外部入侵
Achaemenid Persia 阿契美尼德波斯Military defeat by Alexander; internal weakness 被亚历山大军事击败；内部弱点
Gupta Empire (India) 笈多帝国（印度）Invasions (Hunas), internal fragmentation, weakened central authority 入侵（匈奴）、内部分裂、中央权威削弱
A Recurring Pattern: Across very different times and places, large empires tend to collapse from a combination of internal weakness and external pressure — rarely from just one cause alone.
反复出现的模式：在截然不同的时代和地点，大型帝国往往因内部弱点和外部压力的结合而崩溃——很少仅因单一原因。
