Key Vocabulary · 关键词汇

Transatlantic Slave Trade · 大西洋奴隶贸易

What Was the Transatlantic Slave Trade? · 大西洋奴隶贸易是什么？

The Transatlantic Slave Trade was a massive forced migration of African people across the Atlantic Ocean between approximately 1500 and 1900. European traders — mainly from Portugal, Britain, France, Spain, and the Netherlands — captured or bought enslaved African people and transported them to the Americas to work on plantations producing sugar, tobacco, cotton, and other cash crops.

大西洋奴隶贸易是大约1500年至1900年间，非洲人被强迫穿越大西洋迁移的大规模运动。欧洲商人——主要来自葡萄牙、英国、法国、西班牙和荷兰——捕获或购买被奴役的非洲人，将其运往美洲，在种植园从事蔗糖、烟草、棉花等经济作物的生产。

Scale of Suffering · 苦难的规模： Historians estimate that approximately 12.5 million African people were transported across the Atlantic. Millions more died during capture, imprisonment, and the horrific ocean crossing known as the Middle Passage. · 历史学家估计约有1250万非洲人被运过大西洋。更多人在被捕、监禁以及被称为"中间航程"的可怕海上旅途中死亡。

Cape Coast Castle — Evidence in Stone · 科斯特角城堡——石头中的证据

Cape Coast Castle in present-day Ghana is one of the most important historical sites of the slave trade. Built by the Swedish in 1653 and later controlled by the British, the castle served as a holding station where enslaved Africans were imprisoned in dark, overcrowded dungeons before being loaded onto ships.

The physical structure tells the story: narrow underground dungeons built to hold hundreds of people at a time, tiny ventilation holes, and a "Door of No Return" — the gateway through which enslaved people walked directly onto the slave ships, never to see Africa again.

位于今加纳的科斯特角城堡是奴隶贸易最重要的历史遗址之一。城堡建于1653年，后由英国控制，充当关押站，被奴役的非洲人在被装上船前被关押在黑暗、拥挤的地牢里。建筑本身就诉说着历史：狭窄的地下牢房一次关押数百人，通风孔极小，还有一扇"不归之门"——被奴役者从这里走上奴隶船，再也见不到非洲大陆。

Key Insight · 关键认识： Each leg of the triangle made profit for European merchants. The entire system was built on the suffering and forced labor of African people, who received nothing in return. · 三角贸易的每一程都为欧洲商人带来利润。整个体系建立在非洲人的苦难和强迫劳动之上，而他们什么回报都没有。

Economic Impact on West Africa · 对西非的经济影响

The slave trade devastated West African economies and societies. Coastal kingdoms that participated in slave trading gained European weapons, but this created arms races and inter-African warfare. Millions of young men and women — the most productive members of society — were removed from their communities. This caused long-term economic underdevelopment that lasted for centuries.

Some African rulers resisted the trade, while others participated as intermediaries. The trade was so powerful economically that it reshaped political alliances across the continent.

奴隶贸易摧毁了西非的经济和社会。参与奴隶贸易的沿海王国获得了欧洲武器，但这导致了军备竞赛和非洲内部战争。数百万年轻男女——社会中最有生产力的成员——被从社区中带走，造成了持续数个世纪的长期经济落后。一些非洲统治者抵制奴隶贸易，另一些则作为中间人参与其中。这一贸易在经济上影响巨大，重塑了整个非洲大陆的政治联盟。

Chattel Slavery · 动产奴隶制

The form of slavery practiced in the Americas was called chattel slavery. "Chattel" means property. Under this system, enslaved people were legally treated as objects — they could be bought, sold, inherited, and used as collateral for loans. This was a uniquely brutal form of slavery because enslaved status was inherited — the children of enslaved people were automatically enslaved too.

美洲实行的奴隶制度被称为"动产奴隶制"。"动产"意为财产。在这一制度下，被奴役者在法律上被当作物品对待——可以被买卖、继承，也可以作为贷款抵押品。这是一种极其残酷的奴隶制，因为奴役身份是世袭的——被奴役者的孩子也自动成为奴隶。

Impact of the Slave Trade: Through a Ghanaian Lens · 从加纳视角看奴隶贸易的影响

Why Perspective Matters in History · 为什么视角在历史中很重要

Most accounts of the Transatlantic Slave Trade were written from a European perspective — focusing on trade profits, ship logs, and plantation records. But the people most affected were Africans. Studying the trade through a West African lens reveals a completely different picture: communities shattered, families destroyed, and economies disrupted for generations.

大多数关于大西洋奴隶贸易的记述都是从欧洲视角写成的——关注贸易利润、船只日志和种植园记录。但受影响最大的是非洲人。从西非视角研究这一贸易，揭示出完全不同的图景：社区被摧毁，家庭被破坏，经济数代受到冲击。

Historian's Lens · 历史学家的视角： Oral traditions, community memory, and African-authored sources are essential for understanding how the slave trade was experienced from the inside. Ghana's role as a major exporter of enslaved people makes it a critical case study. · 口述传统、社区记忆和非洲人自己写成的史料，对于从内部理解奴隶贸易的经历至关重要。加纳作为被奴役者的主要输出国，是一个关键的案例研究。

Different regions of West and Central Africa supplied enslaved people at different times. Each region had distinct political systems, cultures, and responses to the trade.

Demographic Consequences · 人口后果

The loss of millions of people — especially young men and women between ages 15 and 35 — had devastating long-term effects on West African communities:

  • Labor shortage: Fewer people to farm, build, and maintain communities · 劳动力短缺：从事农业、建设和维护社区的人减少
  • Gender imbalance: Loss of young adults disrupted family formation and community structure · 性别失衡：年轻成年人的消失破坏了家庭组建和社区结构
  • Increased warfare: Competition among African states to capture people for trade intensified conflicts · 战争增加：非洲各国之间为捕获人口用于贸易而进行的竞争加剧了冲突
  • Political instability: States that traded in enslaved people grew powerful; others were weakened · 政治不稳定：参与奴隶贸易的国家变得强大，而其他国家则被削弱
Historical Debate · 历史争论： Economists Walter Rodney and Nathan Nunn have argued that the regions most affected by the slave trade are still among the poorest in Africa today — showing that this history has consequences reaching into the present. · 经济学家沃尔特·罗德尼和纳森·纳恩认为，受奴隶贸易影响最严重的地区，今天仍是非洲最贫穷的地区之一——这表明这段历史的影响延续至今。

How African Communities Changed · 非洲社区如何改变

The slave trade did not just remove people — it transformed entire societies. Coastal African kingdoms that cooperated with European traders gained access to guns, which they used to raid inland communities. This created cycles of violence: guns → raids → more enslaved people → more guns.

Some inland communities built fortified villages, moved to difficult terrain, or developed new political alliances to protect themselves. Fear and distrust spread across regions. Social bonds that had held communities together for generations were broken.

奴隶贸易不仅带走了人，还改变了整个社会。与欧洲商人合作的沿海非洲王国获得了枪支，并用来突袭内陆社区。这造成了暴力循环：枪支→突袭→更多被奴役者→更多枪支。一些内陆社区建造了设防村庄，迁移到难以到达的地形，或建立新的政治联盟来保护自己。恐惧和不信任在各地区蔓延。维系社区数代人的社会纽带就此断裂。

Scientific Revolution & Enlightenment · 科学革命与启蒙运动

What Was the Scientific Revolution? · 科学革命是什么？

The Scientific Revolution (roughly 1543–1700) was a period when European thinkers began to challenge traditional explanations of the natural world. Instead of accepting religious doctrine or ancient Greek texts as the final word, scientists began using observation, experimentation, and mathematics to understand how the universe works.

科学革命（大约1543—1700年）是欧洲思想家开始挑战对自然界传统解释的时期。科学家们不再接受宗教教义或古希腊典籍作为最终答案，而是开始用观察、实验和数学来理解宇宙的运作方式。

Big Shift · 重大转变： Before the Scientific Revolution, most Europeans believed what the Church said: the Earth was the center of the universe. After it, the evidence showed otherwise — and that willingness to follow evidence, not authority, changed everything. · 在科学革命之前，大多数欧洲人相信教会所说的：地球是宇宙的中心。之后，证据表明并非如此——这种跟随证据而非权威的意愿改变了一切。

Copernicus and Heliocentrism · 哥白尼与日心说

In 1543, Polish astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus published On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres, arguing that the Sun — not the Earth — was at the center of the solar system. This was called the heliocentric model (helios = Greek for sun).

Before Copernicus, most people believed in the geocentric model (geo = Earth at center), which the Catholic Church supported because it matched scripture. Copernicus's idea was so controversial that he waited until he was dying to publish it.

Model · 模型Center · 中心Supported by · 支持者
Geocentric · 地心说Earth · 地球Catholic Church, Aristotle · 天主教会、亚里士多德
Heliocentric · 日心说Sun · 太阳Copernicus, Galileo, Kepler · 哥白尼、伽利略、开普勒

1543年，波兰天文学家尼古拉·哥白尼出版了《天球运行论》，主张太阳——而非地球——是太阳系的中心。这被称为日心说模型（helios = 希腊语"太阳"）。在哥白尼之前，大多数人相信地心说，天主教会也支持这一观点，因为它符合圣经记载。哥白尼的想法极具争议，以至于他等到临终才将其发表。

The Enlightenment · 启蒙运动

The Enlightenment (1680s–1800s) applied scientific thinking to human society and politics. Enlightenment thinkers — called philosophes — argued that reason, not tradition or religion, should guide laws and government. Key ideas included: individual rights, religious tolerance, democracy, and separation of government powers.

These ideas would directly inspire the American Revolution (1776), French Revolution (1789), and eventually the abolition of slavery movements across the Atlantic world.

启蒙运动（1680年代—1800年代）将科学思维运用于人类社会和政治。启蒙思想家——被称为"哲学家"——主张理性而非传统或宗教应当指导法律和政府。核心思想包括：个人权利、宗教宽容、民主以及政府权力的分立。这些思想直接启发了美国革命（1776年）、法国大革命（1789年），并最终在大西洋世界各地推动了废奴运动。

Connection · 联系： Notice the irony: European colonizers used Enlightenment ideas about "civilization" to justify colonizing other peoples — even while those same ideas about rights and freedom were being used to argue against slavery. · 注意其中的讽刺：欧洲殖民者用启蒙运动中关于"文明"的理念为殖民其他民族辩护——尽管同样的关于权利和自由的思想也被用来反对奴隶制。

Protestant Reformation · 新教改革

The Catholic Church in the Early 1500s · 16世纪初的天主教会

In early 16th-century Europe, the Roman Catholic Church was the most powerful institution in European life. It controlled education, marriage, burial, and morality. Kings and emperors needed the Pope's approval for important decisions. The Church also collected taxes and fees from ordinary people throughout Europe.

By 1500, many Europeans felt the Church had become too focused on money and politics, and too far from its spiritual mission. Corruption was widespread — bishops and cardinals often bought their positions, and priests sometimes lived lavishly while their parishioners lived in poverty.

在16世纪初的欧洲，罗马天主教会是欧洲生活中最强大的机构。它控制着教育、婚姻、葬礼和道德。国王和皇帝需要教皇批准重要决定。教会还向整个欧洲的普通人征收税款和费用。到1500年，许多欧洲人认为教会过于关注金钱和政治，偏离了其精神使命。腐败现象普遍——主教和红衣主教经常购买职位，神父有时过着奢侈的生活，而他们的教区居民却生活贫困。

Martin Luther and His Crisis · 马丁·路德与他的危机

Martin Luther (1483–1546) was a German monk and theology professor who became deeply troubled by a theological question: How does a sinful human being find acceptance from a holy God?

Luther studied the Bible intensely and found his answer in the writings of Saint Paul: sola fide — "faith alone." He concluded that salvation came through personal faith in God's grace, NOT through good works, rituals, or payments to the Church. This directly contradicted official Catholic teaching, which held that salvation required faith AND good works.

马丁·路德（1483—1546年）是一位德国修道士和神学教授，他深深困扰于一个神学问题：一个有罪的人怎样才能被圣洁的上帝接受？路德深入研究圣经，在使徒保罗的著作中找到了答案：唯独信仰——"sola fide"。他得出结论：救赎来自对上帝恩典的个人信仰，而非善行、仪式或向教会付款。这与官方天主教教义直接矛盾——后者认为救赎需要信仰与善行共同作用。

The 95 Theses and Indulgences · 九十五条论纲与赎罪券

In 1517, Luther nailed (or sent in a letter) his 95 Theses to the door of a church in Wittenberg, Germany. This was a list of 95 arguments against the practice of selling indulgences — certificates the Church sold that supposedly reduced a person's time in Purgatory after death.

The sale of indulgences had been massively expanded by Pope Leo X to raise money to rebuild St. Peter's Basilica in Rome. Luther called this corrupt: you cannot buy God's forgiveness. The printing press had just been invented, and Luther's Theses spread across Europe rapidly — beginning the Reformation.

Key Consequence · 关键后果： Luther was excommunicated (officially expelled from the Church) by Pope Leo X in 1521. At the Diet of Worms, he refused to recant his beliefs, famously saying he could not go against his conscience. He was declared an outlaw. · 路德于1521年被教皇利奥十世逐出教会（正式开除）。在沃尔姆斯帝国议会，他拒绝放弃自己的信仰，据说说出了"我的良心不允许我这样做"。他被宣布为法外之人。

Calvinism and the Spread of Protestantism · 加尔文主义与新教的传播

Luther's ideas inspired others across Europe to break with Rome. John Calvin (1509–1564) in Geneva developed a strict form of Protestant Christianity called Calvinism. Calvin believed in predestination — the idea that God had already determined before birth who would be saved and who would not.

Calvinist churches spread to France (Huguenots), Scotland (Presbyterians), England (Puritans), and the Netherlands. By the mid-1500s, Europe was permanently divided between Catholic and Protestant regions — a division that would lead to decades of religious wars.

路德的思想激励了整个欧洲的人们与罗马决裂。日内瓦的约翰·加尔文（1509—1564年）发展出一种严格的新教基督教形式，称为加尔文主义。加尔文相信"预定论"——上帝在出生前就已决定谁会得救，谁不会。加尔文教派教会传播到法国（胡格诺派）、苏格兰（长老会）、英格兰（清教徒）和荷兰。到16世纪中期，欧洲被永久地分裂为天主教和新教地区——这一分裂将导致数十年的宗教战争。

Counter-Reformation · 反宗教改革

The Catholic Church Responds · 天主教会的回应

When the Protestant Reformation began rapidly spreading across Europe, the Catholic Church did not simply accept the loss of its followers. Instead, it launched its own reform movement — the Counter-Reformation (also called the Catholic Reformation). This was an effort to correct genuine abuses within the Church, reaffirm Catholic doctrine against Protestant challenges, and win back believers.

当新教改革迅速在欧洲蔓延时，天主教会并没有简单地接受失去信徒的现实。相反，它发起了自己的改革运动——反宗教改革（也称天主教改革）。这是一次努力，旨在纠正教会内部的真正滥权行为，重申天主教教义以对抗新教挑战，并赢回信徒。

The Council of Trent (1545–1563) · 特伦托会议（1545—1563年）

The most important Catholic response was the Council of Trent — a series of meetings held in the Italian city of Trent over 18 years. The Council made major decisions that defined modern Catholicism:

  • Confirmed: Salvation requires both faith AND good works (directly opposing Luther) · 确认：救赎需要信仰与善行（直接反对路德）
  • Confirmed: The Pope and Church tradition have authority alongside the Bible · 确认：教皇和教会传统与圣经一样具有权威
  • Banned: Sale of indulgences and other abusive practices · 禁止：出售赎罪券和其他滥权行为
  • Required: Better education and training for priests · 要求：更好地教育和培训神父
  • Promoted: Religious art, music, and architecture to inspire faith · 推广：宗教艺术、音乐和建筑以激励信仰

最重要的天主教回应是特伦托会议——在意大利城市特伦托举行的一系列历时18年的会议。会议做出了定义现代天主教的重大决定。

The Jesuits — Soldiers of the Pope · 耶稣会——教皇的战士

In 1540, Ignatius of Loyola founded the Society of Jesus, known as the Jesuits. This was a new religious order designed specifically to counter the Reformation. Jesuits were highly educated, strictly disciplined, and committed to spreading Catholicism through education and missionary work.

Jesuits established universities and schools across Europe and beyond — including in China, India, and the Americas. They became the Catholic Church's most powerful intellectual and missionary force, helping to keep southern Europe, Poland, and parts of Germany Catholic while also spreading the faith globally.

1540年，罗耀拉的依纳爵创立了耶稣会。这是一个专门为对抗宗教改革而设计的新宗教修会。耶稣会士受过高等教育，纪律严明，致力于通过教育和传教工作传播天主教。耶稣会在欧洲及世界各地——包括中国、印度和美洲——建立了大学和学校。他们成为天主教会最强大的知识和传教力量，帮助南欧、波兰和德国部分地区保持天主教信仰，同时在全球传播信仰。

Iconoclasm and Religious Art · 圣像破坏与宗教艺术

Iconoclasm was the Protestant practice of destroying religious images, statues, and artwork in churches. Protestant reformers believed that paintings and statues of saints and the Virgin Mary encouraged idol worship, which they saw as sinful. Protestant churches became plain and simple in decoration.

The Catholic Church responded in the opposite direction: it dramatically increased the use of religious art, music, and elaborate church decoration. This new artistic style — the Baroque — was designed to inspire awe and emotional connection to the Church, demonstrating that Catholicism was beautiful, powerful, and worth belonging to.

圣像破坏是新教徒摧毁教堂中宗教图像、雕像和艺术品的做法。新教改革者认为对圣徒和圣母玛利亚图像的绘画和雕塑助长了偶像崇拜，这被他们视为罪恶。新教教堂的装饰变得朴素简单。天主教会则朝相反的方向回应：大幅增加宗教艺术、音乐和精心的教堂装饰的使用。这种新的艺术风格——巴洛克风格——旨在激发对教会的敬畏和情感联系，展示天主教的美丽、力量和归属价值。

Wars of Religion · 宗教战争

The split between Catholicism and Protestantism did not end peacefully. Across 16th and 17th-century Europe, religious differences mixed with political rivalries to create devastating wars. France experienced the Wars of Religion (1562–1598), including the St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre (1572) when thousands of Protestant Huguenots were killed by Catholic mobs.

The Thirty Years' War (1618–1648) was the most destructive — a massive conflict fought mainly in Germany that killed up to 8 million people and destroyed entire cities. It ended with the Peace of Westphalia, which established that each ruler could determine the religion of his territory.
