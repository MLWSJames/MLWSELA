Adding to Left Wrist with Simul Melius

相得益彰
Xiāng dé yì zhāng
"You bring out the best in each other"

Classical Chinese Idiom (成语 chéngyǔ)

This beautiful four-character phrase has been used in Chinese literature for over 2,000 years. It goes beyond "better together" to express that your partnership makes each of you shine brighter.

Literal meaning: When together, you enhance each other's virtues and brilliance, making your strengths more prominent and visible.

Chinese Calligraphy Styles for 相得益彰

楷书 (Kǎishū) - Regular Script

  • Clear, structured, readable
  • Like Times New Roman in English
  • Most formal and legible
  • Each stroke is distinct and precise
  • Perfect for James's right wrist (Simul Melius foundation)

行书 (Xíngshū) - Semi-Cursive

  • Flowing, elegant, artistic
  • Like cursive writing in English
  • More fluid than Regular Script
  • Strokes connect smoothly
  • Perfect for Brittany's left wrist (相得益彰)

隶书 (Lìshū) - Clerical Script

  • Ancient, wide, flat appearance
  • Artistic and distinctive
  • Historical feel from Han Dynasty
  • Bold and architectural

草书 (Cǎoshū) - Cursive Script

  • Highly stylized, very flowing
  • Artistic but harder to read
  • Most expressive and dynamic
  • For advanced calligraphers

Tip: You might need two different artists - one for Chinese calligraphy, one for Latin lettering.

SIZE CONSIDERATIONS

Chinese characters: Four characters need adequate space. Don't go too small - calligraphy needs room to breathe. Minimum 3-4 inches total length.

Latin phrases: Four separate phrases will need careful sizing and spacing. Make sure each is readable but the overall composition doesn't overwhelm the wrist.
