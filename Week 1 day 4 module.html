Cyrus the Great, the Achaemenid Empire & Classical Greece · 居鲁士大帝、阿契美尼德帝国与古典希腊

Cyrus the Great & the Founding of Persia · 居鲁士大帝与波斯的建立

Cyrus the Great (ruled c. 559–530 BCE) founded the Achaemenid Persian Empire, the largest empire the world had yet seen, stretching from Egypt to the edge of India. Cyrus is remembered for his policy of religious tolerance — allowing conquered peoples to keep their own customs, languages, and gods.

居鲁士大帝（约公元前559—530年在位）建立了阿契美尼德波斯帝国，是当时世界上最大的帝国，从埃及延伸到印度边缘。居鲁士因其宗教宽容政策而著名——允许被征服的民族保留自己的习俗、语言和神灵。

The Cyrus Cylinder: An ancient clay artifact recording Cyrus's policies after conquering Babylon, including freeing captives and allowing religious freedom — sometimes called an early "charter of human rights."
居鲁士圆柱：一件记录居鲁士征服巴比伦后政策的古代泥制文物，包括释放俘虏和允许宗教自由——有时被称为早期的"人权宪章"。

Governing a Vast Empire · 治理庞大帝国

The Achaemenid Empire was organized into provinces called satrapies, each ruled by a governor (satrap) loyal to the Persian king. The empire built the Royal Road, a system connecting distant regions, and used a common currency and postal relay system to manage communication.

Tool of Empire 帝国工具Function 功能
Satrapies 行省制Local governors managed regions while answering to the king 地方总督管理地区并向国王负责
Royal Road 御道Connected the empire for trade, army movement, and messages 连接帝国以便贸易、军队调动和传递信息
Religious tolerance 宗教宽容Reduced rebellion by respecting local customs 通过尊重当地习俗减少叛乱

Classical Greece: The City-State World · 古典希腊：城邦世界

While Persia built one vast empire, Greece remained divided into independent city-states (poleis) like Athens, Sparta, Corinth, and Thebes — each with its own government, laws, and military. Athens developed an early form of democracy, while Sparta built a militaristic society focused on discipline and warfare.

当波斯建立起一个庞大的帝国时，希腊仍然分裂为独立的城邦，如雅典、斯巴达、科林斯和底比斯——每个城邦都有自己的政府、法律和军队。雅典发展了早期形式的民主制度，而斯巴达建立了专注于纪律和战争的军事化社会。

Persia vs. Greece: The Greco-Persian Wars (499–449 BCE) saw Greek city-states unite temporarily to resist Persian invasion — famous battles include Marathon, Thermopylae, and Salamis.
波斯与希腊：希波战争（公元前499—449年）期间，希腊城邦暂时联合起来抵抗波斯入侵——著名战役包括马拉松、温泉关和萨拉米斯。

Greek Philosophy: Socrates, Plato & Aristotle · 希腊哲学：苏格拉底、柏拉图与亚里士多德

Socrates: Questioning Everything · 苏格拉底：质疑一切

Socrates (c. 470–399 BCE) developed a method of teaching through asking probing questions — now called the Socratic Method. He believed wisdom began with admitting what you do not know. He was eventually sentenced to death for "corrupting the youth" of Athens with his constant questioning.

苏格拉底（约公元前470—399年）发展了一种通过提出深入问题来教学的方法——现在称为苏格拉底式问答法。他认为智慧始于承认自己的无知。他最终因不断质疑而"腐蚀"雅典青年而被判处死刑。

Plato: The World of Ideas · 柏拉图：理念的世界

Plato (c. 428–348 BCE), a student of Socrates, founded the Academy, an early institution of higher learning. Plato wrote that the physical world is only a shadow of a more perfect world of "Forms" or "Ideas." His most famous work, The Republic, describes his vision of an ideal, just society ruled by philosopher-kings.

The Allegory of the Cave: Plato's famous metaphor describes people chained in a cave, mistaking shadows for reality — representing how most people mistake the physical world for true knowledge.
洞穴寓言：柏拉图著名的寓言描述了被锁在洞穴中的人们将影子误认为现实——代表了大多数人将物质世界误认为真正知识的状态。

Aristotle: The Empirical Thinker · 亚里士多德：经验主义思想家

Aristotle (384–322 BCE), a student of Plato, took a different approach — emphasizing observation and evidence from the physical world rather than abstract Forms. He wrote extensively on logic, biology, politics, ethics, and drama, and later became the tutor of Alexander the Great.

Philosopher 哲学家Core Idea 核心思想
Socrates 苏格拉底Question everything; admit what you don't know 质疑一切；承认自己的无知
Plato 柏拉图True reality exists in a world of perfect Forms/Ideas 真正的现实存在于完美理念的世界中
Aristotle 亚里士多德Knowledge comes from observing the physical world 知识来自对物质世界的观察

亚里士多德强调观察和来自物质世界的证据，而非抽象理念。他广泛撰写关于逻辑学、生物学、政治学、伦理学和戏剧的著作，后来成为亚历山大大帝的导师。

Philip II, Alexander & His Conquests · 腓力二世、亚历山大与他的征服

Philip II: Building the Macedonian War Machine · 腓力二世：建立马其顿战争机器

Philip II of Macedon (ruled 359–336 BCE) transformed Macedonia from a minor kingdom into the dominant power in Greece. He created a professional, highly disciplined army using the phalanx formation with extra-long spears (sarissas), and gradually conquered or allied with the Greek city-states.

马其顿的腓力二世（公元前359—336年在位）将马其顿从一个弱小的王国转变为希腊的主导力量。他创建了一支使用超长矛（萨里沙）方阵战术的专业化、高度纪律化的军队，逐渐征服或与希腊城邦结盟。

Alexander the Great Takes Power · 亚历山大大帝掌权

When Philip II was assassinated in 336 BCE, his 20-year-old son Alexander inherited the throne — and the army his father had built. Trained by Aristotle and inspired by Homer's epics, Alexander quickly crushed rebellions and set out to conquer the Persian Empire.

The Gordian Knot: According to legend, Alexander was challenged to untie an impossibly complex knot. Instead of carefully unraveling it, he simply cut it with his sword — a story symbolizing his bold, decisive approach to problems.
戈尔迪之结：传说亚历山大被要求解开一个极其复杂的绳结。他没有耐心地解开它，而是直接用剑斩断——这个故事象征着他大胆、果断的解决问题方式。

Alexander's Conquests · 亚历山大的征服

Between 334–323 BCE, Alexander led his army on one of history's most remarkable military campaigns, defeating the Persian Empire and pushing as far as modern-day Pakistan and northwestern India. He founded over 70 new cities (many named Alexandria) and spread Greek (Hellenistic) culture across his empire.

Key Battle/Event 关键战役/事件Significance 意义
Battle of Granicus (334 BCE) 格拉尼库斯河战役First major victory over Persian forces 对波斯军队的首次重大胜利
Battle of Issus (333 BCE) 伊苏斯战役Defeated Persian King Darius III's main army 击败波斯国王大流士三世的主力军
Battle of Gaugamela (331 BCE) 高加米拉战役Decisive victory; effectively ended the Achaemenid Empire 决定性胜利；实际上终结了阿契美尼德帝国

What Is an Empire? How Empires Rise & Grow · 什么是帝国？帝国如何兴起与发展

Defining an Empire · 定义帝国

An empire is a large political unit that controls many different peoples, territories, and cultures under a single central authority — usually achieved through military conquest. Unlike a single nation-state, an empire often governs diverse populations who did not choose to be ruled together.

帝国是一个庞大的政治单元，在单一的中央权威下控制着许多不同的民族、领土和文化——通常通过军事征服实现。与单一的民族国家不同，帝国通常统治着没有选择共同被统治的多元人口。

How Empires Rise & Grow · 帝国如何兴起与发展

Historians identify several common factors that allow empires to rise and expand:

Factor 因素Example 例子
Military strength 军事力量Advanced weapons, organized armies (Persian satrapies, Macedonian phalanx) 先进武器、有组织的军队（波斯行省制、马其顿方阵）
Strong leadership 强大的领导力Ambitious, capable rulers (Cyrus, Alexander) 雄心勃勃、有能力的统治者（居鲁士、亚历山大）
Economic resources 经济资源Wealth from trade, agriculture, taxation 来自贸易、农业、税收的财富
Administrative systems 行政体系Roads, bureaucracy, governors to manage distant territories 道路、官僚体系、管理远方领土的总督
Cultural/religious policy 文化/宗教政策Tolerance (Persia) or cultural blending (Hellenism) reduces rebellion 宽容（波斯）或文化融合（希腊化）减少叛乱

Comparing Persia, Maurya & Rome · 比较波斯、孔雀王朝与罗马

Although separated by time and geography, the Persian, Mauryan (India), and later Roman empires shared remarkably similar strategies: organized bureaucracies, extensive road networks, standardized currency, and systems for managing diverse populations across vast territories.

Pattern Recognition: Despite arising independently in different regions, successful empires tend to solve the same basic problems — how to communicate, defend, tax, and govern — in remarkably similar ways.
模式识别：尽管在不同地区独立兴起，成功的帝国往往以惊人相似的方式解决相同的基本问题——如何沟通、防御、征税和治理。

Division of Alexander's Empire & How Empires Fall · 亚历山大帝国的分裂与帝国如何衰亡

Alexander's Sudden Death · 亚历山大的猝死

Alexander the Great died unexpectedly in 323 BCE at age 32, likely from fever or illness, without a clear adult heir. According to legend, when asked who should inherit his empire, he said only, "to the strongest" — leading to decades of conflict among his generals.

亚历山大大帝在公元前323年32岁时意外去世，可能死于发热或疾病，没有明确的成年继承人。据传说，当被问及谁应该继承他的帝国时，他只说"给最强者"——这导致了他的将领们之间数十年的冲突。

The Diadochi: Dividing the Empire · 继业者：帝国的分裂

Alexander's generals, known as the Diadochi ("successors"), fought a series of wars to divide his empire. By around 275 BCE, the empire had split into three major Hellenistic kingdoms:

Kingdom 王国Founded by 建立者Territory 领土
Ptolemaic Kingdom 托勒密王国Ptolemy 托勒密Egypt 埃及
Seleucid Empire 塞琉古帝国Seleucus 塞琉古Persia & the Near East 波斯和近东地区
Antigonid Kingdom 安提柯王国Antigonus 安提柯Macedon & Greece 马其顿和希腊

亚历山大的将领，被称为继业者，进行了一系列战争来分裂他的帝国。到约公元前275年，帝国分裂成三个主要的希腊化王国：埃及的托勒密王国、波斯和近东的塞琉古帝国，以及马其顿和希腊的安提柯王国。

How Empires Fall · 帝国如何衰亡

Just as empires share common factors for rising, they also share common causes of decline:

Political factors: Succession crises, corruption, weak leadership, civil war (as seen after Alexander's death)
Economic factors: Overspending on military campaigns, heavy taxation, trade disruption
Social factors: Inequality, rebellion, loss of loyalty among conquered peoples
Environmental factors: Famine, disease, resource depletion

政治因素：继承危机、腐败、领导力薄弱、内战（如亚历山大死后所见）
经济因素：军事行动过度支出、重税、贸易中断
社会因素：不平等、叛乱、被征服民族失去忠诚
环境因素：饥荒、疾病、资源枯竭

Alexander's empire collapsed primarily due to a succession crisis — no clear plan for leadership after his death — combined with the ambition of his powerful generals.

亚历山大的帝国主要因继承危机——他死后没有明确的领导计划——加上他强大将领们的野心而崩溃。
