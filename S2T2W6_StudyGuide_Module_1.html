📝 Part 1: Vocabulary Assessment — 50 Questions · 词汇测评（50题）
⚠️ The vocabulary questions test words IN SENTENCES — not just definitions. Read the entire sentence carefully before choosing your answer. The word's meaning in that specific sentence matters most.
⚠️ 词汇题将词汇放在句子中考查——不仅仅是定义。在选择答案之前，请仔细阅读整个句子。该词在特定句子中的含义最重要。
How to approach vocabulary-in-sentence questions:
1. Read the whole sentence — not just the underlined word.
2. Cover the choices. Think: what does this word probably mean here?
3. Substitute each answer choice into the sentence. Which one still makes sense?
4. Eliminate choices that are clearly wrong or change the meaning of the sentence.
5. If two choices seem similar, look for the one that fits the specific context best.
如何回答句子中的词汇题：
1. 阅读整个句子。2. 遮住选项，思考这个词在这里大概是什么意思。3. 用每个选项替换该词，哪个仍然合理？4. 排除明显错误的选项。5. 如果两个选项相似，找最符合具体语境的那个。
🔍 Tricky Word Groups — Don't Confuse These · 容易混淆的词汇组
Metaphor vs. Simile
  • Simile uses like or as: "Her voice was like honey."
  • Metaphor says it IS: "Her voice was honey."
  • No like/as = metaphor. Has like/as = simile. It is that simple.
明喻用"like/as"。隐喻直接说"是"。没有like/as就是隐喻。
Connotation vs. Denotation
  • Denotation = dictionary definition (the literal meaning only)
  • Connotation = the emotional feeling the word carries
  • "Serpent" denotation = long, legless reptile. Connotation = evil, danger, threat.
  • The denotation question asks for the LITERAL meaning only.
外延=字典定义（只是字面含义）。内涵=词语携带的情感联想。
Implicit vs. Explicit
  • Explicit = stated directly, clearly written out
  • Implicit = suggested, you must infer it from clues
  • If you can point to the exact sentence where it is written = explicit. If you had to figure it out = implicit.
明确=直接写出来，可以指向原文。隐含=需要从线索推断。
Narrator Types
  • Omniscient = knows ALL characters' inner thoughts
  • Limited = knows only ONE character's thoughts
  • Objective = knows NO character's thoughts (like a camera)
  • Subjective = shares the narrator's own feelings/opinions
  • Unreliable = cannot be fully trusted
全知=所有人的想法。有限=一人的想法。客观=无人的想法。主观=分享感情。不可靠=不可信。
Diction Creates Tone
  • Diction = the specific words the author chooses (the cause)
  • Tone = the emotional mood those words create (the effect)
  • Words like "cold, silent, hollow" (diction) create a sorrowful, bleak tone.
  • Diction is the tool. Tone is the result.
措辞是作者的词语选择（原因）。语气是这些词语产生的情感效果（结果）。
Compare vs. Contrast
  • Compare = finds similarities (signal words: both, also, similarly)
  • Contrast = finds differences (signal words: while, but, however, unlike)
  • "Both the wolf and the fox are cunning" = compare.
  • "Wolves live in packs, while foxes hunt alone" = contrast.
比较=找相同点（both/also）。对比=找不同点（while/but/however）。
❌ Watch Out — Six Common Wrong Answers · 注意——六个常见错误
Traps to avoid on the Vocabulary Assessment
  • conceal — hiding means keeping something SECRET. If a sentence shows someone sharing or revealing something openly, that is NOT conceal.
    隐藏=保密。如果句子说某人公开分享或展示，那就不是conceal。
  • furthermore — it connects two ideas that AGREE. The second idea must support or add to the first. If the two halves of the sentence contradict each other, furthermore is wrong.
    furthermore连接两个方向一致的观点。如果两个想法互相矛盾，furthermore就是错的。
  • deceive — requires deliberate, intentional lying. Simply being wrong or confused is not deceiving. There must be intent.
    欺骗需要故意撒谎。无意中犯错不是欺骗，必须有意图。
  • interpret — goes beyond just reading or counting. To interpret means to use evidence to explain meaning. Counting lines, memorizing, or copying = NOT interpreting.
    解释超出了仅仅阅读或计数。解释意味着用证据解释含义。数行数、背诵、抄写≠解释。
  • denotation of "serpent" — the denotation is ONLY the literal meaning: a long, legless reptile. Evil, danger, and dark presence are connotations, not the denotation.
    'serpent'的外延只是字面含义：无腿爬行动物。邪恶和危险是内涵，不是外延。
  • signify / symbolize — a symbol's meaning is cultural and learned, not random or personal. A black crow does NOT signify friendship or weather. Check what the symbol actually means in its cultural context.
    符号的含义是文化性和约定俗成的，不是随机或个人的。黑乌鸦不象征友谊或天气变化。
📖 Part 2: Unit Assessment — 25 Questions · 单元测评（25题）
💡 General rule: base your answer on what the TEXT says, not what you already know or believe. If a question says "according to the passage" or "according to the infographic" — find it in the text before choosing.
💡 总原则：根据文章内容作答，不是根据你已知或认为的。如果题目说"根据文章"——先在文章里找到再选。
Section A1 — Slang Passage (Questions 1–6)

This passage is about slang words like "bread" (Cockney for money) and how slang travels across cultures.

Remember:
• "Bread" = money because of Cockney rhyming slang ("bread and honey"), not hip-hop or green color.
• "Cabbage" = money because paper money is green, like cabbage leaves — a visual connection.
• Cockney speakers created rhyming slang as a private communication code in public places.
• Slang "travels across borders" through media and culture — not laws or dictionaries.
• The author mentions slang can "deceive" outsiders to show that language both connects and divides people.
• vague in context = not clear or specific.
A1部分——俚语文章（1-6题）
要记住：bread来自科克尼押韵俚语。cabbage因绿色联想。押韵俚语是私密交流方式。俚语通过媒体传播。作者的目的是语言既联系也分隔人们。vague=不清晰。
Section A2 — Symbols Infographic (Questions 7–12)

Remember:
• Hamsa in Arabic literally means FIVE (the number) — not "protection." Protection is what it symbolizes.
• Evil Eye, Hamsa, and Eye of Horus all share one thing: the idea of watching, seeing, and protection from harm.
• Gye Nyame is on Ghana's largest banknote because it represents a belief central to Ghanaian cultural identity — not because of its appearance.
• "More Than Meets the Eye" is a play on words: the symbols literally show eyes, AND the phrase means there is deeper meaning. Do NOT choose "the symbols are too difficult to understand."
• omniscience = the quality of knowing everything.
A2部分——符号信息图（7-12题）
要记住：Hamsa字面意思是"五"。三个眼睛符号共同点是保护和观察。Gye Nyame在货币上因文化重要性。标题是双关语。omniscience=全知。
Section A3 — Story: Mei and the Number 404 (Questions 13–18)

Remember:
• Parents refused the 4th-floor apartment because four (四 sì) sounds like death (死 sĭ) in Chinese. Not cost. Not height.
• The letter under the door = Mei's new school homeroom assignment (number 404).
• Wei shrugs because he does NOT share the same cultural belief. The shrug = indifference, not cruelty.
• Mother's line "You are not the number you are assigned" means numbers on paper do not define Mei as a person. She is NOT saying 404 is secretly lucky.
• Paragraph 19: "Symbols did not need to be scientifically true to be powerfully real" = THEME STATEMENT. This is the central message. It is NOT personification, foreshadowing, or a simile.
• rejected in context = refused to accept or use something.
A3部分——故事（13-18题）
要记住：四楼=四听起来像死。信件=班级号码。Wei耸肩=不持相同信仰。妈妈的话=数字不定义你是谁。第19段=主题陈述。rejected=拒绝接受。
Section A4 — Poem: Inherited Words (Questions 19–24)

Remember:
• When someone says "four" — A MOTHER TURNS AWAY. Not a grandmother smiling. Not a farmer kneeling.
• Green = LIFE to a farmer (crops, growth, survival). Not money. Not envy. Not danger. Context changes meaning.
• "Worn smooth by other mouths" = language is shaped by all who used it before. This is about INHERITANCE, not about pronunciation or practice.
• "I used to think words were mine" = the speaker realized language belongs to EVERYONE, not one person. The tone is reflective, not angry.
• "A door into what someone else once felt" = METAPHOR. Language is compared directly to a door (no "like" or "as"). NOT a simile. NOT personification.
• elaborate in the final line = DETAILED AND COMPLEX (adjective). Not vague. Not simple. Not old.
A4部分——诗歌（19-24题）
要记住：说"four"时母亲转身。绿色=生命（对农民）。词语被磨平=语言继承。词语属于所有人。"门"=隐喻（无like/as）。elaborate=详细复杂（形容词）。
🧠 Test-Taking Strategies · 答题总策略
For "According to..." Questions
  • The answer is IN the text. Find it before you choose.
  • Do not use outside knowledge or personal experience.
  • If an answer sounds plausible but is not in the passage, eliminate it.
答案在文章里。先找到再选。不要用课外知识。
For Inference Questions
  • "What can you INFER?" = the answer is not stated directly.
  • Use clues from the text plus your reasoning.
  • The correct answer fits ALL the evidence — not just one detail.
  • Avoid answers that go too far beyond what the text supports.
推断题的答案没有直接写出来。用全部证据，不只是一个细节。
For "Meaning of Word" Questions
  • Read the full sentence, not just the target word.
  • Substitute each answer choice into the sentence.
  • Ask: does this word still make the sentence logical and meaningful?
  • The answer fits the specific context — not just the general definition.
读完整句子。用每个选项替换，哪个让句子仍然合理？
For Literary Device Questions
  • Simile = like/as present. Metaphor = no like/as, says IS.
  • Personification = non-human thing performs a human action.
  • Theme statement = states the CENTRAL MESSAGE of the whole text.
  • Foreshadowing = hints at a future EVENT in the plot (not a message).
明喻有like/as。隐喻说"是"。拟人=非人类做人类动作。主题陈述=全文核心信息。
Eliminate Wrong Answers First
  • Cross out answers that use extreme language ("always," "never," "only").
  • Cross out answers that directly contradict the text.
  • Cross out answers that are partially true but miss the main point.
  • If two answers are similar, pick the one that is more specific and accurate.
排除极端语言、与文章矛盾、部分正确但遗漏要点的选项。
Question 25 — True / False
  • This question is about YOUR effort this year, not a right or wrong answer.
  • Think honestly: Did you try? Did you show up and participate?
  • Every small effort, every reading, every attempt counts.
  • You do not need to be perfect — you need to be honest.
这道题关于你今年的努力。诚实思考。每一次小小的努力都算数。
🌟 Final reminder: Read every question twice before selecting your answer. You cannot change your answer once you click — so slow down, eliminate wrong options, and commit to your best choice.
🌟 最后提醒：在选择答案之前，每道题读两遍。点击后不能更改答案——所以放慢速度，排除错误选项，然后做出你的最佳选择。
