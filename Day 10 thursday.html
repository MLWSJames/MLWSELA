Age of Exploration: Navigation & Global Trade · 大航海时代：导航与全球贸易

Why Did Europe Look Outward? · 为什么欧洲要向外探索？

By the 1400s, Spain and Portugal sat at the western edge of Afro-Eurasia, cut off from the lucrative overland spice trade that Italian city-states and Muslim merchants controlled across the Mediterranean and Silk Road. Portuguese and Spanish monarchs wanted a direct sea route to Asia's spices, silk, and gold — one that would not require paying middlemen. This hunger for a new path to wealth, combined with new navigation technology, launched what historians call the Age of Exploration and eventually created the first truly global economy.

到15世纪，西班牙与葡萄牙位于亚非欧大陆的西部边缘，无法直接参与意大利城邦与穆斯林商人控制的、横跨地中海与丝绸之路的丰厚香料贸易。葡萄牙与西班牙的君主希望找到一条通往亚洲香料、丝绸与黄金的直接海路——一条无需向中间商支付费用的航线。这种对新财富之路的渴求，加上新的航海技术，开启了历史学家所称的大航海时代，并最终缔造了第一个真正意义上的全球经济。

Borrowed Knowledge: Navigators Before Columbus · 借鉴前人：哥伦布之前的航海者

Portuguese and Spanish sailors did not invent ocean navigation from scratch — they built on centuries of knowledge from Venetian and Genoese Mediterranean traders, Arab astronomers and cartographers, and Viking sailors who had already crossed the North Atlantic to Greenland and briefly North America. Arab scholars had preserved and advanced ancient Greek astronomy, and Arab traders had long used the astrolabe to navigate the Indian Ocean. Portugal's Prince Henry the Navigator gathered this combined knowledge at a school for sailors, training a generation of explorers in the new techniques.

葡萄牙与西班牙的水手并非凭空发明了海洋导航技术——他们建立在数个世纪以来威尼斯与热那亚地中海商人、阿拉伯天文学家与制图师，以及早已横渡北大西洋抵达格陵兰、并短暂抵达北美的维京水手所积累的知识之上。阿拉伯学者保存并发展了古希腊天文学，阿拉伯商人也早已使用星盘在印度洋上航行。葡萄牙的航海家亨利王子在一所水手学校中汇集了这些综合知识，培养了一代采用新技术的探险家。

The Tools That Made It Possible · 使其成为可能的工具

Three technologies made long ocean voyages possible. The astrolabe let sailors measure the angle of stars or the sun to calculate latitude, even far from any landmark. The magnetic compass, adapted from Chinese navigation technology, let sailors hold a steady direction even when clouds hid the stars. Improved Portuguese cartography — increasingly accurate maps built from returning sailors' reports — let explorers plan voyages and pass on hard-won knowledge to the next expedition instead of starting from zero each time.

三项技术使长途远洋航行成为可能。星盘让水手即使远离任何陆标，也能通过测量星辰或太阳的角度来计算纬度。改良自中国导航技术的磁罗盘，让水手在云层遮住星辰时仍能保持稳定的航向。日益精确的葡萄牙制图技术——根据返航水手报告不断改进的地图——让探险家能够规划航程，并将来之不易的知识传递给下一支探险队，而不必每次都从零开始。

Mapping the World — and Distorting It · 绘制世界——以及扭曲世界

Maps are never perfectly neutral. The Mercator projection, created in 1569, keeps shapes and compass directions accurate, which made it extremely useful for sailors plotting straight-line courses. But it badly distorts size near the poles, making Europe and North America look much larger relative to Africa and South America than they really are. The Robinson projection, developed centuries later, sacrifices some accuracy of shape to give a fairer sense of countries' true relative sizes. Which map a society chooses to use can quietly shape how people imagine which regions of the world matter most.

地图从来都不是完全中立的。1569年绘制的墨卡托投影保持了形状与罗盘方向的准确性，这使其对规划直线航线的水手极为有用。但它严重扭曲了两极附近的大小比例，使欧洲与北美相对于非洲与南美看起来远比实际更大。数个世纪后开发的罗宾逊投影则牺牲了一些形状的准确性，以更公正地呈现各国真实的相对大小。一个社会选择使用哪种地图，可能在无形中塑造人们对世界上哪些地区更重要的想象。

Maps Carry Bias: Choosing a map projection is not just a technical decision — it can reflect and reinforce which parts of the world a culture sees as central or important.
地图带有偏见：选择地图投影方式不仅仅是技术决定——它可能反映并强化一种文化认为世界上哪些地区是核心或重要的观念。

European Voyages & Conquest · 欧洲航行与征服

Around the World: Magellan's Circumnavigation · 环绕世界：麦哲伦的环球航行

In 1519, Portuguese navigator Ferdinand Magellan set sail with five ships under the Spanish flag, seeking a western route to Asia's Spice Islands. Magellan himself was killed in the Philippines in 1521, but one battered ship, the Victoria, completed the journey in 1522 with only 18 of the original roughly 270 crew members surviving. This first circumnavigation proved beyond doubt that the Earth was round and that all the world's oceans were connected — a single, navigable global ocean system.

1519年，葡萄牙航海家费迪南德·麦哲伦率领五艘悬挂西班牙旗帜的船只出发，寻找通往亚洲香料群岛的西方航线。麦哲伦本人于1521年在菲律宾被杀，但一艘伤痕累累的船只「维多利亚号」于1522年完成了航程，原本约270名船员中仅有18人幸存。这次首次环球航行无可辩驳地证明了地球是圆的，且世界上所有的海洋是相连的——构成一个统一、可航行的全球海洋系统。

Two Different Models of Empire · 两种不同的帝国模式

Portugal and Spain built very different kinds of overseas empires. Portugal focused on the Indian Ocean, establishing fortified coastal trading posts rather than conquering large territories. Key bases like Goa on India's west coast let Portugal control valuable trading cities and tax the existing spice trade network without governing huge inland populations. Spain, by contrast, sent conquistadors who conquered and directly ruled vast American territories — toppling the Aztec Empire under Hernán Cortés and the Inca Empire under Francisco Pizarro by combining superior weapons, disease, and alliances with rival Indigenous groups who resented Aztec or Inca rule.

葡萄牙与西班牙建立了非常不同类型的海外帝国。葡萄牙将重心放在印度洋，建立设防的沿海贸易据点，而非征服大片领土。果阿等印度西海岸的关键据点，使葡萄牙能够控制重要的贸易城市，并对现有的香料贸易网络征税，而无需治理庞大的内陆人口。相比之下，西班牙派出征服者征服并直接统治了广阔的美洲领土——埃尔南·科尔特斯率军推翻了阿兹特克帝国，弗朗西斯科·皮萨罗率军推翻了印加帝国，他们结合了先进的武器、疾病，以及与不满阿兹特克或印加统治的敌对原住民群体的结盟。

Silver and the Birth of Corporate Trade · 白银与企业贸易的兴起

Spain extracted enormous quantities of silver from mines in Mexico and the Andes (especially Potosí), which financed the Spanish crown and flowed into a genuinely global trade network reaching as far as Ming China. Meanwhile, the cost and risk of long overseas voyages led to a financial innovation: the joint-stock company, which let many investors pool money and share both risk and profit. The Dutch East India Company, founded in 1602, became the most powerful example, and the Amsterdam Stock Exchange emerged to let people buy and sell shares in these ventures — an early ancestor of the modern stock market.

西班牙从墨西哥与安第斯山脉（尤其是波托西）的矿场中开采出大量白银，这些白银为西班牙王室提供资金，并流入一个真正意义上的全球贸易网络，甚至远达明朝中国。与此同时，远洋航行的高成本与高风险催生了一项金融创新：股份制公司，使众多投资者能够集资，共同分担风险与利润。1602年成立的荷兰东印度公司成为其中最强大的范例，阿姆斯特丹证券交易所也随之兴起，使人们能够买卖这些企业的股份——这是现代证券市场的早期雏形。

From Exploration to Finance: The risks of global trade voyages directly led to new financial tools — joint-stock companies and stock exchanges — that still shape the global economy today.
从探索到金融：全球贸易航行的风险直接催生了至今仍塑造全球经济的新金融工具——股份制公司与证券交易所。

Oceanic Empires & Plantation Economies · 海洋帝国与种植园经济

A New Kind of Empire: Networks of Colonies and Commerce · 一种新型帝国：殖民地与商业的网络

Between roughly 1450 and 1750, European powers built what historians call oceanic empires — vast, scattered networks of colonies, trading posts, and shipping routes that linked together Europe, the Americas, Africa, and Asia into one interconnected commercial system. Unlike earlier land-based empires such as Rome or the Mongols, these empires depended entirely on controlling the seas. Whoever controlled the most valuable trade routes and ports could extract wealth from territories thousands of kilometers from their home country.

大约在1450年至1750年之间，欧洲列强建立了历史学家所称的海洋帝国——由殖民地、贸易据点与航运路线组成的庞大、分散网络，将欧洲、美洲、非洲与亚洲连接成一个相互关联的商业体系。与罗马或蒙古等早期陆基帝国不同，这些帝国完全依赖于对海洋的控制。谁掌控了最有价值的贸易路线与港口，谁就能从远在数千公里之外的领土中攫取财富。

The Plantation Economy and Forced Labor · 种植园经济与强迫劳役

Across the Caribbean and the Americas, European colonizers built plantation economies — large farms dedicated to growing a single profitable cash crop like sugar, tobacco, or cotton for export back to Europe. These plantations needed enormous amounts of labor, which Spain initially extracted through the encomienda system, granting colonists the right to demand forced labor and tribute from Indigenous people in exchange for supposedly 'protecting' and converting them. In practice, the encomienda system amounted to brutal forced labor that, combined with disease, devastated Indigenous communities.

在加勒比地区与美洲各地，欧洲殖民者建立了种植园经济——专门种植单一获利经济作物（如蔗糖、烟草或棉花）以出口回欧洲的大型农场。这些种植园需要大量劳动力，西班牙最初通过监护征赋制来获取，授予殖民者向原住民强征劳役与贡品的权利，名义上是为了「保护」并使其皈依宗教。实际上，监护征赋制等同于残酷的强迫劳役，加上疾病的影响，给原住民社区带来了毁灭性的打击。

Rivals for Empire: Britain and France · 帝国的竞争对手：英国与法国

Spain and Portugal were not alone for long. By the 1600s, Britain and France had joined the competition, establishing their own colonies in North America and the Caribbean and fighting repeated wars over valuable sugar islands and fur-trading territory. This rivalry meant that European political conflicts increasingly played out on a global stage — a war in Europe could trigger fighting in the Caribbean, North America, and even India, as competing empires fought to control the most profitable pieces of this new global commercial network.

英国与法国很快也加入了这场竞争。到17世纪，他们在北美与加勒比地区建立了自己的殖民地，并多次为争夺珍贵的蔗糖岛屿与毛皮贸易领地而开战。这种竞争意味着欧洲的政治冲突日益在全球舞台上上演——欧洲的一场战争可能引发加勒比地区、北美甚至印度的战事，因为相互竞争的帝国都在争夺这一新兴全球商业网络中最有利可图的部分。

Connecting the Dots: Oceanic empires turned local European rivalries into global conflicts — a pattern that connects directly to the demographic and economic upheavals you will study next in the Columbian Exchange.
串联线索：海洋帝国将欧洲本地的竞争转化为全球性的冲突——这一模式与你接下来将学习的哥伦布大交换中的人口与经济剧变直接相关。

The Columbian Exchange · 哥伦布大交换

What Was the Columbian Exchange? · 什么是哥伦布大交换？

Christopher Columbus's voyages beginning in 1492 connected two hemispheres that had been almost entirely separate for thousands of years. This contact triggered a massive, two-way transfer of plants, animals, diseases, and people known as the Columbian Exchange. The Americas gave the rest of the world crops like potatoes, maize, and tomatoes, which would later transform diets and population growth across Afro-Eurasia. In return, Europe brought horses, cattle, wheat, and sugarcane to the Americas — along with something far deadlier than any crop.

克里斯托弗·哥伦布从1492年开始的航行，将两个数千年来几乎完全隔绝的半球连接了起来。这次接触引发了大规模的双向交换，涉及植物、动物、疾病与人口的传播，被称为哥伦布大交换。美洲为世界其他地区带来了马铃薯、玉米与番茄等作物，这些作物后来彻底改变了亚非欧大陆的饮食结构与人口增长。作为回报，欧洲将马、牛、小麦与甘蔗带到了美洲——同时还带来了比任何作物都更致命的东西。

The "Great Dying": Disease and Demographic Collapse · 「大死亡」：疾病与人口崩溃

Indigenous Americans had no prior exposure to diseases like smallpox, measles, and influenza, which had circulated among Afro-Eurasian populations for centuries and to which many Europeans and Africans had developed at least partial immunity. When these diseases arrived in the Americas, they spread explosively, killing an estimated 90 percent of some Indigenous populations within decades — a catastrophe historians call the "Great Dying." This was likely the single deadliest demographic event in recorded human history, and it devastated Indigenous societies long before most ever met a European in person, since disease often traveled ahead of explorers along existing trade and contact networks.

美洲原住民此前从未接触过天花、麻疹与流感等疾病，这些疾病已在亚非欧人口中流行了数个世纪，许多欧洲人与非洲人已对其产生了至少部分免疫力。当这些疾病传入美洲时，迅速蔓延，在数十年内导致部分原住民人口约90%死亡——历史学家称这场灾难为「大死亡」。这很可能是有记录的人类历史上最致命的单一人口事件，它在许多原住民社会的成员亲眼见到欧洲人之前，就已经摧毁了他们的社会，因为疾病常常沿着既有的贸易与接触网络，先于探险家本人传播开来。

Disease as History's Deadliest Force: Just as the Black Death devastated Afro-Eurasia in the 1300s, European diseases devastated the Americas after 1492 — connecting these two units through the recurring theme of disease and connectivity.
疾病：历史上最致命的力量：正如黑死病在14世纪重创亚非欧大陆，欧洲疾病在1492年后重创了美洲——通过疾病与互联性这一反复出现的主题，将这两个单元联系起来。

Sugarcane and the Reshaping of Africa · 甘蔗与非洲的重塑

Europeans introduced sugarcane to the Caribbean, where it thrived in the tropical climate and became the foundation of the brutal plantation complex you studied in the last lesson. As Indigenous populations collapsed from disease, plantation owners turned increasingly to enslaved Africans to provide the massive labor force sugarcane demanded. This single agricultural and demographic shift — sugarcane's success in the Americas combined with Indigenous demographic collapse — set in motion the demographic changes in Africa that would unfold over the following centuries, as the demand for enslaved labor grew.

欧洲人将甘蔗引入加勒比地区，甘蔗在热带气候中蓬勃生长，成为你在上一课中所学的残酷种植园体系的基础。随着原住民人口因疾病而崩溃，种植园主越来越多地转向被奴役的非洲人，以提供甘蔗种植所需的庞大劳动力。这一单一的农业与人口变化——甘蔗在美洲的成功结合原住民人口的崩溃——开启了非洲在随后数个世纪中将经历的人口变化，随着对被奴役劳动力的需求不断增长。

Indigenous Impact & the Origins of Slavery · 原住民所受冲击与奴隶制的起源

A Century of Disaster: 1500–1600 · 灾难的世纪：1500–1600年

For Indigenous peoples of the Americas, the century following 1492 brought disaster on a scale rarely matched in human history. Demographic collapse from disease, violent conquest, and forced labor under systems like the encomienda combined to devastate entire societies within just a few generations. This was not a single event but an overlapping cascade of catastrophes — disease arriving first, often followed by military conquest, and then a colonial labor system that extracted whatever population remained.

对美洲原住民而言，1492年之后的一个世纪带来了人类历史上罕见的灾难规模。疾病造成的人口崩溃、暴力征服与监护征赋制等强迫劳役制度相互叠加，在短短几代人的时间内摧毁了整个社会。这并非单一事件，而是一系列相互重叠的灾难——疾病首先到来，随后往往是军事征服，再之后是榨取剩余人口的殖民劳役体系。

From Labor Shortage to a New Slave Trade · 从劳动力短缺到新的奴隶贸易

As Indigenous populations collapsed, European colonizers faced a severe labor shortage on their sugar, tobacco, and silver-mining operations. This shortage was the direct economic driver behind the origins of the Atlantic slave trade: plantation owners and mine operators increasingly turned to enslaved Africans, who were forcibly transported across the ocean in massive numbers to replace the lost Indigenous workforce. The demand for cash crops did not decrease — colonizers simply sought a different source of forced labor, setting the stage for the next lesson's deep examination of the transatlantic slave trade.

随着原住民人口的崩溃，欧洲殖民者在其蔗糖、烟草与银矿作业中面临严重的劳动力短缺。这一短缺正是大西洋奴隶贸易兴起背后的直接经济驱动力：种植园主与矿场主越来越多地转向被奴役的非洲人，他们被大规模强行运送过海，以取代失去的原住民劳动力。对经济作物的需求并未减少——殖民者只是寻求了另一种来源的强迫劳动力，这为下一课深入探讨大西洋奴隶贸易奠定了基础。

Who Benefited? A Question for Historians · 谁从中获益？历史学家的一个问题

Historians use demography — the statistical study of population — as a powerful tool for understanding the true costs and benefits of this era. European economies grew dramatically richer through silver, sugar, and trade profits. Meanwhile, Indigenous American populations suffered catastrophic losses, and West and Central African societies experienced their own demographic upheaval as the demand for enslaved labor grew. Comparing population data, wealth data, and labor systems side by side reveals a stark and uncomfortable pattern: the benefits and the costs of global interaction in this era fell on very different groups of people.

历史学家利用人口统计学——对人口的统计研究——作为理解这一时代真实代价与收益的有力工具。欧洲经济通过白银、蔗糖与贸易利润而迅速变得富裕。与此同时，美洲原住民人口遭受了灾难性的损失，西非与中非社会也随着对被奴役劳动力需求的增长而经历了自身的人口剧变。将人口数据、财富数据与劳役体系并列比较，揭示出一种鲜明而令人不安的规律：这一时代全球互动的收益与代价，落在了截然不同的群体身上。
