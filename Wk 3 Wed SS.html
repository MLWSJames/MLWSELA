Rise of Napoleon · 拿破仑的崛起

Who Was Napoleon Bonaparte? · 拿破仑·波拿巴是谁？

Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821) was a French military general who rose to become Emperor of France and one of the most powerful rulers in European history. Born on the island of Corsica, he trained as an artillery officer and quickly rose through the ranks during the chaos of the French Revolution. He was brilliant, ambitious, and ruthless — a leader who reshaped Europe through war and law.

拿破仑·波拿巴（1769-1821年）是一位法国军事将领，后来成为法国皇帝，是欧洲历史上最有权势的统治者之一。他出生于科西嘉岛，接受炮兵军官训练，在法国大革命的混乱中迅速晋升。他聪明、野心勃勃、冷酷无情——一位通过战争和法律重塑欧洲的领袖。

The Directory and Its Collapse · 督政府及其崩溃

After the Reign of Terror ended in 1794, France was governed by a five-man executive body called the Directory (1795–1799). The Directory was deeply unpopular — it was corrupt, incompetent, and unable to solve France's ongoing economic problems. France was still at war with European powers. The French people were exhausted by years of revolution and longed for strong, stable leadership. The Directory's failures created the perfect opening for Napoleon.

1794年恐怖统治结束后，法国由一个五人行政机构——督政府（1795-1799年）——管理。督政府极不受欢迎——它腐败、无能，无法解决法国持续的经济问题。法国仍与欧洲强国处于战争状态。法国人民被多年的革命弄得精疲力竭，渴望强大稳定的领导。督政府的失败为拿破仑创造了完美的机会。

Napoleon's Coup — 18 Brumaire · 拿破仑政变——雾月18日

November 9, 1799 · 1799年11月9日

Napoleon returned from his military campaigns in Egypt and, with the help of key allies including his brother Lucien, staged a coup d'état on November 9, 1799 — known as 18 Brumaire in the French Revolutionary calendar. He overthrew the Directory and replaced it with a new government called the Consulate, with himself as First Consul — effectively the ruler of France. Within five years, he declared himself Emperor Napoleon I.

拿破仑从埃及军事行动归来，在包括其兄弟吕西安在内的关键盟友的帮助下，于1799年11月9日——法国革命历法中的雾月18日——发动政变。他推翻了督政府，建立了一个新政府——执政府，自任第一执政——实际上成为法国的统治者。五年内，他宣布自己为拿破仑一世皇帝。

Napoleon's Code · 拿破仑法典

Napoleon's most lasting achievement was not military — it was legal. The Napoleonic Code (1804) unified French law into one clear system. It guaranteed equality before the law, protection of property rights, and religious freedom. It abolished feudal privileges. The Code spread across Europe as Napoleon conquered it, and influenced legal systems worldwide — including Quebec, Louisiana, and much of Latin America — to this day.

拿破仑最持久的成就不是军事上的——而是法律上的。《拿破仑法典》（1804年）将法国法律统一为一个清晰的体系。它保证了法律面前平等、财产权保护和宗教自由，废除了封建特权。随着拿破仑征服欧洲，法典传遍整个欧洲，至今仍影响着世界各地的法律体系——包括魁北克、路易斯安那和拉丁美洲大部分地区。

Latin American Independence · 拉丁美洲独立

Colonial Latin America: The Setting · 殖民地拉丁美洲：背景

By 1750, Spain and Portugal controlled most of Latin America — a vast territory stretching from Mexico through Central and South America. The colonial system was built on racial hierarchy. At the top were the Peninsulares — people born in Spain who held all government positions. Below them were Creoles — people of Spanish descent born in the Americas who were wealthy but excluded from political power. Then came Mestizos (mixed European-Indigenous), Indigenous peoples, and enslaved Africans.

到1750年，西班牙和葡萄牙控制着拉丁美洲大部分地区——从墨西哥延伸至中美洲和南美洲的广大领土。殖民体系建立在种族等级制度上。顶层是半岛人——出生于西班牙、掌握所有政府职位的人。其下是克里奥尔人——出生于美洲的西班牙裔人，他们富有但被排除在政治权力之外。再下面是混血儿（欧洲-土著混血）、土著人和被奴役的非洲人。

Simón Bolívar — The Liberator · 西蒙·玻利瓦尔——解放者

Venezuela to Argentina: The Great Campaign · 从委内瑞拉到阿根廷：伟大的战役

Simón Bolívar (1783–1830) was a Venezuelan Creole who became the most important independence leader in South America. Educated in Enlightenment ideas in Europe, he returned to Venezuela determined to free Spanish colonies from colonial rule. Between 1810 and 1825, Bolívar led military campaigns across an enormous territory — modern Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Bolivia (named after him). He is called "El Libertador" — the Liberator.

西蒙·玻利瓦尔（1783-1830年）是一位委内瑞拉克里奥尔人，成为南美洲最重要的独立领袖。在欧洲接受启蒙思想教育后，他返回委内瑞拉，决心将西班牙殖民地从殖民统治中解放出来。1810年至1825年间，玻利瓦尔在广阔的领土上领导军事行动——包括今天的委内瑞拉、哥伦比亚、厄瓜多尔、秘鲁和以他命名的玻利维亚。他被称为"解放者"。

New Granada and Gran Colombia · 新格拉纳达与大哥伦比亚

Bolívar's most daring military feat was the campaign to liberate New Granada (modern Colombia). In 1819, he led 2,500 troops across the flooded plains of the Orinoco River and over the freezing Andes Mountains — a journey most thought impossible — to surprise and defeat the Spanish army at the Battle of Boyacá on August 7, 1819. This victory liberated what is now Colombia and Venezuela. Bolívar then created Gran Colombia — a unified republic that he hoped would unite all of northern South America.

玻利瓦尔最大胆的军事壮举是解放新格拉纳达（今哥伦比亚）的战役。1819年，他率领2,500名士兵穿越奥里诺科河洪涝平原，翻越冰冻的安第斯山脉——这是大多数人认为不可能完成的旅程——于1819年8月7日在博亚卡战役中出其不意地击败了西班牙军队。这一胜利解放了今天的哥伦比亚和委内瑞拉。玻利瓦尔随后建立了大哥伦比亚——一个他希望能统一整个南美洲北部的共和国。

Napoleon's Unexpected Role · 拿破仑意想不到的作用

Napoleon's 1808 invasion of Spain was the key trigger for Latin American independence. When Napoleon removed the Spanish king and placed his own brother on the throne, Spanish colonial authority collapsed. Creole leaders across Latin America saw their chance: if Spain's king had no legitimate power, why should the colonies obey Spain at all?

拿破仑1808年入侵西班牙是拉丁美洲独立的关键导火索。当拿破仑废黜西班牙国王并将其兄弟置于王位时，西班牙殖民权威崩溃了。拉丁美洲各地的克里奥尔领袖看到了机会：如果西班牙国王没有合法权力，殖民地为什么还要服从西班牙？

Industrialization & Imperialism · 工业化与帝国主义

What Was Industrialization? · 什么是工业化？

Industrialization was the shift from hand-made goods produced on farms and in homes to machine-made goods produced in factories. It began in Britain in the 1760s–1780s, driven by inventions like the steam engine (James Watt, 1769) and the spinning jenny. Factories concentrated workers in cities. Britain quickly became the world's most powerful economy — producing more iron, coal, and textiles than any other nation. By 1850, Britain was called "the workshop of the world."

工业化是从农场和家庭中手工制作商品转变为工厂中机器制造商品的过程。它始于18世纪60至80年代的英国，由蒸汽机（詹姆斯·瓦特，1769年）和珍妮纺纱机等发明推动。工厂将工人集中在城市中。英国迅速成为世界上最强大的经济体——生产的铁、煤和纺织品比任何其他国家都多。到1850年，英国被称为"世界工厂"。

How Industry Drove Empire · 工业如何推动帝国扩张

Industrial capitalism created powerful incentives for empire. Factories needed raw materials — cotton, rubber, metals, oil — that Britain did not have in abundance at home. Factories also produced more goods than British consumers could buy — so new markets were needed overseas. Finally, industrial technology gave Britain enormous military advantages: steam-powered warships, railroads to move troops, and rifles that massively outmatched the weapons of non-industrial societies. Empire became both economically necessary and militarily possible.

工业资本主义为帝国扩张创造了强大的动力。工厂需要英国本土不足的原材料——棉花、橡胶、金属、石油。工厂生产的商品也超过了英国消费者的购买能力——因此需要海外新市场。最后，工业技术给予英国巨大的军事优势：蒸汽动力战舰、运送军队的铁路，以及大幅超越非工业社会武器的步枪。帝国在经济上变得必要，在军事上也成为可能。

British Expansion and Cook's Voyages · 英国扩张与库克的航行

Captain James Cook (1728–1779) · 詹姆斯·库克船长

Captain James Cook led three major Pacific voyages (1768–1779) that mapped Australia, New Zealand, Hawaii, and much of the Pacific Ocean for Britain. Cook's voyages were scientific expeditions — he carried botanists, artists, and astronomers. But they were also imperial projects: everywhere Cook went, Britain claimed territory. His voyages opened the Pacific to British colonization. Australia was claimed for Britain in 1770 and became a British penal colony in 1788.

詹姆斯·库克船长领导了三次主要的太平洋航行（1768-1779年），为英国绘制了澳大利亚、新西兰、夏威夷和太平洋大部分地区的地图。库克的航行是科学探险——他带着植物学家、艺术家和天文学家。但它们也是帝国项目：库克到达的每个地方，英国都声索领土。澳大利亚于1770年被英国宣称，并于1788年成为英国流放地。

The "White Man's Burden" — Justifying Empire · "白人的负担"——为帝国辩护

European powers justified imperialism with racist ideology — the belief that European civilization was superior and that colonizing other peoples was a "civilizing mission." Poet Rudyard Kipling called it the "White Man's Burden." In reality, colonialism brought forced labor, land theft, cultural destruction, and violent suppression of resistance. The wealth produced in colonies flowed to European metropoles, not to colonized peoples.

欧洲强国用种族主义意识形态为帝国主义辩护——认为欧洲文明更优越，殖民其他民族是一种"文明使命"。诗人鲁德亚德·吉卜林将其称为"白人的负担"。事实上，殖民主义带来了强迫劳动、土地掠夺、文化破坏和对抵抗的暴力镇压。殖民地生产的财富流向欧洲宗主国，而不是被殖民的人民。

Opium Wars · 鸦片战争

China and the Qing Dynasty · 中国与清朝

In the early 19th century, the Qing Dynasty (1644–1912) ruled China — one of the world's largest and most populous empires. China had little interest in European trade goods. The Qing emperor viewed China as the "Middle Kingdom" — the center of civilization. When British ambassadors arrived seeking trade agreements in 1793, the emperor famously replied that China had everything it needed and had no interest in Britain's manufactures.

19世纪初，清朝（1644-1912年）统治着中国——世界上最大、人口最多的帝国之一。中国对欧洲贸易商品兴趣甚少。清朝皇帝将中国视为"中央之国"——文明的中心。1793年，英国大使到来寻求贸易协议时，皇帝的著名回复是：中国拥有一切所需，对英国的制造品不感兴趣。

The Trade Imbalance and Opium · 贸易逆差与鸦片

Britain had a serious problem: it desperately wanted Chinese goods — silk, porcelain, and tea — but China would only accept silver as payment. British silver was flowing out of Britain into China at an alarming rate. The British East India Company found a solution: opium. Grown in British-controlled India, opium was smuggled into China illegally. Chinese demand for the addictive drug exploded. By the 1830s, the trade imbalance had reversed — silver was now flowing OUT of China INTO Britain. The Qing government was horrified.

英国面临一个严重问题：它迫切需要中国商品——丝绸、瓷器和茶叶——但中国只接受白银作为付款。英国白银以令人担忧的速度从英国流向中国。英国东印度公司找到了解决方案：鸦片。在英国控制的印度种植的鸦片被非法走私到中国。中国对这种成瘾性毒品的需求爆炸式增长。到19世纪30年代，贸易不平衡已经逆转——白银现在从中国流出，流入英国。清政府对此感到震惊。

The First Opium War (1839–1842) · 第一次鸦片战争（1839-1842年）

Lin Zexu and the British Response · 林则徐与英国的回应

In 1839, Qing official Lin Zexu confiscated and destroyed over 1,200 tons of British opium in Canton. He wrote to Queen Victoria personally, asking her to stop the trade. Britain responded not with diplomacy but with warships. British steam-powered warships were vastly superior to Chinese junks. The British navy bombarded Chinese coastal cities and forced China to negotiate. The result was the humiliating Treaty of Nanking (1842).

1839年，清朝官员林则徐在广州没收并销毁了1,200多吨英国鸦片。他亲自致信维多利亚女王，要求她停止这种贸易。英国的回应不是外交，而是军舰。英国蒸汽动力战舰远优于中国帆船。英国海军炮击中国沿海城市，迫使中国谈判。结果是屈辱的《南京条约》（1842年）。

The Second Opium War (1856–1860) · 第二次鸦片战争

The Second Opium War began when China resisted further British demands. Britain and France attacked together, eventually marching on Beijing. In 1860, British and French troops looted and burned the magnificent Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan) — one of the most culturally significant acts of destruction in Chinese history. China was forced to sign more unequal treaties, open more ports, and legalize the opium trade. These wars began what Chinese historians call the "Century of Humiliation."

第二次鸦片战争始于中国抵制英国进一步的要求。英国和法国联合进攻，最终进军北京。1860年，英法联军抢劫并焚烧了壮丽的圆明园——这是中国历史上文化意义最重大的破坏行为之一。中国被迫签署更多不平等条约，开放更多港口，并将鸦片贸易合法化。这些战争开始了中国历史学家所称的"百年屈辱"。

Century of Humiliation · 百年屈辱

The Opium Wars launched a period Chinese historians call the "Century of Humiliation" (1839–1949) — 100 years during which China was repeatedly defeated, carved up, and humiliated by foreign powers. This historical memory remains extremely important in China today and shapes how China thinks about sovereignty, foreign relations, and national strength.

鸦片战争开启了中国历史学家所称的"百年屈辱"时期（1839-1949年）——中国被外国列强一再击败、瓜分和屈辱的100年。这段历史记忆在今天的中国仍然极为重要，塑造了中国对主权、外交关系和国家实力的思考方式。

Japanese Imperialism · 日本帝国主义

Japan Before the Meiji Era · 明治时代之前的日本

For over 200 years (1603–1868), Japan was ruled by the Tokugawa shogunate — a military government that kept Japan almost completely isolated from the outside world. Foreign trade was limited to a tiny Dutch trading post. Japan was a feudal society dominated by samurai warriors. This isolation ended dramatically in 1853 when American Commodore Matthew Perry arrived with four steam-powered warships — the "Black Ships" — and demanded Japan open its ports to trade. Japan had no equivalent military technology and was forced to comply.

200多年来（1603-1868年），日本由德川幕府统治——一个使日本几乎与外界完全隔绝的军事政府。对外贸易仅限于一个微小的荷兰贸易站。日本是一个以武士为主导的封建社会。这种孤立状态于1853年戏剧性地结束，当时美国准将马修·佩里带着四艘蒸汽动力战舰——"黑船"——抵达，要求日本开放港口进行贸易。日本没有同等的军事技术，被迫服从。

The Meiji Restoration (1868) · 明治维新（1868年）

Japan's Revolutionary Response · 日本的革命性回应

In 1868, a group of reformers overthrew the Tokugawa shogunate and restored power to Emperor Meiji — a process called the Meiji Restoration. The new Meiji government made a radical decision: Japan would rapidly modernize and industrialize to avoid being colonized like China and India. Government officials traveled the world studying Western technology, law, military organization, and industry. Japan built railroads, factories, a modern navy, and a conscript army — all within decades.

1868年，一群改革者推翻了德川幕府，恢复了明治天皇的权力——这一过程被称为明治维新。新的明治政府做出了一个激进的决定：日本将迅速现代化和工业化，以避免像中国和印度那样被殖民。政府官员周游世界，学习西方技术、法律、军事组织和工业。日本在数十年内建立了铁路、工厂、现代海军和征兵军队。

Japan Becomes an Imperial Power · 日本成为帝国势力

From Colonized Target to Colonial Power · 从殖民目标到殖民势力

By the 1890s, Japan had industrialized rapidly and began its own imperial expansion across East and Southeast Asia. Japan's motivations mirrored European imperialism: it needed raw materials (coal, iron, oil) it lacked at home, and wanted markets for its growing industrial output. Japanese leaders also argued that having a colonial empire gave a nation status and security — the lesson they drew from watching European powers dominate the world.

到19世纪90年代，日本已迅速工业化，并开始在东亚和东南亚进行帝国扩张。日本的动机与欧洲帝国主义如出一辙：它需要本土缺乏的原材料（煤、铁、石油），并需要为其不断增长的工业产出寻找市场。日本领导人还认为，拥有殖民帝国赋予一个国家地位和安全——这是他们观察欧洲强国主宰世界所得出的教训。

Why Japan's Victory Over Russia Mattered · 为什么日本战胜俄国很重要

In 1905, Japan defeated Russia in the Russo-Japanese War — the first time an Asian nation had defeated a major European power in a modern war. This shocked the world and inspired independence movements across Asia and Africa. It showed that European military dominance was not inevitable. It also alarmed Western powers who began to fear Japan's growing strength in Asia.

1905年，日本在日俄战争中击败俄国——这是亚洲国家首次在现代战争中击败主要欧洲强国。这震惊了世界，激励了亚洲和非洲各地的独立运动。它表明欧洲军事主导地位并非不可避免。这也使西方列强感到震惊，他们开始担心日本在亚洲不断增长的力量。
