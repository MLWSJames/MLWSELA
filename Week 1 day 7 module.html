Justinian & the Byzantine Empire · 查士丁尼与拜占庭帝国

What Was the Byzantine Empire? · 拜占庭帝国是什么？

When the Western Roman Empire collapsed in 476 CE, the Eastern Roman Empire — later called the Byzantine Empire — survived and continued for nearly another thousand years. Centered on its capital, Constantinople, the empire blended Roman law and government, Greek language and culture, and Christian religion into a distinct civilization that controlled trade between Europe and Asia.

西罗马帝国于公元476年崩溃后，东罗马帝国——后来被称为拜占庭帝国——存活下来，并又延续了将近一千年。该帝国以首都君士坦丁堡为中心，融合了罗马法律与政府体制、希腊语言与文化，以及基督教信仰，形成了一种独特的文明，并控制着欧亚之间的贸易。

Emperor Justinian's Achievements · 查士丁尼皇帝的成就

Justinian I (ruled 527–565 CE) is considered the most influential Byzantine emperor. He attempted to reconquer lost Western Roman territory, commissioned a massive legal reform known as the Justinian Code, and oversaw the construction of the Hagia Sophia, one of the greatest architectural achievements of the ancient world.

The Justinian Code · 查士丁尼法典

Roman law had grown disorganized over centuries of additions and contradictions. Justinian ordered legal scholars to collect, simplify, and organize this law into a single, coherent body known as the Corpus Juris Civilis (Body of Civil Law), commonly called the Justinian Code. This code preserved Roman legal traditions and later became a foundation for legal systems across Europe.

Lasting Influence: Centuries after the Byzantine Empire fell, the Justinian Code continued to shape legal systems throughout Europe — a clear example of how ideas can outlast the empires that created them.
持久的影响：在拜占庭帝国灭亡数百年后，查士丁尼法典仍持续影响着整个欧洲的法律体系——这清楚地说明了思想可以比创造它们的帝国存在得更久。

Constantinople: A Crossroads of Trade and Culture · 君士坦丁堡：贸易与文化的十字路口

Constantinople's location, straddling the boundary between Europe and Asia, made it one of the wealthiest and most important trading cities in the world. Its strong walls and strategic position allowed the Byzantine Empire to survive numerous invasions for centuries, even as Western Europe fragmented into smaller, less centralized kingdoms.

君士坦丁堡的地理位置横跨欧亚两洲的边界，使其成为世界上最富有、最重要的贸易城市之一。其坚固的城墙与战略性的位置，使拜占庭帝国能够在数百年间抵御无数次入侵，即便此时西欧已分裂为更小、更分散的王国。

Overview of the Middle Ages · 中世纪概述

What Were the Middle Ages? · 中世纪是什么时期？

The Middle Ages (roughly 500–1500 CE) refers to the long period in European history between the fall of the Western Roman Empire and the start of the Renaissance. This era is sometimes called the "medieval period," and its earlier centuries are sometimes referred to as the "Dark Ages" because of the loss of centralized Roman government, infrastructure, and widespread literacy.

中世纪（大约公元500年—1500年）指的是欧洲历史上西罗马帝国灭亡与文艺复兴兴起之间的漫长时期。这一时代有时被称为"中世纪时期"，而其较早的几个世纪有时被称为"黑暗时代"，因为当时失去了罗马中央集权政府、基础设施以及广泛的识字能力。

Political Fragmentation · 政治分裂

Without the centralized authority of Rome, Western Europe broke apart into many smaller, competing kingdoms and territories. No single power controlled the region the way Rome once had. This fragmentation created instability but also allowed local lords and the Christian Church to grow in influence, filling the gap left by the absence of a strong central government.

Roman Empire (Before) 罗马帝国（之前）Medieval Europe (After) 中世纪欧洲（之后）
Centralized government and law 中央集权的政府与法律Fragmented kingdoms and local rule 分裂的王国与地方统治
Extensive road and trade networks 广泛的道路与贸易网络Smaller, more local economies 更小、更地方化的经济
State-supported infrastructure 国家支持的基础设施Church and local lords filling gaps in authority 教会与地方领主填补权力真空

The Power of the Church · 教会的权力

With Rome's central government gone, the Catholic Church became one of the most powerful and stable institutions in Western Europe. The Church provided education, preserved knowledge in monasteries, and often held more practical authority over daily life than any king. Religion shaped nearly every part of medieval society, from law to art to education.

Filling the Power Vacuum: When political authority weakens or disappears, other institutions — like the Church in medieval Europe — often step in to provide order, structure, and stability for ordinary people.
填补权力真空：当政治权威减弱或消失时，其他机构——如中世纪欧洲的教会——往往会介入，为普通民众提供秩序、结构和稳定。

Setting the Stage for Feudalism · 为封建制度奠定基础

The instability and constant threat of invasion during the early Middle Ages pushed people to seek protection from local strongmen rather than a distant or nonexistent central government. This need for local protection and order became the foundation of a new political and social system that would dominate medieval Europe: feudalism.

中世纪早期的不稳定与持续的入侵威胁，促使人们向地方强人而非遥远或根本不存在的中央政府寻求保护。这种对地方保护与秩序的需求，成为了一种将主导中世纪欧洲的新政治与社会体系的基础：封建制度。

The Feudal System · 封建制度

What Was Feudalism? · 什么是封建制度？

Feudalism was a system of mutual obligations that structured medieval European society. In exchange for protection and use of land, lower-ranking individuals provided loyalty, labor, or military service to those above them. This created a clear hierarchy that organized nearly every part of medieval life, from the king down to the peasants who worked the land.

封建制度是一种相互义务的体系，构成了中世纪欧洲社会的结构。下级人员通过向上级提供忠诚、劳动或军事服务，来换取保护与土地的使用权。这形成了一种明确的等级体系，组织了中世纪生活的几乎每一个方面，从国王到耕种土地的农民。

The Feudal Hierarchy · 封建等级体系

Manorialism: The Economic Side of Feudalism · 庄园制：封建制度的经济层面

While feudalism described the political and military relationships between lords and vassals, manorialism described the economic system of daily life on a lord's estate, or manor. Most peasants were serfs, bound to the land they worked and unable to leave without their lord's permission. In return for their labor, serfs received protection and a small portion of what they grew.

A System of Mutual (but Unequal) Obligation: Every level of feudal society owed something to the level above and received something from it — protection, land, or labor — but the benefits of this exchange were far from equal.
相互（但不平等）的义务体系：封建社会的每一个层级都对上一层级负有义务，同时也从其获得回报——保护、土地或劳动——但这种交换所带来的利益远非平等。

Why Feudalism Developed · 封建制度为何会发展形成

Feudalism developed largely as a response to the chaos and insecurity of the early Middle Ages. With no strong central government to provide protection from invasion or maintain order, people turned to local lords who could offer immediate, practical security in exchange for loyalty and labor.

封建制度的形成主要是为了应对中世纪早期的混乱与不安全感。由于没有强大的中央政府来提供抵御入侵的保护或维持秩序，人们转向能够提供即时、实际安全保障的地方领主，以换取忠诚与劳动。

Life of Muhammad / Origins of Islam · 穆罕默德的生平 / 伊斯兰教的起源

Arabia Before Islam · 伊斯兰教兴起前的阿拉伯

Before the 7th century CE, the Arabian Peninsula was home to many tribes, most of whom worshipped multiple gods and spirits. The city of Mecca was an important trading center and religious site, home to the Kaaba, a shrine that housed statues representing different tribal deities and attracted pilgrims from across the region.

在公元7世纪之前，阿拉伯半岛上居住着许多部落，大多数部落信奉多神与多种精灵。麦加城是一个重要的贸易中心与宗教圣地，是克尔白的所在地——这是一座供奉着代表不同部落神灵雕像的圣殿，吸引了来自该地区各地的朝圣者。

Muhammad's Early Life · 穆罕默德的早年生活

Muhammad was born in Mecca around 570 CE. Orphaned at a young age, he was raised by relatives and later became a successful merchant known for his honesty. Muhammad frequently withdrew to a cave outside Mecca to meditate and reflect, a practice that set the stage for the spiritual experiences that would later transform his life and the history of the region.

Stage of Life 人生阶段Key Details 主要细节
Early life 早年生活Born c. 570 CE in Mecca; orphaned young 大约公元570年生于麦加；幼年成为孤儿
Adulthood 成年时期Became a respected, honest merchant 成为一位受人尊敬、诚实的商人
Spiritual practice 精神实践Regularly meditated in a cave near Mecca 经常在麦加附近的一个洞穴中冥想

The First Revelation · 最初的启示

Around 610 CE, Muslims believe Muhammad received the first of many revelations from God (Allah) through the angel Gabriel while meditating in a cave on Mount Hira. These revelations, delivered over the rest of his life, were later collected into the Quran, the central religious text of Islam. Muhammad began preaching a message of strict monotheism — belief in one God — which directly challenged Mecca's existing polytheistic religious traditions.

A Challenge to the Status Quo: Muhammad's message of one God threatened the religious and economic power of Mecca's elite, who profited from pilgrims visiting the Kaaba's many idols — setting up early conflict between Muhammad's followers and Meccan leaders.
对现状的挑战：穆罕默德【一神】的信息威胁到了麦加权贵的宗教与经济权力，他们从前往克尔白朝拜众多神像的朝圣者中获利——这也为穆罕默德追随者与麦加领袖之间的早期冲突埋下了隐患。

The Five Pillars of Islam · 伊斯兰教的五大支柱

As Muhammad's teachings developed into the religion of Islam, they came to be organized around core practices known as the Five Pillars: declaration of faith (shahada), prayer five times daily (salat), charity to the poor (zakat), fasting during Ramadan (sawm), and pilgrimage to Mecca (hajj). These practices unified Muslim believers and structured daily religious life.

随着穆罕默德的教义发展成为伊斯兰教，这些教义逐渐围绕被称为五大支柱的核心实践组织起来：信仰宣言（清真言）、每日五次礼拜（礼拜）、对穷人的施舍（天课）、斋月斋戒（斋戒）以及前往麦加朝圣（朝觐）。这些实践统一了穆斯林信徒，并构建了日常的宗教生活。

The Hijra to Medina / Conversion of Mecca · 迁往麦地那 / 麦加的归顺

Growing Persecution in Mecca · 麦加日益加剧的迫害

As Muhammad's following grew, Meccan leaders — fearing the loss of their religious and economic power tied to the Kaaba — increased their persecution of Muhammad and his early followers. Facing threats to their safety, Muhammad and his followers needed to find a new place where they could practice and spread their faith without fear of violence.

随着穆罕默德追随者人数的增长，麦加领袖——担心失去与克尔白相关的宗教与经济权力——加剧了对穆罕默德及其早期追随者的迫害。面临着安全威胁，穆罕默德和他的追随者需要寻找一个新的地方，在那里他们能够实践并传播信仰，而不必担心暴力。

The Hijra: Migration to Medina · 希吉拉：迁往麦地那

In 622 CE, Muhammad and his followers migrated from Mecca to the city of Medina. This journey, known as the Hijra, marks such a pivotal turning point in Islamic history that it became Year 1 of the Islamic calendar. In Medina, Muhammad was welcomed as a political and religious leader, allowing him to establish the first Muslim community, or umma, with himself as both spiritual guide and governing authority.

Before the Hijra (Mecca) 希吉拉之前（麦加）After the Hijra (Medina) 希吉拉之后（麦地那）
Muhammad faced persecution and threats 穆罕默德面临迫害与威胁Muhammad was welcomed as a leader 穆罕默德被迎为领袖
Small, vulnerable community of believers 弱小且脆弱的信徒社群Establishment of the first organized Muslim community (umma) 建立了第一个有组织的穆斯林社群（乌玛）
No political authority 没有政治权力Combined religious and political leadership 兼具宗教与政治领导地位

Building the Umma in Medina · 在麦地那建立乌玛

In Medina, Muhammad drafted agreements establishing rules for cooperation between Muslims and other groups in the city, creating one of the earliest examples of a written constitution. This new community combined religious devotion with practical governance — laws, military organization, and social structure — laying the foundation for the rapid growth of Islam that would follow.

Religion and Government Combined: Muhammad's role in Medina shows how, in this case, religious leadership and political authority became deeply intertwined from the very founding of the Muslim community.
宗教与政府的结合：穆罕默德在麦地那的角色表明，在这种情况下，宗教领导地位与政治权力从穆斯林社群建立之初就紧密地交织在一起。

The Conquest and Conversion of Mecca · 麦加的征服与归顺

By 630 CE, Muhammad's growing community in Medina had become powerful enough to return to Mecca. Muhammad and his followers entered the city largely without violence, and Mecca's leaders ultimately converted to Islam. Muhammad then removed the idols from the Kaaba, rededicating it as the central holy site of Islam — a role it still holds today as the destination of the hajj pilgrimage.

到公元630年，穆罕默德在麦地那不断壮大的社群已强大到足以重返麦加。穆罕默德和他的追随者基本上是在没有暴力的情况下进入了这座城市，麦加的领袖最终皈依了伊斯兰教。随后穆罕默德移除了克尔白中的神像，将其重新奉为伊斯兰教的核心圣地——这一地位延续至今，成为朝觐朝圣的目的地。
